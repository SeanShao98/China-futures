# Measures for the Administration of Futures Exchanges  
(CSRC Order No. 6)

# Chapter I  General Provisions

**Article 1**  
For the purpose of strengthening the administration of futures exchanges, clarifying their powers and responsibilities, and maintaining the normal order of the futures market, these Measures are formulated in accordance with the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*.

**Article 2**  
These Measures apply to futures exchanges established within the territory of the People’s Republic of China.

**Article 3**  
A “futures exchange,” as referred to in these Measures, means a legal person established in accordance with the conditions prescribed by the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading* and these Measures, which performs the functions stipulated in the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading* and these Measures, and implements self-regulation pursuant to its articles of association.  
The registered capital of a futures exchange shall be divided into equal shares and subscribed by its members.

**Article 4**  
The China Securities Regulatory Commission (hereinafter, the “CSRC”) shall, in accordance with the law, exercise centralized and unified supervision and administration over futures exchanges.

---

# Chapter II  Establishment, Change, and Termination

**Article 5**  
The establishment of a futures exchange shall be subject to the approval of the CSRC. Without approval, no entity or individual may establish, or in a disguised form establish, a futures exchange.

**Article 6**  
An applicant for the establishment of a futures exchange shall submit the following documents and materials to the CSRC:  
(1) An application letter;  
(2) Draft articles of association and trading rules;  
(3) The business plan of the futures exchange;  
(4) A list of the prospective members of the exchange;  
(5) A list and résumés of candidates for the council (board of directors);  
(6) A list and résumés of proposed senior management personnel;  
(7) Supporting documents and explanatory materials regarding premises, facilities, and funds;  
(8) Other documents and materials as required by the CSRC.

**Article 7**  
A futures exchange established with CSRC approval shall bear in its name the wording “Commodity Exchange” or “Futures Exchange.” No other entity or individual may use the wording “Commodity Exchange,” “Futures Exchange,” or any similar name.

**Article 8**  
In addition to performing the functions prescribed by the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, a futures exchange shall perform the following functions:  
(1) Formulate and implement the business rules of the futures exchange;  
(2) Publish market information;  
(3) Supervise members’ futures business and investigate members’ violations;  
(4) Supervise the futures business of designated delivery warehouses;  
(5) Supervise futures clearing business conducted by clearing banks in relation to the exchange.

**Article 9**  
The articles of association of a futures exchange shall specify the following matters:  
(1) Purpose of establishment and functions;  
(2) Name, domicile, and place of business;  
(3) Registered capital and its composition;  
(4) Term of operation;  
(5) Membership qualifications and their administration;  
(6) Rights and obligations of members;  
(7) Disciplinary sanctions against members;  
(8) Composition, powers, term of office, and rules of procedure of organizational bodies such as the members’ general assembly and the council;  
(9) Selection, appointment and removal of management personnel, and their duties;  
(10) Basic business rules;  
(11) Risk reserve fund administration system;  
(12) Financial accounting and internal audit systems;  
(13) Conditions and procedures for change and termination, and methods of liquidation;  
(14) Procedures for amending the articles of association;  
(15) Other matters that need to be provided for in the articles of association.

**Article 10**  
The trading rules of a futures exchange shall specify the following matters:  
(1) Systems for futures trading, delivery, and settlement;  
(2) Rules for brokerage and proprietary trading;  
(3) Risk control system and procedures for handling abnormal trading situations;  
(4) Systems for the administration and use of margin;  
(5) Rules for the creation, circulation, management, and cancellation of standard warehouse receipts;  
(6) Methods for publishing futures trading information;  
(7) Violations and defaults and measures for handling them;  
(8) Methods for handling trading disputes;  
(9) Other matters that need to be specified in the trading rules.

**Article 11**  
Mergers, divisions, or networked trading of futures exchanges shall be subject to CSRC approval.  
A merger of futures exchanges may take the form of absorption or the establishment of a new exchange; the rights and obligations of the parties prior to the merger shall be succeeded to by the surviving or newly established futures exchange.  
Where a futures exchange is divided, its rights and obligations shall be succeeded to by the futures exchanges resulting from the division.

**Article 12**  
Where a futures exchange encounters circumstances specified in Article 17 of the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, or undergoes any of the following changes, it shall obtain the approval of the CSRC:  
(1) Change of name;  
(2) Change of domicile or place of business.

**Article 13**  
Without the approval of the CSRC, a futures exchange shall not establish branches or any other futures trading venues.

**Article 14**  
Where a futures exchange encounters the circumstances specified in Article 20 of the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, or is terminated due to merger or division, the CSRC shall make a public announcement.  
Upon termination of a futures exchange, a liquidation group shall be formed to conduct liquidation. The liquidation plan formulated by the liquidation group shall be submitted to the CSRC for approval.

# Chapter III  Organizational Structure

**Article 15**  
A futures exchange shall establish a members’ general assembly and a council.

**Article 16**  
The members’ general assembly is the authority of the futures exchange and is composed of all members.  
The members’ general assembly shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Deliberate on and adopt the articles of association and trading rules of the futures exchange and draft amendments thereto;  
(2) Elect and replace member directors;  
(3) Deliberate on and approve the work reports of the council and the general manager;  
(4) Deliberate on and approve the budgets and final accounts of the futures exchange;  
(5) Deliberate on the use of the risk reserve fund of the futures exchange;  
(6) Decide on increases or decreases of the registered capital of the futures exchange;  
(7) Decide on matters relating to merger, division, dissolution, and liquidation of the futures exchange;  
(8) Decide on other major matters submitted by the council;  
(9) Other powers provided in the articles of association.

**Article 17**  
The members’ general assembly shall be convened by the council and, in general, shall be held once a year.  
An extraordinary members’ meeting shall be convened under any of the following circumstances:  
(1) The number of member directors falls below two-thirds of that specified in the articles of association;  
(2) A joint proposal by more than one-third of the members;  
(3) The council deems it necessary.

**Article 18**  
Meetings of the members’ general assembly shall be presided over by the chair of the council. Where the chair is, for special reasons, unable to perform duties, a vice-chair or another director designated by the chair shall preside. Members shall be notified of the matters to be deliberated no later than 10 days before the meeting is held. An extraordinary members’ meeting shall not adopt resolutions on matters not listed in the notice.

**Article 19**  
A meeting of the members’ general assembly shall be valid only if more than two-thirds of the members attend. Minutes shall be kept for voting matters and shall be signed by the directors attending the meeting.  
Within 10 days after the end of the meeting, the futures exchange shall file all meeting documents with the CSRC for the record.

**Article 20**  
The council is the standing body of the members’ general assembly and is responsible to it.  
The council shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Convene meetings of the members’ general assembly and report on its work thereto;  
(2) Elect the chair and vice-chairs of the council;  
(3) Adopt no-confidence motions against council members and submit them to the relevant authority for review and approval;  
(4) Draft the articles of association, trading rules, and amendments thereto, and submit them to the members’ general assembly for adoption;  
(5) Deliberate on the budgets and final accounts proposed by the general manager and submit them to the members’ general assembly for adoption;  
(6) Deliberate on plans for merger, division, dissolution, and liquidation of the futures exchange and submit them to the members’ general assembly for adoption;  
(7) Decide on the establishment of specialized committees;  
(8) Decide on the admission of members;  
(9) Decide on penalties for members’ violations;  
(10) Decide on changes of the futures exchange;  
(11) Review and approve implementing rules and measures formulated pursuant to the trading rules;  
(12) Review and approve the administration and use of the risk reserve fund;  
(13) Review and approve development plans and annual work plans proposed by the general manager;  
(14) Supervise the implementation of resolutions of the members’ general assembly and the council by the general manager;  
(15) Supervise compliance by senior management and other staff with relevant state laws, regulations, rules, policies, and the articles of association and business rules of the futures exchange;  
(16) Organize the annual audit of the financial and accounting reports of the futures exchange and decide on the engagement and change of the accounting firm;  
(17) Other powers provided in the articles of association and conferred by the members’ general assembly.  
The council may delegate the foregoing powers to specialized committees or the general manager.

**Article 21**  
The council shall be composed of member directors and non-member directors. Member directors shall be elected by the members’ general assembly; non-member directors shall be appointed by the CSRC.

**Article 22**  
The council shall have one chair and one to two vice-chairs. The chair and vice-chairs shall be nominated by the CSRC and elected by the council. The chair shall not concurrently serve as the general manager.

**Article 23**  
The chair shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Preside over meetings of the members’ general assembly and the council, and the day-to-day work of the council;  
(2) Organize and coordinate the work of specialized committees;  
(3) Inspect the implementation of council resolutions and report to the council.  
The vice-chairs shall assist the chair. Where the chair is temporarily unable to perform duties, a vice-chair or a director designated by the chair shall act in the chair’s stead.

**Article 24**  
Council meetings shall be held at least once every six months. All directors shall be notified at least 10 days prior to each meeting.  
An extraordinary council meeting shall be convened under any of the following circumstances:  
(1) Upon proposal by the CSRC;  
(2) Upon a joint proposal by more than one-third of the directors;  
(3) Under circumstances specified in the articles of association.  
For extraordinary meetings, the method and time limits for notice may be otherwise specified.

**Article 25**  
A council meeting shall be valid only if more than two-thirds of the directors are present; resolutions shall be adopted by a vote of more than one-half of all directors.  
Within 10 days after the end of the meeting, the council shall file the meeting resolutions and other meeting documents with the CSRC for the record.

**Article 26**  
Council meetings shall be attended by directors in person. A director who is unable to attend shall, in writing, entrust another director to attend on his or her behalf; the letter of authorization shall specify the scope of authorization. Each director may accept authorization from only one other director.  
Minutes shall be kept for voting matters at council meetings and signed by the directors present and by the recorder.

**Article 27**  
The council may, as needed, establish specialized committees such as supervision, trading, delivery, membership qualification review, mediation, finance, and technology committees. Their functions shall be determined by the council, to which they shall be responsible.

**Article 28**  
A futures exchange shall have one general manager and several deputy general managers. The general manager and deputy general managers shall be appointed or removed by the CSRC. The term of office of the general manager shall be three years and may not exceed two consecutive terms.  
The general manager is the legal representative of the futures exchange and shall serve ex officio as a director.

**Article 29**  
The general manager shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Organize the implementation of the systems and resolutions adopted by the members’ general assembly and the council;  
(2) Preside over the day-to-day operations of the futures exchange;  
(3) Draft implementing rules and measures pursuant to the articles of association and trading rules;  
(4) Draft and implement approved development plans and annual work plans of the futures exchange;  
(5) Draft the budgets and final accounts of the futures exchange;  
(6) Draft plans for merger, division, dissolution, and liquidation of the futures exchange;  
(7) Draft plans for the changes specified in Article 12 of these Measures;  
(8) Decide on the organizational set-up of the futures exchange and hire and dismiss staff;  
(9) Decide on staff remuneration and rewards and penalties;  
(10) Other powers provided in the articles of association or conferred by the council.  
Where the general manager is temporarily unable to perform duties, a deputy general manager designated by the general manager shall act in the general manager’s stead.

**Article 30**  
The appointment and removal of middle-level managers of a futures exchange shall be filed with the CSRC for the record.

# Chapter IV  Administration of Members

**Article 31**  
Members admitted by a futures exchange shall be corporate legal persons registered within the territory of the People’s Republic of China and shall have good credit standing.

**Article 32**  
Membership administration measures of a futures exchange shall include:  
(1) Conditions and procedures for acquiring membership;  
(2) Conditions and procedures for changing membership;  
(3) Supervision and administration of members;  
(4) Measures for handling members’ violations and defaults;  
(5) Other matters that need to be provided for in the membership administration measures.

**Article 33**  
Members shall enjoy the following rights:  
(1) Participate in the members’ general assembly and exercise rights to elect, to be elected, and to vote;  
(2) Engage in futures trading on the exchange;  
(3) Use the trading facilities provided by the exchange and obtain information and services related to futures trading;  
(4) Transfer membership as prescribed;  
(5) Jointly propose the convening of an extraordinary members’ meeting;  
(6) Exercise the right to appeal in accordance with the articles of association and trading rules of the exchange;  
(7) Other rights provided in the articles of association of the exchange.

**Article 34**  
Members shall perform the following obligations:  
(1) Comply with relevant state laws, regulations, rules, and policies;  
(2) Comply with the articles of association, business rules, and relevant decisions of the exchange;  
(3) Pay various fees as prescribed;  
(4) Implement resolutions of the members’ general assembly and the council;  
(5) Accept the supervision and administration of the exchange;  
(6) Other obligations provided in the articles of association of the exchange.

**Article 35**  
A futures exchange shall formulate seat administration rules. Members shall not, in whole or in part, lease, contract out, or otherwise permit other institutions or individuals to use their seats.

**Article 36**  
A futures exchange shall, each year, conduct sample or comprehensive inspections on members’ compliance with the business rules of the exchange and report the inspection results to the CSRC.  
When exercising supervisory powers, a futures exchange may, within the scope of authority and procedures provided in its articles of association, trading rules, and implementing details, conduct investigations and collect evidence from members, and members shall cooperate.

**Article 37**  
A futures exchange may, based on the needs of trading and clearing business, establish special categories of members. The types of such special members, their qualification requirements, and their rights and obligations shall be provided for in the articles of association of the futures exchange.

# Chapter V  Basic Business Rules

**Article 38**  
A futures exchange shall collect margin from its members to secure the performance of futures contracts. The exchange shall open a dedicated settlement account with its designated clearing bank to hold margin funds on a segregated basis and shall not misappropriate such funds.

**Article 39**  
A futures exchange’s margin administration system shall include:  
(1) The ratios and forms for collecting margin from members;  
(2) The minimum balance of members’ margin in the dedicated settlement account;  
(3) Disposal methods when a member’s margin balance falls below the minimum specified by the exchange.

**Article 40**  
A futures exchange shall allocate 20% of its fee income to a risk reserve fund, which shall be accounted for separately and held in a dedicated account.  
The CSRC may, in light of the exchange’s business scale, development plan, and potential risks, determine the scale of the risk reserve fund.

**Article 41**  
Futures trading shall implement a daily mark-to-market settlement system.

**Article 42**  
Futures trading shall implement an investor trading code system. Futures brokerage company members and investors must comply with the “one account, one code” requirement and shall not mix codes in trading.

**Article 43**  
Futures trading shall implement a hedging position approval system and a speculative position limit system.  
Where a non–futures brokerage company member or an investor applies for hedging positions, it/he shall submit materials such as scope of business, operating performance, and spot purchase-and-sale contracts to the futures exchange; the exchange shall determine the hedging positions upon review. Where an investor applies for hedging positions, the futures brokerage company member shall review the submitted materials and then submit them to the futures exchange for review.  
The futures exchange shall formulate separate maximum position limit standards for speculative positions held by members and investors in non-delivery months and in delivery months.

**Article 44**  
Futures trading shall implement a large-position reporting system.  
The futures exchange may formulate and adjust position reporting standards in light of market risk conditions.

**Article 45**  
Where there is reason to believe that a member or investor has violated the business rules of the futures exchange and is causing or is likely to cause a significant impact on the market, the exchange may, in order to prevent further expansion of the consequences of the violation, take the following temporary disposal measures against such member or investor:  
(1) Restrict deposits;  
(2) Restrict withdrawals;  
(3) Restrict the opening of new positions;  
(4) Raise margin ratios;  
(5) Order position reduction within a specified time;  
(6) Enforce forced liquidation.  
Where a futures exchange takes the measures set out in items (4), (5), or (6) of the preceding paragraph in accordance with procedures prescribed by the business rules, it shall promptly report to the CSRC.  
When taking temporary disposal measures, the futures exchange shall notify the member or investor in the manner prescribed by the business rules and specify the grounds for such measures.

**Article 46**  
When futures prices hit the up-limit or down-limit for several consecutive sessions in the same direction, the futures exchange may adopt measures such as adjusting the limit range, raising trading margin ratios, and reducing positions according to certain principles to defuse risks.

**Article 47**  
Where any of the following circumstances occurs during futures trading, the futures exchange may declare the occurrence of an abnormal situation and take emergency measures to defuse risks:  
(1) Trading cannot proceed normally due to force majeure such as earthquake, flood, or fire, or for reasons not attributable to the exchange such as computer system failures;  
(2) A member experiences a clearing or delivery crisis that is causing or is likely to cause a significant impact on the market;  
(3) The circumstance described in Article 46 of these Measures occurs, and risks remain unresolved after corresponding measures have been taken;  
(4) Other circumstances specified in the business rules of the exchange.  
Before declaring the occurrence of an abnormal situation and deciding to take emergency measures, the futures exchange must report to the CSRC.

**Article 48**  
Where the exchange declares the occurrence of an abnormal situation and decides to suspend trading, the suspension shall not exceed three trading days, unless an extension is approved by the CSRC.

**Article 49**  
A futures exchange shall publish the following information in an appropriate manner:  
(1) Real-time quotations;  
(2) Rankings of open interest and trading volume;  
(3) The number of standard warehouse receipts and available storage capacity;  
(4) Other information specified in the business rules of the exchange.

**Article 50**  
A futures exchange shall compile weekly, monthly, and annual reports on trading conditions within its market and publish them in a timely manner.

**Article 51**  
A futures exchange shall keep records relating to futures trading, settlement, and delivery for not less than 20 years.

# Chapter VI  Supervision and Administration

**Article 52**  
A futures exchange shall formulate measures for investigating and handling violations of its business rules and submit them to the CSRC for approval.  
Where members or investors commit violations related to futures business, the futures exchange shall promptly investigate and handle such violations within the scope of duties prescribed by the measures in the preceding paragraph; matters beyond such scope shall be reported to the CSRC.

**Article 53**  
When formulating or amending the implementing details of its trading rules, a futures exchange shall file them with the CSRC for the record.

**Article 54**  
Without the approval of the CSRC, the chair of the council, the general manager, and deputy general managers of a futures exchange shall not concurrently hold positions in any profit-making organization.  
Non-member directors and staff of a futures exchange shall not, in any form, concurrently hold positions in member entities of the exchange or other profit-making entities related to futures trading.

**Article 55**  
Staff of a futures exchange shall consciously abide by relevant laws, regulations, rules, and policies; perform their duties faithfully and diligently; uphold honesty and creditworthiness; and possess good professional ethics.  
Staff of a futures exchange shall not seek benefits from members of the exchange or from futures investors, and shall not, directly or indirectly, engage in futures trading.

**Article 56**  
A futures exchange shall fulfill the following reporting obligations to the CSRC:  
(1) Within three months after the end of each year, submit financial accounting reports audited by an accounting firm with qualifications in securities and futures;  
(2) Within 15 days after the end of each quarter and within 30 days after the end of each year, submit quarterly and annual work reports on its operations and on the implementation of relevant laws, regulations, rules, and policies;  
(3) Other reporting matters as required by the CSRC.

**Article 57**  
Upon the occurrence of major events, a futures exchange shall promptly report to the CSRC.  
“Major events,” as referred to in the preceding paragraph, include:  
(1) Discovery that staff of the futures exchange are engaged in, or may be engaged in, serious violations of relevant state laws, regulations, rules, or policies;  
(2) Litigation involving the futures exchange amounting to 10% or more of its net assets or having a significant impact on its operational risk;  
(3) Other matters specified by the CSRC.

**Article 58**  
Where the CSRC deems that abnormal market conditions have arisen, it may decide to adopt necessary risk-disposal measures such as delayed market opening or suspension of trading.

**Article 59**  
Where the CSRC deems it necessary, it may issue reminders to senior management personnel of a futures exchange.

**Article 60**  
The CSRC may station inspectors at a futures exchange. Inspectors shall perform their duties in accordance with relevant provisions of the CSRC.  
A futures exchange shall cooperate with inspectors in the performance of their duties.

**Article 61**  
A futures exchange shall pay futures market regulatory fees to the CSRC in a timely manner in accordance with relevant state provisions.

# Chapter VII  Supplementary Provisions

**Article 62**  
“Business rules,” as used in these Measures, refers to the trading rules of a futures exchange and their implementing details.

**Article 63**  
These Measures shall come into force on July 1, 2002. The *Measures for the Administration of Futures Exchanges* promulgated on August 31, 1999 shall be repealed simultaneously.
