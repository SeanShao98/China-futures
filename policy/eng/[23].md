# Measures for the Administration of Futures Companies  
(Decree No. 43 of the China Securities Regulatory Commission)

## Chapter I  General Provisions

**Article 1**  
In order to regulate the business activities of futures companies, strengthen the supervision and administration of futures companies, protect the lawful rights and interests of clients, and promote the positive and steady development of the futures market, these Measures are formulated pursuant to the *Company Law*, the *Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, and other laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 2**  
These Measures apply to futures companies established within the territory of the People’s Republic of China.

**Article 3**  
Futures companies shall comply with laws, administrative regulations, and the provisions of the China Securities Regulatory Commission (hereinafter, the “CSRC”), operate prudently, and fulfill their duties of honesty and good faith toward clients.

**Article 4**  
The controlling shareholders, de facto controllers, and other affiliated parties of a futures company shall not abuse their rights, occupy the assets of the futures company, misappropriate client margin or other assets, or otherwise infringe the lawful rights and interests of the futures company or its clients.

**Article 5**  
The CSRC and its dispatched offices shall, in accordance with the law, exercise supervision and administration over futures companies and their branches.  
The China Futures Association and futures exchanges shall exercise self-regulatory administration over futures companies in accordance with the law.  
The futures margin security depository and monitoring institution shall, in accordance with the law, monitor the security of margin funds.

## Chapter II  Establishment, Changes, and Termination of Business

**Article 6**  
An applicant for the establishment of a futures company, in addition to meeting the conditions specified in Article 16 of the *Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, shall also satisfy the following requirements:  
(1) Have no fewer than 15 personnel holding futures practitioner qualifications;  
(2) Have no fewer than 3 senior managers who meet the qualifications for office.

**Article 7**  
Shareholders applying to establish a futures company shall be legal persons incorporated in China. A shareholder holding 5% or more of the equity shall satisfy the following conditions:  
(1) Paid-in capital and net assets each not less than RMB 30 million, with continuous operation for more than two full fiscal years and profits realized in at least one of the most recent two fiscal years; **or** paid-in capital and net assets each not less than RMB 200 million;  
(2) Net assets not less than 50% of paid-in capital, or liabilities not exceeding 50% of net assets, and no other risks that would cause significant uncertainty to the financial condition;  
(3) The aggregate amount of long-term equity investments (including the investment in the futures company) shall not exceed the shareholder’s own net assets;  
(4) No material overdue debts outstanding;  
(5) No administrative or criminal penalties for illegal or non-compliant operations in the past three years;  
(6) Not currently under case-filing investigation or subject to coercive measures by a competent authority due to suspected illegal or non-compliant operations;  
(7) In the past three years, where acting as a shareholder or de facto controller of a financial institution, or as a controlling shareholder or de facto controller of a listed company, has not engaged in bad-faith conduct such as abusing shareholder rights or evading shareholder obligations;  
(8) Its natural-person shareholders, legal representative, or senior managers have not been subjected to market-entry bans for the securities or futures markets, or if they have, at least two years have elapsed since the ban expired; have not had their qualifications as senior managers in the securities or futures industry or as practitioners revoked, or if revoked, at least two years have elapsed since revocation; do not fall under any circumstance listed in the first paragraph of Article 147 of the *Company Law*;  
(9) Do not fall under other circumstances that, according to the prudential regulation principle, the CSRC deems inappropriate for holding equity in a futures company.

**Article 8**  
Where a futures company is to be established with a single shareholder holding 100% equity, in addition to satisfying Article 7, such shareholder’s net capital shall not be less than RMB 1 billion; if the shareholder is not subject to a net capital or similar indicator, its net assets shall not be less than RMB 1.5 billion.

**Article 9**  
Where shareholders that have an affiliated relationship together hold 5% or more of the equity of a futures company, the shareholder with the highest shareholding shall meet the conditions set out in Article 7.  
Where shareholders that have an affiliated relationship together hold 100% of the equity, the shareholder with the highest shareholding shall meet the conditions set out in Article 8.

**Article 10**  
An applicant for the establishment of a futures company shall submit the following materials to the CSRC:  
(1) Application for the establishment of a futures company;  
(2) Draft articles of association;  
(3) Business plan;  
(4) List of promoters and their audit reports;  
(5) List, résumés, and relevant qualification certificates of proposed senior managers and practitioners;  
(6) Draft texts of futures business rules, internal control systems, and risk management systems;  
(7) Documentary evidence regarding premises, equipment, and funds;  
(8) Legal opinion issued by a law firm;  
(9) Other application materials as prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 11**  
A futures company established in accordance with these Measures may engage in commodity futures brokerage business. To engage in other futures business, it shall additionally obtain the corresponding business qualifications.

**Article 12**  
A futures company applying for qualification to conduct financial futures brokerage business shall satisfy the following conditions:  
(1) The risk regulatory indicators have continuously met prescribed standards for the two months preceding the application date;  
(2) Sound corporate governance, risk management system, and internal control system are in place and effectively implemented;  
(3) Compliance with the CSRC’s provisions on the security depository and monitoring of futures margin;  
(4) A detailed plan for engaging in financial futures brokerage business;  
(5) Business facilities and technical systems conform to relevant technical specifications and operate in good condition;  
(6) Senior managers have not been subject to criminal penalties within the past two years, have not been administratively penalized for illegal or non-compliant operations, have no adverse credit records, and are not under investigation by a competent authority for suspected illegal or non-compliant operations;  
(7) No supervisory measures have been taken by the CSRC or its dispatched offices pursuant to Article 59(2) or Article 60 of the *Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*;  
(8) Not under investigation by administrative or judicial authorities for suspected illegal or non-compliant operations;  
(9) No criminal or administrative penalties for illegal or non-compliant operations within the past two years. However, where the controlling shareholder or de facto controller has changed and more than 50% of senior managers have changed, and those senior managers and business leaders responsible for the aforementioned circumstances no longer serve at the company, and rectification has been completed and accepted by the CSRC’s dispatched office at the company’s domicile, this limitation may not apply;  
(10) The controlling shareholder’s net assets are not less than RMB 30 million;  
(11) The controlling shareholder and the de facto controller have not been subjected to criminal penalties or administrative penalties for illegal or non-compliant operations within the past two years, and are not under case-filing investigation by a competent authority for suspected illegal or non-compliant operations;  
(12) Other conditions prescribed by the CSRC based on the principle of prudential regulation.

**Article 13**  
A futures company applying for qualification to conduct financial futures brokerage business shall submit the following materials to the CSRC:  
(1) Application for qualification for financial futures brokerage business;  
(2) Copies of the business license and business permit affixed with the company seal;  
(3) Resolution of the shareholders’ meeting or board of directors regarding the application;  
(4) The company’s risk regulatory reports as of the end of each of the two months preceding the application date, and a written undertaking that the risk regulatory indicators have continuously met prescribed standards during those two months;  
(5) Texts of corporate governance, risk management system, and internal control system and a report on their implementation;  
(6) A business plan for conducting financial futures brokerage;  
(7) A report on the operation of business facilities and technical systems;  
(8) Forms on senior managers, principal departmental heads, and practitioners;  
(9) The prior fiscal year’s financial report audited by an accounting firm with securities and futures-related business qualifications; if the application date falls in the second half of the year, an audited semi-annual financial report shall also be provided;  
(10) The most recent financial report of the controlling shareholder audited by an accounting firm with securities and futures-related business qualifications;  
(11) A legal opinion issued by a law firm as to whether the company satisfies the conditions in Article 12(6), (8), (9), and (11), and as to the legality of the resolutions of the shareholders’ meeting or board of directors;  
(12) Where the circumstance in Article 12(9) exists, a special acceptance opinion on rectification issued by the CSRC’s dispatched office at the company’s domicile;  
(13) Other application materials as prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 14**  
Where any of the following equity change circumstances arises, a futures company shall obtain the approval of the CSRC:  
(1) The shareholding ratio of a single shareholder increases to 5% or more, or the aggregate shareholding ratio of shareholders with an affiliated relationship increases to 5% or more;  
(2) A shareholder holding 5% or more of the equity acquires additional equity, or shareholders with an affiliated relationship and together holding 5% or more of the equity acquire additional equity.

**Article 15**  
Where an equity change falls under Article 14, the following conditions shall be satisfied:  
(1) The equity to be changed is not subject to seizure, freezing, or similar measures;  
(2) There is no cross-shareholding between the futures company and its shareholders, and the futures company does not provide any form of financial support to the equity transferee;  
(3) The shareholders involved satisfy the conditions prescribed in Articles 7, 8, and 9 of these Measures.

**Article 16**  
Where an equity change falls under Article 14, the futures company shall submit the following application materials to the CSRC:  
(1) Application for equity change;  
(2) Resolution of the shareholders’ meeting regarding the equity change;  
(3) Equity transfer agreement and letters of undertaking from the other shareholders waiving their pre-emptive rights;  
(4) A chart showing the equity background of the futures company’s shareholders after the change;  
(5) Information declaration forms for natural persons indirectly holding 5% or more of the company’s equity;  
(6) A statement on whether affiliated relationships will exist among shareholders after the change and whether the company will provide any form of financial support to the equity transferee;  
(7) Relevant resolutions of the shareholders’ meeting or board of directors of shareholders proposing to increase their capital contribution;  
(8) Basic information reports of shareholders proposing to increase their contribution and of shareholders who meet Article 9;  
(9) Audit reports of such shareholders;  
(10) Feasibility reports and plans of such shareholders on investing in the futures company;  
(11) Legal opinion issued by a law firm;  
(12) Other materials prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 17**  
A futures company changing its registered capital shall satisfy the following conditions:  
(1) After the change, the registered capital shall not be less than the minimum registered capital required for the futures business engaged in;  
(2) Simulations of net capital and other financial indicators after the change in registered capital shall meet risk regulatory indicator standards;  
(3) Where registered capital is increased, shareholders proposing to increase their contribution or acquire equity shall meet Articles 7, 8, and 9 of these Measures.

**Article 18**  
A futures company changing its registered capital shall be subject to the CSRC’s review and shall submit the following application materials:  
(1) Application for change of registered capital;  
(2) Resolution of the shareholders’ meeting on the change;  
(3) Contract on the change of shareholders’ contributions and letters of undertaking from other shareholders waiving their pre-emptive rights;  
(4) A detailed plan for the change of registered capital;  
(5) Simulated balance sheet and risk regulatory reports after the change;  
(6) The materials specified in Article 16(4) through (11);  
(7) Other materials prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 19**  
Where, as a result of an equity or registered capital change, a single shareholder or shareholders with an affiliated relationship propose to hold 100% equity of the futures company, the CSRC shall, based on the principle of prudential regulation, examine the matter and decide whether to approve.

**Article 20**  
Where a futures company changes its legal representative, the proposed legal representative shall meet the qualifications for office. The company shall submit the following to the CSRC’s dispatched office at its domicile:  
(1) Application for change of legal representative;  
(2) Resolution of the shareholders’ meeting on the change (if otherwise provided in the articles of association, such provisions shall prevail);  
(3) Proof of the proposed legal representative’s qualifications;  
(4) Other materials prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 21**  
Where a futures company changes its domicile, it shall properly handle clients’ margin and positions, and the proposed new domicile and facilities to be used shall meet the needs of the futures business. Where the domicile is changed across the jurisdictions of different CSRC dispatched offices, the following conditions shall also be satisfied:  
(1) Compliance with the rules on continuity of operation;  
(2) No major records of illegal or non-compliant operations within the past two years and no major risk events;  
(3) Other conditions prescribed by the CSRC based on the principle of prudential regulation.

**Article 22**  
Where a futures company changes its domicile, it shall submit the following to the CSRC’s dispatched office at the proposed new domicile:  
(1) Application for change of domicile;  
(2) A detailed plan for the change;  
(3) Proof of ownership or right of use for the proposed new domicile and a fire safety acceptance certificate;  
(4) A report on the proper handling of clients’ margin and positions;  
(5) Other materials prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 23**  
A futures company applying to establish a business department (营业部) shall satisfy the following conditions:  
(1) Not under investigation by a competent authority for suspected illegal or non-compliant operations, and no administrative or criminal penalties for illegal or non-compliant operations within the past one year;  
(2) Compliance with risk regulatory indicator standards for the three months preceding the application date;  
(3) Compliance with relevant provisions on client asset protection and the security depository and monitoring of futures margin;  
(4) Corporate governance and internal control systems comply with relevant provisions and are effectively implemented;  
(5) The proposed person-in-charge meets the qualifications for office, and business post personnel hold futures practitioner qualifications;  
(6) Clear and reasonable division of responsibilities among business posts, compatible with the operating plan of the business department;  
(7) Business premises and facilities that meet the needs of the futures business;  
(8) Other conditions prescribed by the CSRC based on the principle of prudential regulation.

**Article 24**  
A futures company applying to establish a business department shall submit the following to the CSRC’s dispatched office at the proposed location of the business department:  
(1) Application for establishment of a business department;  
(2) Resolution on the establishment;  
(3) Risk regulatory reports as of the end of each of the three months preceding the application date;  
(4) Texts of management systems for the business department;  
(5) Application materials or proofs of the proposed person-in-charge’s qualifications;  
(6) A roster of proposed practitioners and copies of their futures practitioner qualification certificates;  
(7) Proof of ownership or right of use for the business premises and a fire safety acceptance certificate;  
(8) Other materials prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 25**  
Where the person-in-charge of a business department is to be changed, the proposed person-in-charge shall meet the corresponding qualifications for office. The futures company shall submit an application for the change and qualification proofs of the proposed person-in-charge to the CSRC’s dispatched office at the business department’s location.

**Article 26**  
Where a business department changes its business premises, the clients’ margin and positions shall be properly handled, and the proposed new premises and facilities shall meet the needs of the futures business. The futures company shall submit the following to the CSRC’s dispatched office at the business department’s location:  
(1) Application for change of business department premises;  
(2) A detailed plan for the change;  
(3) A report on the proper handling of clients’ margin and positions;  
(4) Proof of ownership or right of use for the proposed new premises and a fire safety acceptance certificate;  
(5) Other materials prescribed by the CSRC.  
For purposes of these Measures, a change of business department premises is limited to changes within the jurisdiction of the same CSRC dispatched office.

**Article 27**  
Where a business department is to be terminated, the clients’ margin and other assets of that department shall first be properly handled, futures business shall be settled, and business activities shall cease. The futures company shall submit the following to the CSRC’s dispatched office at the department’s location:  
(1) Application for termination of the business department;  
(2) Resolution on the termination;  
(3) A report on the handling of clients’ margin and other assets, settlement of futures business, and cessation of business activities;  
(4) Other materials prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 28**  
Where a futures company applies to suspend business due to force majeure or other legitimate causes, it shall properly handle clients’ margin and other assets and return or transfer clients.  
To resume business, a futures company shall comply with the rules on continuity of operation. If, upon expiry of the suspension period, the company still cannot resume business or still fails to meet continuity rules, the CSRC may cancel its futures business permit pursuant to Article 21(1) of the *Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*.

**Article 29**  
A futures company applying for suspension of business shall submit the following to the CSRC:  
(1) Application for suspension;  
(2) Resolution on the suspension;  
(3) A report on the handling of clients’ margin and other assets and the settlement of futures business;  
(4) Other materials prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 30**  
Where a futures company dissolves or is declared bankrupt, it shall first properly handle clients’ margin and other assets and settle futures business.  
Where all futures business permits of a futures company are revoked, it shall properly handle clients’ margin and other assets and settle futures business; if the company continues to exist, it shall duly handle industrial and commercial registration changes regarding name, scope of business, and articles of association. The surviving company shall not continue to engage in futures business in the name of a futures company, and its name shall not include the word “futures” or similar wording.

**Article 31**  
Where a futures company is established, undergoes changes, dissolves, bankrupts, or has its futures business permits revoked, or where any of its business departments is established, changed, or terminated, the futures company shall publish an announcement in newspapers or media designated by the CSRC.

**Article 32**  
The licenses of futures companies and their business departments shall be uniformly printed by the CSRC. Where the original or duplicate of a license is lost or destroyed, the futures company shall, within 30 days, declare it void in newspapers or media designated by the CSRC and apply to the CSRC for reissuance with the published declaration.

## Chapter III  Corporate Governance

**Article 33**  
A futures company shall, in accordance with the principles of clear division of responsibilities, strengthened checks and balances, and enhanced risk management, establish and improve its corporate governance.

**Article 34**  
A futures company and its controlling shareholder shall strictly separate business, personnel, assets, finance, and premises, and shall operate and account independently.  
The controlling shareholder or de facto controller shall not, beyond the authority of the shareholders’ meeting or the board of directors of the futures company, appoint or remove the company’s directors, supervisors, or senior managers, nor illegally interfere in operating and management activities such as margin custody, trading, settlement, risk management, financial accounting, or business department management.  
A futures company shall not make commitments to shareholders regarding minimum returns or dividends. Where the company provides brokerage services to shareholders, de facto controllers, or their affiliates, it shall not lower risk management requirements.

**Article 35**  
The shareholders’ meeting shall deliberate and vote on matters within its authority in accordance with the *Company Law* and the articles of association, and shall convene at least once a year.  
Shareholders shall exercise voting rights in proportion to their capital contributions.

**Article 36**  
Where any of the following circumstances arises for a shareholder or de facto controller, they shall notify the futures company within three days:  
(1) The company equity they hold is frozen, seized, or subject to compulsory execution;  
(2) They pledge the company equity they hold;  
(3) They decide to transfer the company equity they hold;  
(4) They cannot normally exercise shareholder rights or undertake shareholder obligations, potentially causing major defects in corporate governance;  
(5) They are investigated or subject to coercive measures by a competent authority for suspected serious illegal or non-compliant operations;  
(6) Change of name;  
(7) Merger, division, or major asset or debt restructuring;  
(8) Being revoked, taken over, placed under trusteeship, closed, or dissolved or bankrupt;  
(9) Other circumstances that may affect changes in the company’s equity.  
Where any of the foregoing occurs, the futures company and the relevant shareholders shall, within five days, submit a written report to the CSRC’s dispatched office at the company’s domicile; where any of items (5) through (8) occurs to the de facto controller, the futures company and its de facto controller shall, within five days, submit a written report to the same.

**Article 37**  
A futures company shall immediately notify all shareholders in writing and report to the CSRC’s dispatched office at its domicile if any of the following occurs:  
(1) The company or any of its directors, supervisors, or senior managers is placed on file for investigation by a competent authority or subjected to coercive measures due to suspected major illegal or non-compliant conduct;  
(2) The company plans to replace its chairman or general manager;  
(3) The company’s financial condition deteriorates and no longer meets the risk regulatory indicator standards prescribed by the CSRC;  
(4) Major client overdrafts or “run-through” losses (穿仓) occur;  
(5) Sudden incidents occur that have or may have a material adverse impact on the company or client interests;  
(6) Other circumstances that may affect the company’s sustainable operation.  
Rectification notices, supervisory measures, and administrative penalties imposed by the CSRC or its dispatched offices on the company or its business departments shall be notified in writing by the company to all shareholders.

**Article 38**  
A futures company shall establish a board of directors. The board shall convene at least two meetings each year. Minutes of board meetings shall be true, accurate, and complete.

**Article 39**  
In addition to exercising the powers provided in the *Company Law*, the board of directors of a futures company shall also perform the following duties:  
(1) Deliberate and decide on the system for the secure custody of client margin, ensuring client margin custody complies with the requirements on client asset protection and the security depository and monitoring of futures margin;  
(2) Deliberate and decide on risk management and internal control systems.

**Article 40**  
Futures companies that have clearing qualifications at futures exchanges implementing a tiered member clearing system, as well as wholly-owned futures companies, shall appoint independent directors.  
Independent directors shall remain independent, shall not hold any position in the company other than director, and shall not have relationships with the company, its controlling shareholder, de facto controller, or other affiliated parties that may impede independent and objective judgment.  
Independent directors shall comply with laws, administrative regulations, and the CSRC’s provisions, abide by the articles of association, owe duties of loyalty and diligence to the company, and safeguard the lawful rights and interests of clients, the company, and all shareholders. Other directors, supervisors, and senior managers shall actively cooperate with and assist independent directors in performing their duties.

**Article 41**  
A futures company shall establish a board of supervisors or supervisors in accordance with the *Company Law*, and effectively ensure the right of the board of supervisors or supervisors to be informed of the company’s operations. The board of supervisors or supervisors shall perform their duties pursuant to the *Company Law* and the articles of association.

**Article 42**  
The articles of association shall specify the responsibilities to be borne by the legal representative and the corresponding accountability procedures where, in externally representing the company in business activities, the legal representative violates the company’s business plan as decided by the board or violates systems such as margin custody, risk management, internal control, or other board resolutions.

**Article 43**  
A futures company shall establish the position of chief risk officer, who shall supervise and inspect the legality and compliance of the company’s management actions and its risk management.  
Where the chief risk officer discovers suspected illegal or non-compliant conduct such as occupying or misappropriating client margin or other potential risks, the officer shall immediately report to the CSRC’s dispatched office and the company’s board of directors.  
Where the company intends to dismiss the chief risk officer, there shall be a legitimate reason, and the company shall report to the CSRC’s dispatched office. Where the chief risk officer fails to perform duties, the CSRC and its dispatched offices have the authority to order a replacement.

**Article 44**  
The chairman, general manager, and chief risk officer shall not be close relatives of one another. The offices of chairman and general manager shall not be held concurrently by the same person.

**Article 45**  
A futures company shall establish business departments and functions rationally, create post-responsibility systems for trading, clearing, risk management, and finance, implement key controls over critical posts and businesses, and ensure segregation among the front, middle, and back offices.  
Trading, clearing, and finance shall be conducted by different departments and personnel.

**Article 46**  
A futures company shall establish a risk management department or post to manage and control operational risks.  
It shall establish a compliance review department or post to review and audit the legality and compliance of management actions.

**Article 47**  
A futures company shall, in accordance with the CSRC’s provisions, implement centralized and unified settlement, unified risk management, unified fund allocation, and unified financial management and accounting for its business departments, and establish standardized and comprehensive post-responsibility systems and operational procedures for such departments.  
A futures company shall not operate or manage a business department in joint venture, cooperation, or consortium with any other party, nor subcontract, lease, or entrust the operation or management of a business department to others.

## Chapter IV  Brokerage Business Rules

**Article 48**  
A futures company shall, in line with the principle of prudent operation, establish and effectively implement business systems and processes such as risk management, internal control, and margin custody, maintain financial soundness and continuously meet the CSRC’s risk regulatory indicator standards, and ensure the security of client trading and assets.

**Article 49**  
A futures company shall follow the principle of honesty and good faith, execute client instructions with professional skills and due diligence, and safeguard clients’ lawful rights and interests.  
A futures company shall avoid conflicts of interest with clients, and where unavoidable, shall ensure that client interests take precedence.

**Article 50**  
Unless otherwise provided in Article 26 of the *Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, the following personnel shall not, in their own name or in the name of others, engage in futures trading:  
(1) Persons without civil capacity or with limited civil capacity;  
(2) Staff of futures companies and their spouses;  
(3) Staff of the CSRC and its dispatched offices, futures exchanges, the futures margin security depository and monitoring institution, and the China Futures Association, and their spouses.

**Article 51**  
To open an account, a client must present proof of PRC citizen identity, or lawful documents proving PRC legal person status or other economic organization status, unless otherwise provided by the CSRC.

**Article 52**  
Before opening an account for a client, a futures company shall present to the client the *Futures Trading Risk Disclosure Statement*, obtain the client’s signature confirming that it understands the contents of the statement, and sign a Futures Brokerage Agreement. A futures company shall not open an account for any client who has not signed a Futures Brokerage Agreement.  
The content and format of the *Guidelines on the Futures Brokerage Agreement* and the *Futures Trading Risk Disclosure Statement* shall be formulated by the China Futures Association.  
A futures company shall update its standard form of the Futures Brokerage Agreement in a timely manner in accordance with the *Guidelines on the Futures Brokerage Agreement*, submit it to the China Futures Association for review and filing, and file it with the CSRC’s dispatched office at its domicile.  
Advertising and promotional materials released by a futures company shall be filed with the CSRC’s dispatched office at its domicile within five working days from the date of release.

**Article 53**  
A futures company shall fully disclose the risks of futures trading to clients, keep on its business premises the relevant laws and regulations on futures trading and the business rules of futures exchanges, and make public its brokerage business processes and qualification certificates of relevant practitioners for client inspection.  
A futures company shall, in the Futures Brokerage Agreement, on its website, and at its business premises, remind clients that the practitioners’ qualification information can be checked on the China Futures Association’s website.

**Article 54**  
A futures company shall apply for trading codes for clients as prescribed. When closing a client’s account, it shall, as prescribed, promptly apply to the futures exchange to cancel the client’s trading code.

**Article 55**  
Where a client needs to entrust another to place orders, transfer funds, or handle other matters, the client shall designate the trustee and specify the scope of authority in the Futures Brokerage Agreement, agree on the contact methods and order placement methods, and provide the trustee’s specimen signature.

**Article 56**  
Clients may place trading orders by written form, telephone, computer, the Internet, or other entrusted methods. Where written orders are used, the client shall complete a written order form; where orders are placed by telephone, the futures company shall synchronize audio recording; where orders are placed by computer, the Internet, or other entrusted methods, the futures company shall preserve the order in an appropriate manner.

**Article 57**  
A futures company providing clients with Internet order placement services shall establish an Internet trading risk management system and give special risk warnings regarding Internet trading.

**Article 58**  
A futures company shall transmit client trading orders according to the time priority principle.

**Article 59**  
A futures company shall, in the Futures Brokerage Agreement, agree on the standards, conditions, and disposal measures for risk management.

**Article 60**  
A futures company shall provide clients with a trading settlement report after the close of trading each day. Clients shall check the contents of the report according to the time and method agreed in the Futures Brokerage Agreement.  
A futures company shall perform daily settlement for clients based on the settlement results of the futures exchange or an institution with clearing qualifications, and the content, format, handling method, and date of settlement subjects shall be consistent with those of the futures exchange.  
A futures company shall, in the Futures Brokerage Agreement, on its website, and at its business premises, remind clients that they may check trading settlement results and other information relating to futures trading via the inquiry service system of the futures margin security depository and monitoring institution.

**Article 61**  
Where a client has objections to the contents of the trading settlement report, the client shall submit a written objection to the futures company within the time agreed in the Futures Brokerage Agreement; where the client has no objection, the client shall confirm the report as agreed. Where the client neither confirms the report nor raises objections within the agreed time, the report shall be deemed confirmed.  
Where a client raises objections, the futures company shall verify them within the time agreed in the Futures Brokerage Agreement.

**Article 62**  
A futures company shall formulate and implement business rules for handling erroneous orders.

**Article 63**  
A futures company shall establish client data archives and, except when legally subject to investigation and inspection, shall keep client information confidential.

**Article 64**  
A futures company shall establish and improve a client complaint handling system, and shall archive client complaint materials and the handling results.

**Article 65**  
Disputes arising from futures business between futures companies, or between a futures company and a client, may be submitted to the China Futures Association or a futures exchange for mediation.

**Article 66**  
Where the entrustment relationship between a client and a futures company is terminated, the relevant account-closing procedures shall be completed. A futures company shall not lend a client’s unclosed fund account or trading code to others.

**Article 67**  
A futures company shall establish backup systems for trading, clearing, and financial data.  
Client data archives relating to account opening, changes, and account closing shall be kept for at least 20 years from the termination of the Futures Brokerage Agreement; records of trading orders, trading settlements, erroneous orders, client complaints, and other business records shall be kept for at least 20 years.  
Where a futures company preserves or backs up relevant materials in electronic form, it shall ensure the authenticity and reliability of the electronic data, take effective measures to prevent tampering or destruction, and ensure that the preserved electronic data can be converted into paper form at any time.

**Article 68**  
A futures company may entrust other institutions approved by the CSRC to engage in introducing broker (intermediary introduction) business.

## Chapter V  Protection of Client Assets

**Article 69**  
Futures margin funds held in custody by a futures company belong to the clients. Except for transfers of client margin pursuant to Article 29 of the *Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, no entity or individual may occupy or misappropriate such funds in any form. In the event of the bankruptcy or liquidation of a futures company, client margin and other assets used to offset margin shall not constitute bankruptcy or liquidation property. Unless due to the client’s own debts or as otherwise provided by laws or administrative regulations, client margin and other assets used to offset margin shall not be sealed, frozen, deducted, or subject to compulsory execution.  
Client margin shall be independent from the futures company’s own assets and managed separately.

**Article 70**  
A futures company shall open futures margin accounts at banks approved for the custody of futures margin.  
Where a futures company opens, changes, or cancels a futures margin account, it shall, on the same day, file with the CSRC’s dispatched office at its domicile and with the futures margin security depository and monitoring institution, and disclose to clients, by prescribed means, the opening, change, or cancellation of such accounts.  
Clients shall deposit margin into the futures margin accounts disclosed by the futures company through the website of the futures margin security depository and monitoring institution.  
A futures margin account refers to a special deposit account opened by a futures company at a margin depository bank for holding and managing client margin, including a special fund account opened at the location of the futures exchange for handling funds movements with the exchange.

**Article 71**  
Client margin held in custody by a futures company shall be fully deposited in futures margin accounts and the futures exchange’s special clearing accounts. It is strictly prohibited to deposit client margin outside such accounts.

**Article 72**  
Clients shall register with the futures company a futures settlement account opened in their own name for depositing and withdrawing margin.  
The futures company and clients shall transfer margin through filed futures margin accounts and registered futures settlement accounts.

**Article 73**  
A futures company shall, in accordance with the provisions on the security depository and monitoring of futures margin, promptly submit information to the futures margin security depository and monitoring institution.

**Article 74**  
Where a margin depository bank fails to submit relevant futures margin information to the futures margin security depository and monitoring institution in accordance with the CSRC’s provisions, the futures company shall, as required by the CSRC and its dispatched offices, transfer margin to another depository bank that meets the requirements.

**Article 75**  
A futures company shall, in accordance with the rules of the futures exchange, use its own funds to pay the clearing guarantee fund and clearing reserve, and maintain the minimum amount of clearing reserves and other special funds, so as to ensure the normal conduct of clients’ futures trading and the security of client margin.

**Article 76**  
Where a client’s breach in futures trading results in insufficient margin, the futures company shall make advances with its risk reserve and own funds, and shall not occupy other clients’ margin.  
A futures company shall draw, manage, and use its risk reserve in accordance with regulations, and shall not divert it for other uses.

## Chapter VI  Supervision and Administration

**Article 77**  
A futures company shall, as prescribed, regularly submit annual reports, monthly reports, and other relevant materials.  
Directors, senior managers, and the person in charge of finance shall sign confirmation opinions on the annual report; the legal representative, the principal person in charge of operations and management, and the person in charge of finance shall sign confirmation opinions on the monthly report.  
Persons who sign the annual or monthly reports shall ensure the reports are true, accurate, and complete; where there is disagreement with the report, they shall state their own opinions and reasons.

**Article 78**  
The CSRC and its dispatched offices may require the following institutions or individuals to submit, within a specified time limit, materials related to the operation and management and financial condition of a futures company:  
(1) The futures company and its directors, supervisors, senior managers, and other staff;  
(2) The shareholders, de facto controllers, or other affiliates of the futures company;  
(3) Intermediary service institutions such as accounting firms, law firms, and asset appraisal institutions that provide services to the futures company.

**Article 79**  
Where shareholders, de facto controllers, or other affiliates of a futures company conduct futures trading at the company, the company shall, within five working days from the date of account opening, file with the CSRC’s dispatched office at its domicile, regularly report the relevant trading to all shareholders, the board of directors, and the board of supervisors or the supervisors, and regularly report such trading to the CSRC’s dispatched office at its domicile.

**Article 80**  
Where any of the following occurs, a futures company shall, within five working days, submit a written report to the CSRC’s dispatched office at its domicile:  
(1) Change of name or articles of association;  
(2) Equity changes other than those specified in Article 14 of these Measures;  
(3) Major resolutions such as termination of business;  
(4) Appointment or removal of directors, supervisors, senior managers, person in charge of finance, or person in charge of a business department, or changes in the division of responsibilities among senior managers;  
(5) Being placed on file for investigation or subjected to coercive measures by a competent authority;  
(6) Other matters prescribed by the CSRC.  
Where a major event occurs that affects or may affect the company’s operation and management, financial condition, or client asset security, the futures company shall immediately report to the CSRC’s dispatched office, explaining the cause of the event, current status, possible consequences, and response plans or measures.

**Article 81**  
Where a futures company engages or dismisses an accounting firm, it shall report to the CSRC’s dispatched office at its domicile within five working days from the date of decision; where it dismisses an accounting firm, it shall state the reasons.  
Where the CSRC’s dispatched office has reason to believe that an accounting firm is not suitable to engage in auditing business for a futures company, it may require the company to replace it.

**Article 82**  
A futures company shall disclose information on its basic circumstances and operating and management conditions in accordance with the CSRC’s provisions.

**Article 83**  
Materials and information submitted, provided, or disclosed by relevant institutions and individuals shall be true, accurate, and complete, and shall not contain false records, misleading statements, or material omissions.

**Article 84**  
The CSRC may organize its dispatched offices to conduct regular or ad hoc on-site inspections of futures companies or business departments.  
The CSRC’s dispatched offices may conduct on-site inspections of futures companies or business departments within their jurisdiction.  
When conducting inspections in accordance with the law, there shall be no fewer than two inspectors, who shall present valid identification and inspection notices, and shall not disclose any trade secrets obtained in the course of duty.

**Article 85**  
The CSRC and its dispatched offices may take the following measures to inspect the operation and management, business activities, and financial conditions of a futures company or its business department:  
(1) Interview staff of the futures company and its business departments and require explanations and clarifications on matters under inspection;  
(2) Review and copy documents and materials related to the matters under inspection;  
(3) Inquire into the futures margin accounts of the company and its business departments;  
(4) Inspect the company’s and its business departments’ computer systems for trading, clearing, and finance.

**Article 86**  
Where the CSRC or its dispatched offices believe a futures company may be in any of the following circumstances, it may require the company to engage intermediary service institutions to conduct special audits, assessments, or issue legal opinions:  
(1) The company’s annual, monthly, or interim reports contain false records, misleading statements, or material omissions;  
(2) Violations of provisions relating to client asset protection, the security depository and monitoring of margin, or risk regulatory indicator management;  
(3) Other significant circumstances determined by the CSRC based on the principle of prudential regulation.  
A futures company shall cooperate with the relevant intermediary service institutions and truthfully provide relevant documents and materials.

**Article 87**  
Where a futures company and its shareholders, de facto controllers, or other affiliates, or intermediary service institutions such as accounting firms, law firms, and asset appraisal institutions that provide services to the company, are suspected of violating these Measures, the CSRC and its dispatched offices may conduct regulatory interviews with their responsible persons and relevant staff, order corrections, and issue warning letters.

**Article 88**  
Where a futures company or its business department falls into any of the following circumstances, the CSRC and its dispatched offices may, pursuant to Article 59 of the *Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, take corresponding supervisory measures:  
(1) Corporate governance is unsound or not effectively implemented; there are significant defects in departmental or post settings; key business posts or personnel are vacant or fail to perform due duties, potentially affecting the company’s sustainable operation;  
(2) Brokerage business rules are unsound or not effectively implemented; there are significant defects in risk management or internal control; management is chaotic, potentially affecting the company’s sustainable operation or client trading security;  
(3) Clearing business rules are unsound or not effectively implemented, potentially damaging the lawful rights and interests of other futures companies or clients;  
(4) Non-compliance with client asset protection or margin security depository and monitoring provisions, potentially affecting client asset security;  
(5) Failure to implement centralized and unified management of business departments as prescribed, with significant operational risks or hidden dangers;  
(6) Failure to entrust introducing broker business as prescribed, with significant business risks or hidden dangers;  
(7) Major defects in trading, clearing, or financial systems that may cause data distortion or damage clients’ lawful rights and interests;  
(8) Suspension of operations, major risks, or suspected serious illegal or non-compliant circumstances arise for shareholders, de facto controllers, or other affiliates, potentially affecting corporate governance or sustainable operation;  
(9) Disputes, arbitrations, or litigations that may affect financial soundness;  
(10) Reports, materials, or information submitted, provided, or issued contain falsehoods, misleading statements, or omissions;  
(11) Non-compliance with other continuity rules or the emergence of other operational risks.  
For business departments that still fail to meet operating conditions after rectification, the CSRC’s dispatched office has the authority to close them according to law.

**Article 89**  
Without the CSRC’s approval, where any individual or entity and its affiliates hold 5% or more of a futures company’s equity without authorization, or become a shareholder by submitting false application materials, the CSRC may order the transfer of such equity within a time limit.  
Such equity shall not carry voting or dividend rights prior to the transfer.

**Article 90**  
Where any of the following circumstances occurs to shareholders, de facto controllers, or other affiliates of a futures company, the CSRC and its dispatched offices may order rectification within a time limit:  
(1) Occupation of the company’s assets in a manner that may affect the company’s sustainable operation;  
(2) Direct appointment or removal of the company’s directors, supervisors, or senior managers, or illegal interference in the company’s operating and management activities;  
(3) Failure to exercise voting rights in proportion to capital contributions;  
(4) Reports, materials, or information submitted, provided, or issued contain falsehoods, misleading statements, or omissions.  
Where the circumstances in the preceding paragraph cause the company to fail to meet continuity rules or create operational risks, the CSRC and its dispatched offices may, pursuant to Article 59 of the *Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, order the controlling shareholder to transfer equity or restrict its exercise of shareholder rights.

## Chapter VII  Legal Liability

**Article 91**  
Without the CSRC’s approval, where any individual or entity and its affiliates hold 5% or more of a futures company’s equity without authorization, or become a shareholder by submitting false application materials, and the circumstances are serious, a warning shall be given and a fine of up to RMB 30,000 may be imposed, either separately or concurrently.

**Article 92**  
Where a futures company or its business department accepts entrustment for futures trading from a unit or individual that has not completed account-opening procedures, or lends a client’s fund account or trading code to other units or individuals, a warning shall be given and a fine of up to RMB 30,000 may be imposed, either separately or concurrently.

**Article 93**  
Where intermediary service institutions such as accounting firms, law firms, or asset appraisal institutions fail to perform reporting obligations as prescribed, or the reports, materials, or opinions they provide or issue are incomplete, they shall be ordered to make corrections, their business income shall be confiscated, and a fine of up to RMB 30,000 may be imposed, either separately or concurrently. The directly responsible persons in charge and other responsible persons shall be given warnings and may be fined up to RMB 30,000.

**Article 94**  
Where a futures company or its business department commits any of the following acts, penalties shall be imposed pursuant to Article 70 of the *Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*:  
(1) Failing to manage client margin independently and separately from the company’s own assets as prescribed;  
(2) Failing to pay the clearing guarantee fund or clearing reserve as prescribed, or failing to maintain the minimum amount of clearing reserves and other special funds;  
(3) Lowering risk management requirements for futures trading by shareholders, de facto controllers, or their affiliates, thereby infringing the lawful rights and interests of other clients;  
(4) Establishing a business department by way of joint venture, cooperation, or consortium, or subcontracting or leasing a business department to others, or violating the centralized and unified management provisions for business departments;  
(5) Depositing client margin outside futures margin accounts and the futures exchange’s special clearing accounts;  
(6) Submitting information to the margin security depository and monitoring institution that contains falsehoods, misleading statements, or material omissions;  
(7) Occupying client margin;  
(8) Violating the CSRC’s provisions on the administration of clearing business and using clearing relationships to damage the lawful rights and interests of other futures companies or their clients;  
(9) Violating provisions on entrusting introducing broker business and harming clients’ lawful rights and interests;  
(10) Violating the CSRC’s provisions on risk regulatory indicators;  
(11) Refusing to cooperate with, obstructing, or undermining the supervision and administration by the CSRC and its dispatched offices;  
(12) Violating provisions on the administration of the futures investor protection fund;  
(13) Violating provisions on using marketable securities to offset margin.

**Article 95**  
Where a futures company or its business department commits any of the following, penalties shall be imposed pursuant to Article 71 of the *Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*:  
(1) Publishing false advertisements or conducting false publicity to induce clients to participate in futures trading;  
(2) Failing to change or cancel futures margin accounts as prescribed, or failing to disclose information on futures margin accounts to clients in the prescribed manner.

## Chapter VIII  Supplementary Provisions

**Article 96**  
Where Hong Kong or Macao service providers invest in futures companies, the relevant CSRC provisions shall apply.

**Article 97**  
These Measures shall come into force on April 15, 2007. The *Measures for the Administration of Futures Brokerage Companies* (CSRC Decree No. 7) promulgated on May 17, 2002, the CSRC’s *Notice on Issues Concerning the Establishment, Change, and Termination of Business Departments of Futures Brokerage Companies* (Zhengjian Qihuo Zi [2004] No. 41), the *Notice on Issues Concerning Changes in Legal Representative, Registered Capital, Shareholders or Equity Structure, and Domicile of Futures Brokerage Companies* (Zhengjian Qihuo Zi [2004] No. 42), the *Notice on Issues Concerning the Establishment, Dissolution, and Merger of Futures Brokerage Companies* (Zhengjian Qihuo Zi [2004] No. 46), and the *Notice on the Qualifications of Shareholders of Futures Brokerage Companies and Related Issues* (Zhengjian Qihuo Zi [2005] No. 155) shall be repealed simultaneously.  

> **Note:** This regulation was subsequently repealed by the *Measures for the Supervision and Administration of Futures Companies* (CSRC Decree No. 110, October 29, 2014).
