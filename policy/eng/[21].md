# Measures for the Administration of Futures Brokerage Companies  
(“Zhengjian Qihuo Zi [1999] No. 43”)

## Chapter I  General Provisions

**Article 1**  
To regulate futures brokerage activities, strengthen the supervision and administration of futures brokerage companies, and protect the lawful rights and interests of futures investors, these Measures are formulated pursuant to the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*.

**Article 2**  
These Measures apply to futures brokerage activities conducted within the territory of the People’s Republic of China.

**Article 3**  
“Futures brokerage,” as used in these Measures, refers to the business activity whereby, upon a client’s entrustment and according to the client’s instructions, a broker executes futures trades in its own name for the client, charges handling fees, and the trading results are borne by the client.

**Article 4**  
Any entity operating futures brokerage business must be a futures brokerage company lawfully established.  
No other entity or individual may operate futures brokerage business.

**Article 5**  
The China Securities Regulatory Commission (hereinafter, the “CSRC”) shall, in accordance with law, exercise centralized and unified supervision and administration over all futures brokerage companies nationwide.  
The CSRC’s local offices—regional securities regulatory offices, agencies, and resident commissioners’ offices (hereinafter, the “CSRC local offices”)—shall regulate futures brokerage companies in accordance with these Measures and as authorized by the CSRC.

## Chapter II  Establishment, Change, and Termination

### Section 1  Establishment

**Article 6**  
The establishment of a futures brokerage company is subject to CSRC approval. Without approval, no entity or individual may establish or in a disguised form establish a futures brokerage company.  
A futures brokerage company’s name shall include the words “Futures Brokerage.” No other entity or individual may use “Futures Brokerage” or similar wording.

**Article 7**  
A futures brokerage company may engage in futures brokerage and other businesses approved by the CSRC.  
A futures brokerage company shall not engage in businesses beyond its approved scope; shall not become an investor bearing unlimited liability; shall not speculate in stocks in violation of regulations; and, except for providing the performance guarantee stipulated in Article 44 of the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, shall not provide guarantees for others.

**Article 8**  
To establish a futures brokerage company, the following conditions shall be met:  
(1) Minimum registered capital of RMB 30 million, of which cash contribution shall be no less than RMB 18 million;  
(2) A standardized articles of association;  
(3) Senior management personnel who meet qualification requirements;  
(4) At least 15 other practitioners holding practice qualifications;  
(5) A sound organizational structure and management systems;  
(6) Brokerage business rules, financial and accounting systems, risk management and internal control systems that meet requirements;  
(7) Business premises and other facilities that meet requirements;  
(8) Other conditions prescribed by laws, regulations, and the CSRC.

**Article 9**  
Shareholders of a futures brokerage company must be PRC enterprise legal persons and comply with applicable laws, regulations, rules, and policies.

**Article 10**  
An applicant for establishment shall first submit to the CSRC:  
(1) An application stating the proposed company’s name, domicile, business premises, registered capital, etc.;  
(2) A feasibility report;  
(3) Other materials required by the CSRC.

**Article 11**  
After preliminary approval, the applicant shall conduct preparation in accordance with the *Company Law* and these Measures. If the conditions in Article 8 are met, the applicant shall submit to the CSRC:  
(1) Draft articles of association;  
(2) Shareholder register and details of capital contributions;  
(3) Audited financial reports of shareholders for the most recent two years by an accounting firm with appropriate qualifications;  
(4) Capital verification certificate issued by a statutory capital verification institution;  
(5) Risk disclosure statement for futures trading, a standard futures brokerage agreement, brokerage business rules, financial system, risk management and internal control systems;  
(6) Lists of proposed directors and supervisors, list of senior managers with proof of qualification;  
(7) Lists of other proposed employees with practice qualifications;  
(8) Proof of ownership or right to use business premises;  
(9) Description of facilities related to the brokerage business;  
(10) Pre-approval of name from the State Administration for Industry and Commerce (SAIC);  
(11) Other materials required by the CSRC.

**Article 12**  
A futures brokerage company may establish business departments (branches) as needed, subject to CSRC approval.  
A business department does not have legal person status; it conducts business within the scope authorized by the company, and the company bears its civil liabilities.  
The business department’s name must read “×× Futures Brokerage Co., Ltd. ×× Business Department.”

**Article 13**  
To apply to establish a business department, a company shall meet:  
(1) No major violations in the preceding year;  
(2) Good operating performance at already approved business departments;  
(3) Managerial personnel that meet requirements and at least three other practitioners;  
(4) Complete management systems for business departments;  
(5) Business premises and facilities that meet requirements;  
(6) Other conditions prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 14**  
To apply to establish a business department, the company shall first submit to the CSRC:  
(1) An application stating the proposed name and business premises;  
(2) A feasibility report;  
(3) Other materials required by the CSRC.

**Article 15**  
After preliminary approval, the applicant shall prepare for establishment and, upon meeting Article 13 conditions, submit to the CSRC:  
(1) Audited financial report of the applicant for the preceding year;  
(2) Shareholders’ meeting or board resolution on establishing the business department;  
(3) Practice qualification certificates of proposed staff of the business department;  
(4) Proof of ownership or right to use the premises;  
(5) Description of facilities related to the brokerage business;  
(6) Opinions of the CSRC local office where the company is located;  
(7) The company’s risk management, internal control and other management systems applicable to the business department;  
(8) Other materials required by the CSRC.

**Article 16**  
A business department shall not be established through joint ventures or cooperation; shall not be contracted out or leased; and shall not set up additional business premises.

**Article 17**  
A futures brokerage company shall not, under the guise of a “representative office,” “liaison office,” etc., in effect establish a business department to engage in brokerage business.

**Article 18**  
Applications to establish a company or business department shall be preliminarily reviewed by the CSRC local office at the proposed place of establishment.  
Where the local office decides to approve, it shall issue a preliminary opinion and submit it to the CSRC for approval; where it decides not to approve, it shall provide a written explanation to the applicant.

**Article 19**  
Within one month from receipt of compliant application materials, the CSRC shall notify the applicant in writing whether approval is granted. If not approved, reasons shall be stated.

**Article 20**  
In reviewing applications, the CSRC and its local offices shall consider the needs of market development and the geographic layout of the brokerage industry.

**Article 21**  
Upon obtaining a *Futures Brokerage Business License* or a *Business Department Operating License* from the CSRC, the applicant shall file for registration with the SAIC within 90 days and obtain a business license.  
If, without just cause, registration is not completed within 90 days of obtaining the license, the license becomes void.

**Article 22**  
Where a company or business department fails to commence business within six months after obtaining its business license, or voluntarily ceases business for six consecutive months after opening, its license shall be revoked, unless otherwise approved by the CSRC.

**Article 23**  
Within 30 days of obtaining its business license, a company or business department shall file with the CSRC and the local CSRC office where it is located; for cross-region business departments, the company shall also file with the CSRC local office where the head office is located within 30 days of the business department obtaining its license.

### Section 2  Changes

**Article 24**  
Any of the following changes by a futures brokerage company shall be subject to CSRC approval:  
(1) Amendments to the articles of association;  
(2) Change of legal representative;  
(3) Change of shareholders or equity structure;  
(4) Increase or decrease of registered capital;  
(5) Change of domicile or business premises of the company or any business department;  
(6) Revocation of a business department;  
(7) Merger or division;  
(8) Other matters prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 25**  
Changes to the chair, vice chair, general manager, or deputy general manager shall be submitted to the CSRC for review of appointees’ qualifications.

**Article 26**  
Where a company changes its name, it shall apply to the SAIC for change of registration and, within 15 days from obtaining the new business license, apply to the CSRC to change its Futures Brokerage Business License; failure to do so renders the license void.

**Article 27**  
For change approval, a company shall submit a change application, the shareholders’ or board resolution on the change, and other materials required by the CSRC.

**Article 28**  
Change applications shall be preliminarily reviewed by the CSRC local office where the company is located.  
Revocation of a business department shall be preliminarily reviewed by the CSRC local office where the business department is located.  
Changes of domicile or business premises shall be preliminarily reviewed by the CSRC local office of the new location.

**Article 29**  
Where a local office decides to approve a change, it shall issue a preliminary opinion and submit to the CSRC for approval; where it decides not to approve, it shall provide a written explanation.  
Within one month after receipt of compliant change materials, the CSRC shall notify the applicant in writing whether approval is granted; if not, reasons shall be stated.

**Article 30**  
For capital increase, in addition to the materials in Article 27, the company shall submit:  
(1) Capital increase plan;  
(2) List of new shareholders, copies of business licenses, their contribution methods and amounts, and their prior-year audited financial statements;  
(3) Capital verification certificate;  
(4) Post-increase equity proportions.  
New shareholders must meet the shareholder conditions in Article 9.

**Article 31**  
For mergers, in addition to Article 27 materials, the company shall submit:  
(1) Merger agreement;  
(2) Merger feasibility report;  
(3) Balance sheets and property inventories of all parties.

**Article 32**  
A merger agreement shall include:  
(1) Names, domiciles, and legal representatives of all parties;  
(2) Merger method and conditions;  
(3) Equity proportions of parties in a newly established company (for new establishment mergers);  
(4) Assumption and settlement of debts and claims;  
(5) Asset transfer method or asset changes;  
(6) Succession or changes concerning business departments;  
(7) Liability for breach;  
(8) Time and place of execution.

**Article 33**  
A company shall apply for change of shareholders or equity structure under any of the following:  
(1) Equity transfer by agreement among shareholders;  
(2) Equity transfer by a shareholder to another transferee;  
(3) Agreed adjustment of registered capital resulting in equity structure change;  
(4) Equity pledge where the pledgee acquires equity pursuant to law or contract;  
(5) A shareholder’s dissolution, termination, or revocation where the transferee lawfully acquires the equity;  
(6) Other causes of equity change.

**Article 34**  
For equity change, in addition to Article 27 materials, the company shall submit:  
(1) Equity transfer agreement;  
(2) Amended articles of association;  
(3) Post-change list of board members.

**Article 35**  
An equity transfer agreement shall include:  
(1) Names, domiciles, and legal representatives of transferor and transferee;  
(2) Proportion and conditions of the equity transferred;  
(3) Term and method of transfer;  
(4) Rights and obligations of both parties;  
(5) Liability for breach;  
(6) Time and place of execution.  
The transferee must meet the shareholder conditions in Article 9.

**Article 36**  
Within 30 days of approval, the company shall, with the changed license for the company or business department, complete change registration with the SAIC and, within 30 days thereafter, file with the CSRC; failure to make the changes renders the license void.

**Article 37**  
Where a company or business department changes domicile or business premises, or where a company changes its name or revokes a business department, it shall publish an announcement within 10 days of approval in major local newspapers or CSRC-recognized industry media; if premises change, a notice shall also be posted at the original site.

### Section 3  Dissolution and Liquidation

**Article 38**  
A futures brokerage company shall be terminated upon any of the following:  
(1) Expiry of business term or occurrence of a dissolution event specified in the articles;  
(2) Resolution of the shareholders’ meeting;  
(3) Dissolution due to merger or division;  
(4) Closure ordered by law;  
(5) Bankruptcy declared by law.  
For dissolution under items (1)–(3), the company shall apply to the CSRC local office and attach reasons and a plan for handling clients’ positions and margins; dissolution shall be effective upon approval.

**Article 39**  
Upon dissolution, a liquidation group shall be formed to conduct liquidation pursuant to law. The CSRC local office shall supervise the handling of clients’ positions and margins.

**Article 40**  
During liquidation, the group shall formulate a plan, clean up property, prepare balance sheets and property inventories, handle claims and debts, collect capital contributions subscribed but unpaid, settle taxes, and dispose of remaining property.  
Clients’ positions shall be handled and clients’ margins returned on a priority basis.

**Article 41**  
Where a business department is revoked, the company shall lawfully handle that department’s clients’ positions and shall first return clients’ margins.  
Before completing the handling of clients’ positions and margins, the department’s assets shall not be transferred.

**Article 42**  
Within 10 days of completing liquidation, the company shall return its Futures Brokerage Business License; overdue returns shall be cancelled by the CSRC.  
The company shall proceed with deregistration at the SAIC.

## Chapter III  Basic Rules of Futures Brokerage Business

### Section 1  General Provisions

**Article 43**  
A futures brokerage company shall comply with applicable laws, regulations, rules, and CSRC provisions; manipulation of futures prices, insider trading, and defrauding clients are prohibited.

**Article 44**  
A futures brokerage company shall adhere to the principles of openness, fairness, impartiality, and good faith, safeguard clients’ lawful rights and interests, and ensure sound market operation.

**Article 45**  
A futures brokerage company shall execute client orders with appropriate skill, due care, and diligence to protect client interests.

**Article 46**  
A futures brokerage company shall avoid any conflict of interest with clients and ensure fair treatment of all clients.

**Article 47**  
A futures brokerage company shall continuously maintain qualified personnel and technological facilities, implement internal rules, and ensure sound and lawful operations.

**Article 48**  
A futures brokerage company shall explain to clients the risks of futures trading and keep at its business premises the relevant laws and regulations, exchange trading rules, and its brokerage business rules and implementing details for client inspection.

### Section 2  Futures Brokerage Agreement

**Article 49**  
A futures brokerage agreement is a written agreement between the client and the company setting forth rights and obligations.  
Clients include entities and individuals. An entity shall not sign an agreement in the name of an individual, nor an individual in the name of an entity.  
“Entities” include companies, enterprises, and other economic organizations.

**Article 50**  
A futures brokerage agreement shall include:  
(1) Client’s name, ID number, domicile, phone number (for entities: name, legal representative or principal, domicile, phone number);  
(2) Client’s contact address and method, means of giving and confirming orders;  
(3) Account management and clearing methods;  
(4) Handling of the client’s account when the company is unable to engage in brokerage business;  
(5) Margin standards and payment methods;  
(6) Conditions for margin calls and the timing and means of notice;  
(7) Conditions for forced liquidation and related arrangements;  
(8) Brokerage commissions and other related fees;  
(9) Methods of delivery and confirmation of trade confirmations and monthly statements;  
(10) Scope of advisory, training, and other services;  
(11) Exemption clauses;  
(12) Dispute resolution;  
(13) Amendment and termination of the contract;  
(14) Other matters prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 51**  
A company’s standard brokerage agreement must conform to the CSRC’s model contract and be submitted to the CSRC local office for review.

**Article 52**  
None of the following may become a client of a futures brokerage company:  
(1) Natural persons without or with limited civil capacity;  
(2) Staff of futures regulatory authorities or futures exchanges;  
(3) Employees of the company and their spouses or lineal relatives;  
(4) Persons barred from entering the futures market;  
(5) Financial institutions, public institutions, and state organs;  
(6) State-owned enterprises, or enterprises in which state-owned assets hold a controlling or leading position, that fail to provide approval documents signed by the legal representative;  
(7) Entity accounts opened by entrustment without providing the entrustment authorization documents;  
(8) Other situations as prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 53**  
Before signing, the company shall inquire into and, where necessary, require proof of the client’s eligibility, source of funds, and other credit-related matters; the client shall truthfully disclose and provide information.  
Where a client intentionally conceals facts or, due to negligence, fails to fulfill the disclosure obligation, the company shall refuse to open an account or may terminate the contract; losses shall be borne by the client, and handling fees will not be refunded.

**Article 54**  
The client shall specify in the contract the authorized agent(s) to place orders, transfer funds, etc., agree on contact methods, and deposit specimen signatures.  
Changes shall be notified in writing to the company and take effect upon confirmation.

**Article 55**  
Under any of the following, the company may terminate the agreement, settle the client’s account, and close it:  
(1) The client dies, loses civil capacity, or terminates (for entities);  
(2) The client is declared by a people’s court to enter bankruptcy proceedings;  
(3) Other circumstances prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 56**  
A client may terminate the entrustment by closing the account in accordance with required procedures.

**Article 57**  
When closing an account, the company shall settle all claims and debts with the client.

### Section 3  Basic Rules of Brokerage Operations

**Article 58**  
A company must accept client orders and conduct futures trading at lawfully established business premises.

**Article 59**  
Publicity regarding a company’s credit standing must be truthful; inducing clients through false publicity is prohibited.  
A company shall not cite the CSRC’s business license as a guarantee of its operational capability or financial soundness.

**Article 60**  
Advertising must comply with CSRC provisions; promotional materials must be signed by the legal representative or an authorized person and filed with the CSRC local office.

**Article 61**  
Operational activities shall be conducted by staff who hold practice qualifications pursuant to the *Measures for the Administration of Qualifications for Futures Practitioners*.

**Article 62**  
Before opening an account, the company shall present the *Futures Trading Risk Disclosure Statement*; after the client signs to acknowledge understanding, the written brokerage agreement may be signed.  
The format and content of the Risk Disclosure Statement are unified by the CSRC.  
A company shall not open an account for a client without a written agreement.

**Article 63**  
Before signing, the company shall explain the meaning of the contract terms; after the client clearly understands the parties’ rights and obligations, the client shall sign to confirm.

**Article 64**  
The company shall assign client trading codes in accordance with exchange coding rules and file them with the exchange.  
Cancellation of a client code shall be filed with the exchange.

**Article 65**  
Clients must open accounts under their true identity.  
Individual clients must present PRC identity documents; entity clients must provide lawful certificates evidencing PRC legal-person or other economic-organization status.  
The company shall verify the authenticity of client identity.

**Article 66**  
A company shall not, under any pretext, open an account in its own name at itself or another brokerage to engage in futures trading.

**Article 67**  
The company shall establish client account-opening files and keep them confidential except as required by law for inspection.

**Article 68**  
The company shall keep a “error-order log” to record the handling of erroneous orders.

**Article 69**  
The company shall establish and improve a client complaint handling system and archive complaints and their dispositions.

**Article 70**  
The company shall collect margins from clients and keep detailed margin sub-ledgers.

**Article 71**  
The company shall open a client margin account at the clearing bank designated by the exchange, to hold client margins.  
Such accounts shall be filed with the CSRC local office and be subject to lawful inspection.

**Article 72**  
A company may set the client trading margin ratio, which shall be at least 3 percentage points higher than the margin ratio the exchange charges the company.  
The company may adjust margin ratios reasonably according to risk and shall notify clients as agreed in the brokerage contract.

**Article 73**  
A company may agree with the client on trading risk control conditions.  
Where the client’s margin is insufficient and risk reaches the agreed condition, the company shall notify the client to add margin as agreed; if the client fails to do so in time, the company may forcibly close some or all positions until the balance meets the agreed risk control condition.  
Where, after daily settlement, the client’s margin falls below the exchange’s required margin level, the company shall notify the client to add margin as agreed; if the client fails, the company shall forcibly close some or all positions until the margin can maintain remaining positions.  
If close-out instructions cannot be executed due to market reasons after being submitted to the exchange, the company bears no liability.  
Fees and losses arising from forced liquidation shall be borne by the client.

**Article 74**  
Transfers of client margin must meet one of the following:  
(1) Payment of margin balance per the client’s instructions;  
(2) Deposit of margin or settlement differences to the exchange for the client;  
(3) Brokerage commissions, taxes, and other fees payable by the client to the company;  
(4) Other situations approved by the CSRC.

**Article 75**  
Client orders shall specify product, contract month, quantity, buy or sell, open or close, and price.  
A company shall not accept discretionary mandates, nor unclear or incomplete orders.  
“Discretionary mandate” means the company or its staff decides any of the order elements listed in paragraph 1 on behalf of the client.

**Article 76**  
Clients may place orders in writing, by telephone, or by other CSRC-approved means.  
For telephone orders, the company must record the call; for telex, fax, computer, or other means, the company shall retain records sufficient to prove order contents.

**Article 77**  
For written orders, a written order ticket shall be filled out, stating:  
(1) Client code;  
(2) Method of order placement;  
(3) Date and time;  
(4) Order contents;  
(5) Signatures of the salesperson and order placer;  
(6) Other necessary items.

**Article 78**  
Companies shall execute client orders following the principle of time priority.

**Article 79**  
After market close, the company shall deliver trade confirmations to clients at the time and in the manner agreed.  
Confirmations shall state:  
(1) Account number and name;  
(2) Trade date;  
(3) Product and contract month;  
(4) Quantity and price;  
(5) Buy or sell;  
(6) Open or close;  
(7) Settlement price of the day;  
(8) Margin used and margin balance;  
(9) Commissions and other fees;  
(10) Taxes;  
(11) Other necessary items.

**Article 80**  
If the client disputes a confirmation, a written objection shall be submitted before the next trading day opens; if no objection, the client shall sign the confirmation or confirm as agreed. Failure to confirm or object shall be deemed confirmation.  
For objections, the company shall verify against original order records and trade records.

**Article 81**  
If results do not conform to the client’s order, or forced liquidation does not meet legal or agreed conditions due to the company’s fault, the company shall, before the next close, re-execute the order or restore the liquidated position and compensate direct losses.  
If the client fails to object within the period in Article 80, no further objection may be raised.

**Article 82**  
The company shall send monthly statements for the preceding month at the agreed time and in the agreed manner, stating:  
(1) Account number and name;  
(2) Open positions by product at month-end;  
(3) Margin deposits/withdrawals and balance for the month;  
(4) Trading profits and losses;  
(5) Commissions, taxes, and other fees;  
(6) Physical deliveries during the month;  
(7) Other necessary items.

**Article 83**  
Where the company sends forced-liquidation notices, margin call notices, trade confirmations, and monthly statements as agreed, the notice obligations under Articles 73, 79, and 82 are deemed fulfilled.

**Article 84**  
Account-opening data, order records, confirmations, monthly statements, and other business records shall be retained for at least two years; if disputes arise, they shall be kept until the dispute is resolved.

**Article 85**  
Commissions charged comprise fees collected on behalf of the exchange and the company’s own commissions.  
The company’s commission standard shall not be lower than three times the commission standard specified in the futures contract, unless otherwise provided by the CSRC.  
Disguised reduction of commission standards is prohibited.

## Chapter IV  Supervision and Administration

### Section 1  Financial Administration

**Article 86**  
In addition to statutory reserves under the *Company Law*, a company shall also appropriate 5% of profits to a surplus reserve.

**Article 87**  
Companies shall appropriate risk reserves at ratios stipulated by the state and keep them in separate accounts; except as permitted by state rules, reserves shall not be used for other purposes.

**Article 88**  
Where net assets fall below 60% of registered capital, the company shall, apart from handling existing trades, cease accepting new opening orders, explain the causes to the CSRC local office within three business days, and propose a remediation plan.

**Article 89**  
Where adjusted net assets to client margin fall below 8%, the company shall report to the CSRC local office within three business days.  
If the ratio falls below 4%, the company shall, apart from handling existing trades, cease accepting new opening orders, explain the situation within three business days, and propose a remediation plan.  
Adjusted net assets are calculated as:  
Adjusted Net Assets = Owners’ Equity + Risk Reserves − Receivables aged over one year − Deferred expenses − Net fixed assets − Intangible assets − Establishment expenses and long-term deferred expenses.

**Article 90**  
Financial/accounting and settlement functions must be separated into different departments, and a risk assessment system shall be established and improved per CSRC requirements.

**Article 91**  
The CSRC may adjust the financial ratios in Articles 86, 88, and 89 generally or individually based on company operations and market conditions.

### Section 2  License Administration

**Article 92**  
*Futures Brokerage Business Licenses* and *Business Department Operating Licenses* are uniformly designed and printed by the CSRC.  
Forgery, alteration, leasing, lending, transfer, or sale of licenses is prohibited.

**Article 93**  
If a license (original or copy) is lost or seriously damaged, the company shall, within 30 days of discovery, announce its invalidation in CSRC-recognized newspapers and apply to the CSRC for reissuance with the announcement attached.

**Article 94**  
The contents on the business license of the company or business department must be consistent with those on the relevant CSRC license.

### Section 3  Routine Supervision and Inspection

**Article 95**  
Companies shall establish and strictly implement risk management and internal control systems.

**Article 96**  
Companies shall appoint internal auditors to periodically or randomly audit finance and business operations and compliance with laws, regulations, policies, articles, and business rules, and prepare audit reports for record.

**Article 97**  
The CSRC and its local offices may conduct routine inspections at any time.  
The main items include:  
(1) Whether approval procedures for establishment or changes are complete;  
(2) Consistency between application materials and actual conditions;  
(3) Authenticity and adequacy of capital or working capital;  
(4) Whether business is within the approved scope;  
(5) Whether there are illegal operations;  
(6) Whether business operations are sound;  
(7) Whether premises and trading facilities meet requirements;  
(8) Whether required reports are submitted;  
(9) Other matters deemed necessary.

**Article 98**  
Local offices shall conduct ad hoc inspections on:  
(1) Implementation of the margin system by companies and business departments;  
(2) Implementation of risk management and internal control systems;  
(3) Other ad hoc items deemed necessary.  
Companies lacking such systems shall be ordered to rectify and be subject to focused inspection.

**Article 99**  
Companies and business departments shall submit monthly operating and financial reports to their local CSRC office in the required format.

**Article 100**  
Within 10 days of occurrence, companies shall report to the local CSRC office any of:  
(1) Replacement of directors or supervisors;  
(2) Equity investments of RMB 1 million or more;  
(3) Pending litigation of RMB 500,000 or more;  
(4) Opening or resumption of business;  
(5) Other matters prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 101**  
A company may apply to the CSRC to suspend operations of the company or a business department, with a plan for handling clients’ positions and margins; suspension shall take effect upon CSRC approval.  
Upon approval, licenses shall be returned to the CSRC.

### Section 4  Annual Inspection

**Article 102**  
The CSRC shall conduct annual inspections (“annual review”) of companies to confirm business qualifications.  
The format of the annual report, inspection label, and stamp shall be unified by the CSRC.

**Article 103**  
New companies shall participate in the following year’s inspection.  
Companies penalized with suspension shall participate after rectification and acceptance by the CSRC.  
Suspended companies or business departments shall participate after resumption.

**Article 104**  
Annual inspection covers:  
(1) Changes to the business license;  
(2) Financial conditions;  
(3) Compliance with laws, regulations, rules, and policies;  
(4) Formulation and implementation of internal rules;  
(5) Handling of client complaints;  
(6) Other matters prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 105**  
Within 15 days of receiving notice, companies shall submit:  
(1) Annual inspection report;  
(2) Audited financial statements by a qualified accounting firm;  
(3) Copy of the business license (copy);  
(4) Other materials required by the CSRC.

**Article 106**  
Local offices conduct preliminary reviews and submit materials and opinions to the CSRC for re-examination.  
If qualified, the CSRC will affix the inspection label or stamp to the license.

**Article 107**  
For companies not conforming to applicable requirements, the CSRC shall order suspension for rectification or revoke the license.

### Section 5  Special Procedures for Margin-Refund Crises

**Article 108**  
Where a company has experienced or may experience a client margin refund crisis that seriously affects client interests, the CSRC may decide to implement special handling.

**Article 109**  
A special handling decision shall state:  
(1) The company name;  
(2) Reasons;  
(3) Duration.

**Article 110**  
Upon entry into special handling, the company’s shareholders shall organize a special handling working group. If necessary, the CSRC local office may send representatives or engage intermediaries to participate.  
The working group’s work is subject to CSRC supervision.

**Article 111**  
From the commencement of special handling, operating and management authority shall be exercised by the working group.  
Debts and claims arising during the period shall continue to be borne by the company.

**Article 112**  
The working group may take measures including:  
(1) Verifying clients’ positions, margin balances, and causes of the crisis;  
(2) Disposing of company assets and recovering due claims;  
(3) Recovering misappropriated client margins;  
(4) Deciding plans for handling clients’ positions and margins;  
(5) Formulating and implementing a rectification plan;  
(6) Other necessary measures.

**Article 113**  
If liquidation is deemed necessary during special handling, the working group may apply to the CSRC; liquidation may proceed upon approval.

**Article 114**  
Upon expiry, the CSRC may extend special handling, but the total duration shall not exceed one year.

**Article 115**  
Special handling shall terminate upon any of:  
(1) Expiry of the specified or extended period;  
(2) Restoration of normal operating capability before expiry;  
(3) Merger or bankruptcy of the company before expiry.

## Chapter V  Penalties

**Article 116**  
Where a company commits any of the following, penalties shall be imposed pursuant to Article 59 of the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*:  
(1) Failure to perform reporting or filing obligations;  
(2) Opening accounts for ineligible entities or individuals in violation of Article 52;  
(3) Failure to examine client eligibility and credit per Article 53;  
(4) Failure to meet CSRC financial requirements after a rectification deadline;  
(5) Providing guarantees in violation of regulations or becoming an investor bearing unlimited liability;  
(6) False statements or major omissions in application materials;  
(7) Establishing business departments via joint venture or cooperation;  
(8) Contracting out or leasing business departments;  
(9) Opening additional premises for a business department;  
(10) Disguised reduction of commission or margin standards;  
(11) Failure to establish complaint files and error-order records;  
(12) Speculating in stocks in violation of regulations.

**Article 117**  
Where a company commits any of the following, penalties shall be imposed pursuant to Article 60 of the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*:  
(1) Improper handling of a business department’s clients’ positions and margins, or unauthorized transfer of its assets;  
(2) Accepting client orders outside lawfully established business premises;  
(3) Accepting orders from clients who have not completed account-opening procedures;  
(4) Inducing clients to trade through false publicity;  
(5) Misrepresenting the company’s credit standing, or citing the CSRC’s business license as a guarantee of capability or financial soundness;  
(6) Evading debts by withdrawing capital or concealing assets.

**Article 118**  
Other violations of these Measures shall be punished pursuant to the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*.

**Article 119**  
Those who violate these Measures and cause damage to others shall bear civil liability according to law.

## Chapter VI  Supplementary Provisions

**Article 120**  
Futures brokerage companies lawfully established before the implementation of these Measures may continue to exist; those not fully meeting the conditions herein shall meet them within the timeframe prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 121**  
Where provisions issued before the implementation of these Measures are inconsistent with these Measures, these Measures shall prevail.

**Article 122**  
These Measures shall be interpreted by the CSRC.

**Article 123**  
These Measures shall come into force on September 1, 1999.
