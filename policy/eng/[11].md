# Decree of the State Council of the People’s Republic of China

**No. 489**

The *Regulation on the Administration of Futures Trading* was adopted at the 168th Executive Meeting of the State Council on 7 February 2007 and is hereby promulgated. It shall enter into force on 15 April 2007.

**Premier: Wen Jiabao**
6 March 2007

------

# Regulation on the Administration of Futures Trading

## Chapter I General Provisions

**Article 1** This Regulation is formulated in order to regulate futures trading activities, strengthen the supervision and administration of futures trading, maintain market order, prevent risks, protect the lawful rights and interests of all parties to futures trading as well as the public interest, and promote the active and steady development of the futures market.

**Article 2** Any entity or individual engaging in futures trading—including trading in commodity and financial futures contracts, options contracts, and related activities—shall abide by this Regulation.

**Article 3** Participants in futures trading shall follow the principles of openness, fairness, impartiality, and good faith. Fraud, insider trading, manipulation of futures prices, and other illegal acts are prohibited.

**Article 4** Futures trading shall be conducted on futures exchanges lawfully established, or on other trading venues approved by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Futures trading conducted outside trading venues approved by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council is prohibited, as is disguised futures trading.

**Article 5** The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall exercise centralized and unified supervision and administration over the futures market.

Dispatched offices of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall, in accordance with the provisions of this Regulation and the authorization of the said authority, perform supervisory and administrative duties.

## Chapter II Futures Exchanges

**Article 6** The establishment of a futures exchange shall be subject to approval by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Without such approval, no entity or individual may establish a futures exchange or organize, in any form, futures trading and related activities.

**Article 7** A futures exchange shall be a non-profit institution and shall implement self-regulation in accordance with its articles of association. A futures exchange shall be liable for civil obligations to the extent of all its assets. The principal persons in charge of a futures exchange shall be appointed or removed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Administrative measures for futures exchanges shall be formulated by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 8** Members of a futures exchange shall be legal persons or other economic organizations registered within the territory of the People’s Republic of China.

A futures exchange may implement a tiered clearing membership system. Under such system, members shall consist of clearing members and non-clearing members.

The clearing qualifications of clearing members shall be approved by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, which shall make a decision to approve or disapprove within three months from accepting an application.

**Article 9** Persons who fall under Article 147 of the *Company Law of the People’s Republic of China* or any of the following circumstances shall not serve as principal persons in charge of a futures exchange or as finance and accounting personnel:

1. Principals of a futures exchange, a securities exchange, or a securities registration and clearing institution, or directors, supervisors, or senior management personnel of a futures company or a securities company, or other persons as specified by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, who were removed from their posts due to violations of laws or disciplines, within five years from the date of removal;
2. Lawyers, certified public accountants, or professionals of investment advisory, financial advisory, credit rating, asset appraisal, or verification institutions whose qualifications were revoked due to violations of laws or disciplines, within five years from the date of revocation.

**Article 10** A futures exchange shall, in accordance with this Regulation and the provisions of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, establish and improve its internal rules and systems, strengthen risk control over trading activities, and supervise and administer its members and staff. A futures exchange shall perform the following duties:

1. Provide trading venues, facilities, and services;
2. Design contracts and arrange their listing;
3. Organize and supervise trading, clearing, and delivery;
4. Ensure the performance of contracts;
5. Supervise and manage members in accordance with its articles of association and trading rules;
6. Other duties as prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

A futures exchange shall not, directly or indirectly, participate in futures trading. Without review by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council and approval by the State Council, a futures exchange shall not engage in businesses unrelated to its duties, such as trust investment, stock investment, or investment in non-self-use real estate.

**Article 11** A futures exchange shall, in accordance with State provisions, establish and improve the following risk management systems:

1. Margin system;
2. Mark-to-market settlement on a daily, no-debt basis;
3. Price limit (limit up/limit down) system;
4. Position limits and large-trader position reporting system;
5. Risk reserve system;
6. Other risk management systems as prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

A futures exchange implementing a tiered clearing membership system shall also establish and improve a clearing guarantee fund system.

**Article 12** When abnormal circumstances arise in the futures market, a futures exchange may, within the authority and procedures stipulated in its articles of association, decide to take the following emergency measures and shall immediately report to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council:

1. Raise margin requirements;
2. Adjust limits on price movements;
3. Restrict the maximum positions of members or clients;
4. Temporarily suspend trading;
5. Take other emergency measures.

“Abnormal circumstances” as used in the preceding paragraph refer to manipulation of futures prices during trading, force majeure emergencies, and other circumstances as prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Once abnormal circumstances cease, the futures exchange shall promptly lift the emergency measures.

**Article 13** A futures exchange shall obtain approval from the futures regulatory authority of the State Council for the following matters:

1. Formulating or amending its articles of association or trading rules;
2. Listing, suspending, delisting, or relisting a product;
3. Listing, amending, or terminating a contract;
4. Changing its domicile or business premises;
5. Merger, division, or dissolution;
6. Other matters as prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

When approving the listing of a new futures product, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall solicit the opinions of relevant State Council departments.

**Article 14** The income of a futures exchange shall be administered and used in accordance with State provisions, but shall be used first to ensure the operation and improvement of its trading venues and facilities.

## Chapter III Futures Companies

**Article 15** A futures company is a financial institution that engages in futures business established in accordance with the *Company Law of the People’s Republic of China* and this Regulation. The establishment of a futures company shall be subject to the approval of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council and registration with the company registration authority.

Without such approval, no entity or individual may establish, or in disguised form establish, a futures company or engage in futures business.

**Article 16** An applicant for establishing a futures company shall comply with the *Company Law of the People’s Republic of China* and meet the following conditions:

1. The minimum registered capital shall be RMB 30 million;
2. Directors, supervisors, and senior management personnel shall possess the qualifications for office, and practitioners shall hold futures practice qualifications;
3. The company shall have articles of association that comply with laws and administrative regulations;
4. Major shareholders and actual controllers shall have continuous profitability, good credit, and no major violations of laws or regulations in the last three years;
5. The company shall have qualified business premises and facilities;
6. The company shall have sound risk management and internal control systems;
7. Other conditions as prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Based on prudential supervision principles and the risk profile of various businesses, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council may raise the minimum registered capital requirement. Registered capital shall be paid-in capital. Shareholders shall contribute in monetary funds or in non-monetary assets necessary for the operation of the futures company, and monetary contributions shall not be less than 85%.

Within six months from accepting an application for establishment, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall, based on prudential supervision principles, conduct a review and make a decision to approve or disapprove.

Without approval by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, no entity or individual may entrust others or accept entrustment to hold or manage the equity of a futures company.

**Article 17** Futures company business is subject to licensing. Licenses for commodity futures and financial futures business categories shall be issued by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council. In addition to applying to conduct domestic futures brokerage, a futures company may apply to conduct overseas futures brokerage, futures investment advisory, and other futures businesses prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

A futures company shall not engage in activities unrelated to futures business unless otherwise provided by laws, administrative regulations, or the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

A futures company shall not engage in, or in disguised form engage in, proprietary futures trading.

A futures company shall not provide financing to its shareholders, actual controllers, or other affiliates, nor provide external guarantees.

**Article 18** In conducting brokerage business, a futures company shall accept client mandates, trade in its own name on behalf of clients, and the results of the trades shall be borne by the clients.

**Article 19** A futures company shall obtain approval from the futures regulatory authority of the State Council for the following matters:

1. Merger, division, suspension of business, dissolution, or bankruptcy;
2. Change of company form;
3. Change of business scope;
4. Change of registered capital;
5. Change of 5% or more of equity;
6. Establishment, acquisition, equity participation in, or termination of overseas futures-related operating institutions;
7. Other matters as prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

For the matters listed in items (4) and (7) of the preceding paragraph, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall decide whether to approve within 20 days from accepting the application; for the other matters listed, a decision shall be made within two months from acceptance.

**Article 20** A futures company shall obtain approval from the dispatched office of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council for the following matters:

1. Change of legal representative;
2. Change of domicile or business premises;
3. Establishment or termination of domestic branches;
4. Change of a domestic branch’s business premises, person in charge, or business scope;
5. Other matters as prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

For the matters listed in items (1), (2), (4), and (5), the dispatched office shall decide whether to approve within 20 days from accepting the application; for the matter in item (3), a decision shall be made within two months.

**Article 21** Where a futures company or its branch falls under Article 70 of the *Administrative Licensing Law of the People’s Republic of China* or any of the following circumstances, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall cancel its futures business license in accordance with law:

1. Its business license has been lawfully canceled by the company registration authority;
2. Without justifiable reason, it has failed to commence business for more than three months after establishment, or has ceased business for more than three consecutive months without justifiable reason after opening;
3. It voluntarily applies for cancellation;
4. Other circumstances as prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

Before cancellation of a futures business license, a futures company shall settle relevant futures business and lawfully return client margins and other assets. Before cancellation of an operating license, a branch shall cease operations and properly handle client assets.

**Article 22** A futures company shall establish, improve, and strictly implement business management rules and risk management systems, comply with information disclosure requirements, ensure the safety of client margin custody, and, in accordance with the rules of the futures exchange, report large-trader lists and trading conditions to the exchange.

**Article 23** Other futures operating institutions engaging in futures investment advisory or intermediary introduction services for futures companies shall obtain business qualifications approved by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council. Specific administrative measures shall be formulated by that authority.

## Chapter IV Basic Rules of Futures Trading

**Article 24** Persons conducting futures trading on a futures exchange shall be members of the exchange.

**Article 25** When accepting a client mandate to conduct futures trading, a futures company shall first present to the client a risk disclosure statement; after the client signs to confirm receipt, the company shall enter into a written contract with the client. A futures company shall not, without a client’s mandate or contrary to a client’s mandate, conduct futures trading on its own initiative.

A futures company shall not provide profit guarantees to clients; it shall not, in brokerage business, agree with clients to share profits or jointly bear risks.

**Article 26** The following entities and individuals shall not engage in futures trading, and a futures company shall not accept their mandates to trade:

1. State organs and public institutions;
2. Staff of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, futures exchanges, the futures margin safety depository monitoring institution, and the futures industry association;
3. Persons banned from entering the securities or futures markets;
4. Entities and individuals unable to provide account-opening documentation;
5. Other entities and individuals prohibited by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 27** Clients may place trading instructions with a futures company in writing, by telephone, via the Internet, or by other means prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council. Client instructions shall be clear and complete.

A futures company shall not conceal material facts or use improper means to induce clients to issue trading instructions.

**Article 28** A futures exchange shall promptly publish transaction volume, transaction price, open interest, highest and lowest prices, opening and closing prices, and other real-time market data for listed contracts, and shall ensure the truthfulness and accuracy of such data. A futures exchange shall not publish price forecasts.

Without the permission of a futures exchange, no entity or individual may disseminate real-time futures market data.

**Article 29** Futures trading shall strictly implement the margin system. Margins collected by a futures exchange from members and by a futures company from clients shall not be lower than the standards set by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council and the futures exchange, and shall be segregated from proprietary funds and kept in dedicated accounts.

Margins collected by a futures exchange from members belong to the members and shall not be misappropriated for any purpose other than members’ trading settlement.

Margins collected by a futures company from clients belong to the clients and shall not be misappropriated, except in the following transferable circumstances:

1. Payment of available funds at the client’s request;
2. For the client’s margin deposits and payment of commissions and taxes;
3. Other circumstances as prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 30** A futures company shall open a dedicated account and set a trading code for each client, and shall not mix codes for trading.

**Article 31** Where a futures company conducts brokerage business alongside other futures businesses, it shall strictly implement business and fund separation, and shall not commingle operations.

**Article 32** Members of futures exchanges and clients may use standard warehouse receipts, government bonds, and other highly liquid, stable-value securities to offset margins. The categories of such securities, methods for valuing them, and offset ratios shall be prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 33** Banking financial institutions engaging in margin depository or futures clearing business shall obtain approval from the futures regulatory authority of the State Council after consent by the banking regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 34** Futures exchanges, futures companies, and non-futures-company clearing members shall, in accordance with the provisions of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council and the finance authorities, accrue, administer, and use risk reserves, and shall not misappropriate them.

**Article 35** Items and standards of fees for futures trading and their administrative measures shall be formulated and published by the relevant competent departments of the State Council.

**Article 36** Futures trading shall adopt open centralized trading or other methods approved by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 37** Clearing of futures trades shall be uniformly organized by the futures exchange.

A futures exchange shall implement daily mark-to-market settlement on a no-debt basis and shall promptly notify members of settlement results on the same day.

A futures company shall, based on the settlement results of the futures exchange, settle with clients and promptly notify clients of the results in the manner agreed with them. Clients shall promptly check and properly manage their positions.

**Article 38** When a member’s margin is insufficient, it shall promptly add margin or liquidate positions on its own. If a member fails to add margin or liquidate positions within the time specified by the exchange, the exchange shall forcibly liquidate the member’s contracts, and the relevant expenses and losses shall be borne by the member.

When a client’s margin is insufficient, the client shall promptly add margin or liquidate positions. If the client fails to do so within the time specified by the futures company, the futures company shall forcibly liquidate the client’s contracts, and the relevant expenses and losses shall be borne by the client.

**Article 39** Delivery in futures trading shall be uniformly organized by the futures exchange.

Delivery warehouses shall be designated by the futures exchange. A futures exchange shall not restrict the total volume of physical deliveries and shall conclude agreements with delivery warehouses specifying mutual rights and obligations. Delivery warehouses shall not:

1. Issue false warehouse receipts;
2. Restrict the storage in or out of deliverable commodities in violation of exchange business rules;
3. Disclose trade secrets related to futures trading;
4. Participate in futures trading in violation of State provisions;
5. Engage in other conduct as prescribed by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 40** Where a member defaults in futures trading, the futures exchange shall first use the member’s margins to bear default liabilities; where margins are insufficient, the exchange shall use its risk reserves and proprietary funds to assume liabilities on behalf of the member and thereby obtain corresponding rights of recourse.

Where a client defaults in futures trading, the futures company shall first use the client’s margins to bear default liabilities; where margins are insufficient, the futures company shall use its risk reserves and proprietary funds to assume liabilities on behalf of the client and thereby obtain corresponding rights of recourse.

**Article 41** A futures exchange implementing a tiered clearing membership system shall collect clearing guarantee funds from clearing members. The exchange shall clear only with clearing members, collect and call margins, assume default liabilities with guarantee funds, risk reserves, and proprietary funds, and take related measures. For non-clearing members, clearing, margin collection and calls, assumption of default liabilities, and related measures shall be performed by the clearing members.

**Article 42** Futures exchanges, futures companies, and non-futures-company clearing members shall ensure the integrity and security of records related to trading, clearing, and delivery.

**Article 43** No entity or individual may fabricate or disseminate false information about futures trading, or collude maliciously, conduct wash trades, or otherwise manipulate futures prices.

**Article 44** No entity or individual may illegally use credit funds or fiscal funds for futures trading.

The qualifications of banking financial institutions to engage in financing or guaranteeing for futures trading shall be approved by the banking regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 45** State-owned enterprises and state-controlled enterprises engaging in domestic or overseas futures trading shall follow the principle of hedging and strictly comply with provisions of the State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration authority of the State Council and other relevant departments on the entry of state-owned assets into the futures market.

**Article 46** The competent commerce department of the State Council shall approve the varieties for overseas commodity futures trading by domestic entities or individuals.

Foreign exchange purchases and settlements, and foreign exchange receipts and payments under overseas futures shall comply with State foreign exchange administration provisions.

Measures for domestic entities or individuals engaging in overseas futures trading shall be formulated by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council jointly with the competent commerce department of the State Council, the State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration authority, the banking regulatory authority, the foreign exchange authority, and other relevant departments, and implemented after approval by the State Council.

## Chapter V Futures Industry Association

**Article 47** The Futures Industry Association is a self-regulatory organization of the futures industry and a social organization legal person.

Futures companies and other institutions specialized in futures business shall join the Futures Industry Association and pay membership fees.

**Article 48** The supreme authority of the Futures Industry Association shall be the general assembly of all members.

The charter of the Association shall be formulated by the general assembly and filed with the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

The Association shall have a council, whose members shall be elected in accordance with the charter.

**Article 49** The Futures Industry Association shall perform the following duties:

1. Educate and organize members to comply with futures laws, regulations, and policies;
2. Formulate self-regulatory rules for members, supervise and inspect member conduct, and impose disciplinary sanctions for violations of the charter and self-regulatory rules;
3. Be responsible for the accreditation, administration, and revocation of qualifications of futures practitioners;
4. Accept complaints related to futures business and mediate disputes among members or between members and clients;
5. Protect members’ lawful rights and interests in accordance with law and communicate members’ proposals and requests to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council;
6. Organize business training for practitioners and exchanges among members;
7. Organize research on the development and operation of the futures industry and related matters;
8. Other duties as stipulated in the Association’s charter.

The business activities of the Association shall be subject to the guidance and supervision of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

## Chapter VI Supervision and Administration

**Article 50** The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall implement supervision and administration over the futures market and perform the following duties according to law:

1. Formulate rules and regulations on supervision and administration of the futures market and exercise approval authority according to law;
2. Supervise and administer listing, trading, clearing, delivery, and other futures trading and related activities;
3. Supervise and administer the futures business activities of futures exchanges, futures companies and other futures operating institutions, non-futures-company clearing members, the margin safety depository monitoring institution, margin depository banks, delivery warehouses, and other market participants;
4. Formulate qualification standards and administrative measures for futures practitioners and supervise their implementation;
5. Supervise and inspect the disclosure of futures trading information;
6. Guide and supervise the activities of the Futures Industry Association;
7. Investigate and handle violations of futures market supervisory laws and administrative regulations;
8. Conduct international exchanges and cooperation related to futures market supervision and administration;
9. Other duties prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 51** In performing duties according to law, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council may take the following measures:

1. Conduct on-site inspections of futures exchanges, futures companies and other futures operating institutions, non-futures-company clearing members, the margin safety depository monitoring institution, and delivery warehouses;
2. Enter sites suspected of illegal conduct to investigate and collect evidence;
3. Question parties and relevant entities and individuals and require them to explain matters related to the investigation;
4. Examine and copy materials such as property rights registration related to the investigation;
5. Examine and copy futures trading records, financial and accounting materials, and other relevant documents and materials of parties and relevant entities and individuals; documents and materials that may be transferred, concealed, or destroyed may be sealed;
6. Inquire into margin accounts and bank accounts of entities related to the investigation;
7. When investigating major futures violations such as manipulation of futures prices or insider trading, with the approval of the principal person in charge of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, restrict a party’s futures trading; the restriction shall not exceed 15 trading days, and may be extended to 30 trading days in complicated cases;
8. Other measures prescribed by laws and administrative regulations.

**Article 52** Futures exchanges, futures companies and other futures operating institutions, and the margin safety depository monitoring institution shall submit financial and accounting reports, business data, and other relevant materials to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

For annual reports submitted by futures companies and other futures operating institutions, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall designate personnel to review and prepare review reports, which shall be signed by the reviewers. Where problems are identified during review, appropriate measures shall be taken in a timely manner.

When necessary, non-futures-company clearing members, delivery warehouses, as well as shareholders, actual controllers, or other affiliates of futures companies may be required to submit relevant materials.

**Article 53** When the futures regulatory authority of the State Council performs its duties according to law and conducts supervision, inspection, or investigation, the entities and individuals being inspected or investigated shall cooperate, truthfully provide documents and materials, and shall not refuse, obstruct, or conceal. Other relevant departments and entities shall provide support and cooperation.

**Article 54** The State shall, based on the needs of futures market development, establish a Futures Investor Protection Fund.

Specific measures for the raising, administration, and use of the Fund shall be formulated by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council jointly with the finance authorities of the State Council.

**Article 55** The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall establish and improve a monitoring system for the safe custody of margins and shall set up a margin safety depository monitoring institution.

Clients, futures exchanges, futures companies and other futures operating institutions, non-futures-company clearing members, and margin depository banks shall comply with provisions of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council on the monitoring of margin safety depository.

**Article 56** The margin safety depository monitoring institution shall, in accordance with relevant provisions, monitor margin safety and conduct daily audits; upon discovering problems, it shall immediately report to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, which shall handle them in a timely manner pursuant to this Regulation.

**Article 57** The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall implement a qualification management system for directors, supervisors, senior management personnel, and other practitioners of futures exchanges, futures companies and other futures operating institutions, and the margin safety depository monitoring institution.

**Article 58** The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall formulate rules on the continuous operation of futures companies and set risk regulatory indicators, including the ratio of net capital to net assets, the ratio of net capital to business scale for domestic and overseas brokerage, and the ratio of current assets to current liabilities. It shall also set requirements for business conditions, risk management, internal control, margin depository, and related-party transactions of futures companies and their branches.

**Article 59** Where a futures company or its branch fails to meet continuous operation rules or faces operational risks, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council may take supervisory measures such as interviews, reminders, and recording in credit files, or order rectification within a time limit, and shall inspect and accept the rectification.

If a futures company fails to rectify on time, and its conduct seriously endangers sound operation or harms clients’ lawful rights and interests, or it is under investigation by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council for suspected serious violations, the authority may, as the case may be, take the following measures:

1. Restrict or suspend certain futures businesses;
2. Stop approving new businesses or branches;
3. Restrict dividend distributions and restrict payment of remuneration and provision of benefits to directors, supervisors, and senior management;
4. Restrict property transfers or the establishment of other rights over property;
5. Order the replacement of directors, supervisors, senior management, or persons in charge of relevant departments or branches, or restrict their powers;
6. Restrict the allocation and use of proprietary funds or risk reserves;
7. Order controlling shareholders to transfer equity or restrict relevant shareholders from exercising shareholder rights.

For companies that, after rectification, meet legal and regulatory requirements and continuous operation rules, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall lift the measures within three days from completion of acceptance.

For companies that, after rectification, still fail to meet continuous operation rules and whose normal operation is seriously affected, the authority may revoke part or all of their futures business licenses or close their branches.

**Article 60** Where a futures company operates illegally or faces major risks that seriously endanger market order or harm client interests, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council may order suspension and rectification, appoint another institution to take over custody, or implement receivership. With approval by the authority, the following measures may be taken against directors, supervisors, senior management, and other directly responsible personnel:

1. Notify the exit administration to lawfully prevent their departure from the country;
2. Apply to judicial organs to prohibit transfer, assignment, or other disposal of property, or the creation of other rights over property.

**Article 61** Where shareholders of a futures company make false capital contributions or withdraw capital contributions, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall order correction within a time limit and may order the transfer of their equity in the futures company.

Before the shareholders correct their illegal conduct and transfer their equity as required in the preceding paragraph, the authority may restrict the exercise of their shareholder rights.

**Article 62** When abnormal circumstances arise in the futures market, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council may take necessary risk-disposal measures.

**Article 63** A futures company’s trading and clearing software shall meet the prudential operation and risk management requirements of the company and the provisions of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council on monitoring the safe custody of margins. Where the software fails to meet requirements, the authority has the right to require improvement or replacement.

The authority may require the software suppliers to provide relevant materials, and suppliers shall cooperate. The authority shall keep such materials confidential.

**Article 64** Where a futures company is involved in major litigation or arbitration, or its equity is frozen or pledged, or other major events occur, the company and its relevant shareholders and actual controllers shall submit a written report to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council within five days from the occurrence.

**Article 65** Intermediary service institutions such as accounting firms, law firms, and asset appraisal institutions shall comply with futures laws, administrative regulations, and State provisions when providing services to market participants such as futures exchanges and futures companies, and shall provide relevant materials as required by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council.

**Article 66** The futures regulatory authority of the State Council shall establish mechanisms for information sharing and coordinated cooperation with relevant departments.

It may also establish supervisory cooperation mechanisms with futures regulatory authorities of other countries or regions to implement cross-border supervision.

**Article 67** Staff of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, futures exchanges, the margin safety depository monitoring institution, margin depository banks, and other relevant units shall be devoted to their duties, act according to law, be fair and honest, keep State secrets and commercial secrets of members and clients, and shall not use their positions for improper gains.

## Chapter VII Legal Liability

**Article 68** Where a futures exchange or a non-futures-company clearing member commits any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to correct, given a warning, and its illegal gains confiscated:

1. Admitting members in violation of provisions;
2. Charging commissions in violation of provisions;
3. Using or distributing income in violation of provisions;
4. Failing to publish real-time market data as required or publishing price forecasts;
5. Failing to fulfill reporting obligations to the futures regulatory authority of the State Council as required;
6. Failing to submit documents and materials to the authority as required;
7. Failing to establish or improve a clearing guarantee fund system as required;
8. Failing to accrue, administer, or use risk reserves as required;
9. Violating provisions on monitoring of margin safety depository set by the authority;
10. Restricting the total volume of physical deliveries;
11. Employing unqualified futures practitioners;
12. Other acts in violation of provisions set by the authority.

For any of the acts above, the principal persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be subject to disciplinary sanctions and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 100,000.

For the act listed in item (2), the entity shall be ordered to refund overcharged commissions.

Where the margin safety depository monitoring institution commits the acts listed in items (5), (6), (9), (11), or (12), penalties and sanctions shall be imposed mutatis mutandis under paragraphs 1 and 2 of this Article. Margin depository banks committing the acts listed in items (9) or (12) shall be penalized accordingly.

**Article 69** Where a futures exchange or a non-futures-company clearing member commits any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to correct, warned, its illegal gains confiscated, and fined between one and five times the illegal gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 100,000, a fine between RMB 100,000 and RMB 500,000 shall be imposed; where the circumstances are serious, suspension and rectification shall be ordered:

1. Handling any matter listed in Article 13 without approval;
2. Allowing members to trade when their margin is insufficient;
3. Directly or indirectly participating in futures trading or engaging in businesses unrelated to its duties in violation of provisions;
4. Collecting margins in violation of provisions or misappropriating margins;
5. Forging, altering, or failing to keep records of trading, clearing, or delivery as required;
6. Failing to establish or implement daily no-debt settlement, price limits, position limits, or large-trader reporting systems;
7. Refusing or obstructing supervision and inspection by the authority;
8. Other acts violating provisions of the authority.

The principal persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be given disciplinary sanctions and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 100,000.

Where the margin safety depository monitoring institution commits the acts listed in items (3), (7), or (8), penalties and sanctions shall be imposed mutatis mutandis.

**Article 70** Where a futures company commits any of the following acts, it shall be ordered to correct, warned, its illegal gains confiscated, and fined between one and three times the illegal gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 100,000, a fine between RMB 100,000 and RMB 300,000 shall be imposed; where the circumstances are serious, suspension and rectification shall be ordered or its futures business license revoked:

1. Accepting mandates from entities or individuals that do not meet prescribed conditions;
2. Allowing clients to trade when their margin is insufficient;
3. Handling matters listed in Articles 19 or 20 without approval;
4. Engaging in activities unrelated to futures business in violation of provisions;
5. Engaging in, or in disguised form engaging in, proprietary futures trading;
6. Providing financing to shareholders, actual controllers, or other affiliates, or providing external guarantees;
7. Violating provisions on monitoring of margin safety depository set by the authority;
8. Failing to fulfill reporting obligations or submit documents and materials as required;
9. Using trading or clearing software that does not meet prudential operation, risk management, or margin monitoring requirements;
10. Failing to accrue, administer, or use risk reserves as required;
11. Forging, altering, or failing to keep records of trading, clearing, or delivery as required;
12. Employing unqualified futures practitioners;
13. Forging, altering, leasing, lending, buying, or selling futures business licenses or operating permits;
14. Conducting mixed-code trading;
15. Refusing or obstructing supervision and inspection by the authority;
16. Other acts in violation of provisions of the authority.

For any of the acts above, the principal persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be warned and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 50,000; where circumstances are serious, their qualifications for office or practice shall be suspended or revoked.

Other futures operating institutions, for the acts listed in items (8), (12), (13), (15), or (16), shall be penalized mutatis mutandis under paragraphs 1 and 2.

Where shareholders, actual controllers, or other affiliates of a futures company, without approval, entrust others or accept entrustment to hold or manage equity, or refuse to cooperate with inspections by the authority, or refuse to fulfill reporting obligations or provide information and materials as required, or submit information or materials with false records, misleading statements, or major omissions, penalties shall be imposed mutatis mutandis under paragraphs 1 and 2.

**Article 71** Where a futures company engages in any of the following acts of defrauding clients, it shall be ordered to correct, warned, its illegal gains confiscated, and fined between one and five times the illegal gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 100,000, a fine between RMB 100,000 and RMB 500,000 shall be imposed; where the circumstances are serious, suspension and rectification shall be ordered or its futures business license revoked:

1. Providing profit guarantees to clients or failing to present risk disclosure statements as required;
2. Agreeing with clients to share profits or jointly bear risks in brokerage business;
3. Failing to accept client mandates or trading contrary to client mandates as required;
4. Concealing material facts or using improper means to induce clients to issue trading instructions;
5. Providing clients with false execution reports;
6. Failing to transmit clients’ trading instructions to the exchange as required;
7. Misappropriating client margins;
8. Failing to open margin accounts at depository banks as required or transferring client margins in violation of rules;
9. Other fraudulent acts against clients as prescribed by the authority.

For any of the acts above, the principal persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be warned and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 100,000; where circumstances are serious, their qualifications for office or practice shall be suspended or revoked.

Any entity or individual that fabricates and disseminates false information about futures trading and disrupts the market shall be penalized mutatis mutandis under paragraphs 1 and 2.

**Article 72** Where a futures company, other futures operating institution, non-futures-company clearing member, or margin depository bank obtains a futures business license by submitting false application documents or by other fraudulent means that conceal material facts, its license shall be revoked and illegal gains confiscated.

**Article 73** Insiders of futures trading information or persons who unlawfully obtain such information who, before the information that may have a material impact on futures prices is disclosed, use the insider information to trade futures, or disclose such information to others for trading, shall have their illegal gains confiscated and be fined between one and five times the illegal gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 100,000, a fine between RMB 100,000 and RMB 500,000 shall be imposed. Where an entity engages in insider trading, the principal persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be warned and fined between RMB 30,000 and RMB 300,000.

Staff of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, futures exchanges, and the margin safety depository monitoring institution who engage in insider trading shall be subject to heavier penalties.

**Article 74** Where any entity or individual manipulates futures prices by any of the following means, it shall be ordered to correct, its illegal gains confiscated, and fined between one and five times the illegal gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 200,000, a fine between RMB 200,000 and RMB 1 million shall be imposed:

1. Acting alone or in concert, using capital or position advantages, or information advantages to jointly or continuously buy or sell contracts and manipulate prices;
2. Colluding deliberately to trade with each other at prearranged times, prices, and methods to affect prices or volumes;
3. Trading with oneself (wash trades) to affect prices or volumes;
4. Hoarding spot commodities to affect market conditions;
5. Other acts of manipulating prices as prescribed by the authority.

Where an entity commits the acts above, the principal persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be warned and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 100,000.

**Article 75** Where a delivery warehouse commits any of the acts listed in paragraph 2 of Article 39, it shall be ordered to correct, warned, its illegal gains confiscated, and fined between one and five times the illegal gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 100,000, a fine between RMB 100,000 and RMB 500,000 shall be imposed; where circumstances are serious, the futures exchange shall be ordered to suspend or cancel its qualification as a delivery warehouse. The principal persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be warned and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 100,000.

**Article 76** Where state-owned or state-controlled enterprises violate this Regulation or provisions of the State-owned Assets Supervision and Administration authority and other departments on the entry of state-owned assets into the futures market, or where entities or individuals illegally use credit or fiscal funds for futures trading, they shall be warned, their illegal gains confiscated, and fined between one and five times the illegal gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 100,000, a fine between RMB 100,000 and RMB 500,000 shall be imposed. The principal persons in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be subject to disciplinary sanctions, up to and including dismissal.

**Article 77** Where domestic entities or individuals engage in overseas futures trading in violation of provisions, they shall be ordered to correct, warned, their illegal gains confiscated, and fined between one and five times the illegal gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 200,000, a fine between RMB 200,000 and RMB 1 million shall be imposed; where circumstances are serious, their overseas futures trading shall be suspended. The principal persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be warned and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 100,000.

**Article 78** Where any entity or individual illegally establishes or in disguised form establishes a futures exchange, a futures company, or other futures operating institution, or engages in futures business without authorization, or organizes disguised futures trading activities, such activities shall be banned, illegal gains confiscated, and a fine between one and five times the illegal gains imposed; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 200,000, a fine between RMB 200,000 and RMB 1 million shall be imposed. The principal persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be warned and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 100,000.

**Article 79** Where a supplier of a futures company’s trading or clearing software refuses to cooperate with investigations by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, or fails to provide relevant software materials as required, or provides materials with falsehoods or major omissions, it shall be ordered to correct and fined between RMB 30,000 and RMB 100,000. The principal persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be warned and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 50,000.

**Article 80** Where intermediary service institutions such as accounting firms, law firms, or asset appraisal institutions fail to exercise due diligence and issue documents containing false records, misleading statements, or major omissions, they shall be ordered to correct, have their business income confiscated, have relevant business licenses suspended or revoked, and be fined between one and five times their business income. The principal persons in charge and other directly responsible persons shall be warned and fined between RMB 30,000 and RMB 100,000.

**Article 81** Where any entity or individual seriously violates this Regulation, the futures regulatory authority of the State Council may declare the person, the entity, or directly responsible persons of the entity to be barred from the futures market.

**Article 82** Where staff of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, futures exchanges, the margin safety depository monitoring institution, margin depository banks, or other relevant units disclose State secrets or members’ or clients’ commercial secrets, or commit favoritism, neglect duties, abuse power, or accept bribes, they shall be subject to administrative or disciplinary sanctions according to law.

**Article 83** Where a violation of this Regulation constitutes a crime, criminal liability shall be pursued according to law.

**Article 84** Administrative penalties for violations stipulated in this Regulation shall be decided by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council. Where the statutory functions of other departments are involved, the authority shall handle jointly with them; where the matters fall within the statutory functions of other departments, the authority shall transfer such matters to those departments.

## Chapter VIII Supplementary Provisions

**Article 85** For the purposes of this Regulation, the following terms shall have the meanings indicated:

1. **Futures contract**: a standardized contract uniformly formulated by a futures exchange that stipulates delivery of a specified quantity of an underlying at a specified time and place in the future. According to the underlying, futures contracts are divided into commodity futures and financial futures. Underlyings of commodity futures include agricultural products, industrial products, energy, other commodities, and related index products; underlyings of financial futures include securities, interest rates, exchange rates, and other financial products and related index products.
2. **Options contract**: a standardized contract uniformly formulated by a futures exchange granting the buyer the right to buy or sell the agreed underlying (including a futures contract) at a specified price at a specified time in the future.
3. **Margin**: funds paid by a futures trader according to prescribed standards for clearing and performance assurance.
4. **Clearing**: the process of settling funds and effecting transfers based on profits and losses of both parties determined by settlement prices published by the futures exchange.
5. **Delivery**: upon a contract’s expiration, the process of closing out open contracts in accordance with exchange rules and procedures by transfer of ownership of the underlying specified in the contract or by cash settlement at prescribed settlement prices.
6. **Offsetting (closing out)**: the act by which a futures trader buys or sells a contract that is the same in variety, quantity, and delivery month as the one held but in the opposite direction, thereby concluding the futures trade.
7. **Open interest**: the number of contracts held by a trader that have not been offset.
8. **Position limit**: the maximum number of contracts that a trader may hold as prescribed by the futures exchange.
9. **Warehouse receipt**: a standardized delivery voucher issued by a delivery warehouse and recognized by the futures exchange.
10. **Price limit (limit up/limit down)**: the mechanism under which the trading price of a contract shall not exceed prescribed up or down limits within a trading day; quotations beyond the limits are invalid and cannot be executed.
11. **Insider information**: information not yet disclosed that may have a material impact on futures prices, including: policies formulated by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council and other relevant departments that may materially affect futures prices; decisions made by futures exchanges that may materially affect prices; fund movements and trading trends of exchange members or clients; and other important information identified by the authority as having a significant impact on prices.
12. **Insiders**: persons who, by virtue of managerial, supervisory, or professional positions, or as employees or professional advisors performing duties, can access or obtain insider information, including: managers and other practitioners of futures exchanges who can obtain insider information due to their positions; staff of the futures regulatory authority of the State Council and other relevant departments; and other persons specified by the authority.

**Article 86** The futures regulatory authority of the State Council may approve the establishment of a specialized futures clearing institution to specifically perform the clearing and related duties of a futures exchange and to bear corresponding legal liabilities.

**Article 87** Administrative measures for overseas institutions establishing, acquiring, or taking equity in futures operating institutions within the territory, and for overseas futures operating institutions establishing branches (including representative offices) within the territory, shall be formulated by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council jointly with the competent commerce department of the State Council, the foreign exchange authority, and other relevant departments, and implemented after approval by the State Council.

**Article 88** Futures trading conducted on trading venues approved by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council other than futures exchanges shall be governed by relevant provisions of this Regulation.

**Article 89** Any institution or market that, without approval by the futures regulatory authority of the State Council, conducts standardized contract trading by centralized matching and simultaneously adopts any of the following trading mechanisms or has any of the following features shall be deemed to be conducting disguised futures trading:

1. Providing performance guarantees for all buyers and sellers participating in centralized trading;
2. Implementing daily no-debt settlement and a margin system while the margin ratio is below 20% of the contract (or agreement) notional.

Institutions or markets that, prior to the implementation of this Regulation, adopted or possessed the mechanisms or features set forth in the preceding paragraph shall make rectifications within the time limit specified by the competent commerce department of the State Council.

**Article 90** Other trading activities in commodities or financial products that do not constitute futures trading shall be supervised and administered by relevant State departments and are not governed by this Regulation.

**Article 91** This Regulation shall enter into force on 15 April 2007. The *Interim Regulation on the Administration of Futures Trading* promulgated by the State Council on 2 June 1999 shall be repealed simultaneously.
