# Measures for the Administration of Futures Exchanges  

(CSRC Order No. 42)



# Chapter I  General Provisions

**Article 1**  
For the purpose of strengthening the supervision and administration of futures exchanges, clarifying their responsibilities, maintaining order in the futures market, and promoting its sound and steady development, these Measures are formulated in accordance with the *Regulation on the Administration of Futures Trading*.

**Article 2**  
These Measures apply to futures exchanges established within the territory of the People’s Republic of China.

**Article 3**  
“Futures exchange,” as used in these Measures, refers to a legal person established pursuant to the *Regulation on the Administration of Futures Trading* and these Measures, which is non-profit, performs the duties prescribed in the *Regulation on the Administration of Futures Trading* and these Measures, and implements self-regulation in accordance with its articles of association and trading rules.

**Article 4**  
Upon approval by the China Securities Regulatory Commission (hereinafter the “CSRC”), a futures exchange may adopt either a membership or a corporate organizational form.  
For a membership futures exchange, its registered capital shall be divided into equal shares and subscribed by its members.  
A corporate futures exchange shall adopt the form of a joint stock limited company.

**Article 5**  
The CSRC shall, in accordance with the law, exercise centralized and unified supervision and administration over futures exchanges.

---

# Establishment, Change, and Termination

**Article 6**  
The establishment of a futures exchange shall be subject to the approval of the CSRC. Without approval, no entity or individual may establish a futures exchange or organize futures trading and related activities in any form.

**Article 7**  
A futures exchange established with the approval of the CSRC shall bear in its name the words “Commodity Exchange” or “Futures Exchange.” No other entity or individual may use the name of a futures exchange or any similar name.

**Article 8**  
In addition to performing the duties prescribed by the *Regulation on the Administration of Futures Trading*, a futures exchange shall perform the following duties:  
(1) Formulate and implement the trading rules of the futures exchange and their implementing details;  
(2) Publish market information;  
(3) Supervise the futures business of members and their clients, designated delivery warehouses, futures margin depository banks, and other market participants;  
(4) Investigate and handle violations.

**Article 9**  
To apply for the establishment of a futures exchange, the following documents and materials shall be submitted to the CSRC:  
(1) An application letter;  
(2) Draft articles of association and trading rules;  
(3) The business plan of the futures exchange;  
(4) A list of proposed members or shareholders;  
(5) A list and résumés of candidates for the council (board of directors) and the board of supervisors, as applicable;  
(6) A list and résumés of proposed senior management personnel;  
(7) Supporting documents and explanatory materials on premises, facilities, and funds;  
(8) Other documents and materials as required by the CSRC.

**Article 10**  
The articles of association of a futures exchange shall specify the following matters:  
(1) Purpose of establishment and duties;  
(2) Name, domicile, and place of business;  
(3) Registered capital and its composition;  
(4) Term of operation;  
(5) Composition, duties, term of office, and rules of procedure of the organizational bodies;  
(6) Methods for the selection, appointment and removal of management personnel, and their duties;  
(7) Basic business systems;  
(8) Risk reserve fund administration system;  
(9) Financial, accounting, and internal control systems;  
(10) Conditions and procedures for change and termination, and methods of liquidation;  
(11) Procedures for amending the articles of association;  
(12) Other matters that need to be provided for in the articles of association.

**Article 11**  
In addition to the matters set out in Article 10 of these Measures, the articles of association of a membership futures exchange shall also specify the following matters:  
(1) Membership qualifications and their administration;  
(2) The rights and obligations of members;  
(3) Disciplinary measures against members.

**Article 12**  
The trading rules of a futures exchange shall specify the following matters:  
(1) Systems for futures trading, clearing, and delivery;  
(2) Risk management systems and procedures for handling abnormal trading situations;  
(3) Systems for the administration and use of margin;  
(4) Methods for publishing futures trading information;  
(5) Violations and defaults and the measures for handling them;  
(6) Methods for handling trading disputes;  
(7) Other matters that need to be specified in the trading rules.  
A corporate futures exchange shall also specify in its trading rules the matters provided for in Article 11 of these Measures.

**Article 13**  
Any change in the name or registered capital of a futures exchange shall be subject to the approval of the CSRC.

**Article 14**  
Mergers and divisions of futures exchanges shall be approved by the CSRC.  
A merger of futures exchanges may take the form of absorption or the establishment of a new exchange; the rights and obligations of the parties prior to the merger shall be succeeded to by the surviving or newly established futures exchange.  
Where a futures exchange is divided, its rights and obligations shall be succeeded to by the futures exchanges resulting from the division.

**Article 15**  
Where a futures exchange conducts networked trading, it shall report to the CSRC within 10 days from the date the decision is made.

**Article 16**  
Without the approval of the CSRC, a futures exchange shall not establish branches or any other futures trading venues.

**Article 17**  
A futures exchange shall be dissolved under any of the following circumstances:  
(1) The operating term specified in its articles of association expires;  
(2) The members’ general assembly or the shareholders’ meeting resolves to dissolve it;  
(3) The CSRC decides to close it.  
Dissolution under subparagraphs (1) and (2) of the preceding paragraph shall be subject to the approval of the CSRC.

**Article 18**  
Where a futures exchange is terminated due to merger, division, or dissolution, the CSRC shall make a public announcement.  
Upon termination of a futures exchange, a liquidation group shall be formed to conduct liquidation. The liquidation plan formulated by the liquidation group shall be submitted to the CSRC for approval.

# Chapter III  Organizational Structure

## Section 1  Membership Futures Exchanges

**Article 19**  
A membership futures exchange shall establish a members’ general assembly. The members’ general assembly is the authority of the futures exchange and is composed of all members.

**Article 20**  
The members’ general assembly shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Review and approve the articles of association, trading rules, and draft amendments thereto of the futures exchange;  
(2) Elect and replace member directors;  
(3) Deliberate on and approve the work reports of the council (board of directors) and the general manager;  
(4) Deliberate on and approve the budgets and final accounts of the futures exchange;  
(5) Deliberate on the use of the risk reserve fund of the futures exchange;  
(6) Decide on increases or decreases of the registered capital of the futures exchange;  
(7) Decide on matters relating to merger, division, dissolution, and liquidation of the futures exchange;  
(8) Decide on other major matters submitted by the council;  
(9) Other powers provided in the articles of association.

**Article 21**  
The members’ general assembly shall be convened by the council and held once a year.  
An extraordinary members’ meeting shall be convened under any of the following circumstances:  
(1) The number of member directors falls below two-thirds of that specified in the articles of association;  
(2) A joint proposal by more than one-third of the members;  
(3) The council deems it necessary.

**Article 22**  
Meetings of the members’ general assembly shall be presided over by the chair of the council. Members shall be notified of the matters to be deliberated no later than 10 days before the meeting is held.  
An extraordinary members’ meeting shall not adopt resolutions on matters not listed in the notice.

**Article 23**  
A meeting of the members’ general assembly shall be valid only if more than two-thirds of the members attend. Minutes shall be kept for voting matters, and shall be signed by the directors attending the meeting.  
Within 10 days from the end of the meeting, the futures exchange shall submit all meeting documents to the CSRC.

**Article 24**  
A futures exchange shall establish a council (board of directors) with a term of three years. The council is the standing body of the members’ general assembly and is responsible to it.

**Article 25**  
The council shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Convene meetings of the members’ general assembly and report on its work thereto;  
(2) Draft the articles of association, trading rules, and amendments thereto, and submit them to the members’ general assembly for review and approval;  
(3) Deliberate on the budgets and final accounts proposed by the general manager, and submit them to the members’ general assembly for adoption;  
(4) Deliberate on plans for merger, division, dissolution, and liquidation of the futures exchange, and submit them to the members’ general assembly for adoption;  
(5) Decide on the establishment of specialized committees;  
(6) Decide on the admission and withdrawal of members;  
(7) Decide on disciplinary sanctions for violations;  
(8) Decide on changes to the name, domicile, or place of business of the futures exchange;  
(9) Deliberate on and approve implementing rules and measures formulated pursuant to the articles of association and trading rules;  
(10) Deliberate on the use of settlement guaranty funds;  
(11) Deliberate on and approve plans for the use of the risk reserve fund;  
(12) Deliberate on and approve development plans and annual work plans proposed by the general manager;  
(13) Deliberate on and approve the futures exchange’s plans for external investment;  
(14) Supervise the implementation of resolutions of the members’ general assembly and the council by the general manager;  
(15) Supervise compliance by senior management and other staff with laws, administrative regulations, rules, policies, and the articles of association, trading rules, and implementing details;  
(16) Organize the annual audit of the financial and accounting reports of the futures exchange, and decide on the engagement and change of the accounting firm;  
(17) Other powers provided in the articles of association and conferred by the members’ general assembly.

**Article 26**  
The council shall be composed of member directors and non-member directors; member directors shall be elected by the members’ general assembly, and non-member directors shall be appointed by the CSRC.

**Article 27**  
The council shall have one chair and one to two vice-chairs. The appointment and removal of the chair and vice-chairs shall be nominated by the CSRC and adopted by the council. The chair shall not concurrently serve as the general manager.

**Article 28**  
The chair shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Preside over meetings of the members’ general assembly and the council, and the day-to-day work of the council;  
(2) Organize and coordinate the work of specialized committees;  
(3) Inspect the implementation of council resolutions and report to the council.  
The vice-chairs shall assist the chair. Where the chair is temporarily unable to perform duties, a vice-chair or director designated by the chair shall act in the chair’s stead.

**Article 29**  
The council shall meet at least once every six months. All directors shall be notified at least 10 days prior to each meeting.  
An extraordinary council meeting shall be convened under any of the following circumstances:  
(1) A joint proposal by more than one-third of the directors;  
(2) Circumstances specified in the articles of association;  
(3) A proposal by the CSRC.  
For extraordinary meetings, the method and time limits for notice may be otherwise specified.

**Article 30**  
A council meeting shall be valid only if more than two-thirds of the directors are present. Resolutions shall be adopted by a vote of more than one-half of all directors.  
Within 10 days from the end of the meeting, the council shall submit the meeting resolutions and other meeting documents to the CSRC.

**Article 31**  
Council meetings shall be attended by directors in person. A director who is unable to attend for a reason shall, in writing, entrust another director to attend on his or her behalf; the letter of authorization shall specify the scope of authorization. Each director may accept the proxy of only one other director.  
Minutes shall be kept for voting matters at council meetings and signed by the directors present and the recorder.

**Article 32**  
The council may, as needed, establish specialized committees such as supervision, trading, clearing, delivery, membership qualification review, discipline, mediation, finance, and technology committees.  
Each specialized committee shall be responsible to the council; its duties, term of office, and composition shall be prescribed by the council.

**Article 33**  
A futures exchange shall have one general manager and several deputy general managers. The general manager and deputy general managers shall be appointed or removed by the CSRC. The term of office of the general manager shall be three years and may not exceed two consecutive terms.  
The general manager is the legal representative of the futures exchange and shall serve ex officio as a director.

**Article 34**  
The general manager shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Organize the implementation of the systems and resolutions adopted by the members’ general assembly and the council;  
(2) Preside over the day-to-day operations of the futures exchange;  
(3) Draft implementing rules and measures pursuant to the articles of association and trading rules;  
(4) Decide on the use of settlement guaranty funds;  
(5) Draft plans for the use of the risk reserve fund;  
(6) Draft and implement approved development plans and annual work plans of the futures exchange;  
(7) Draft and implement approved plans for the futures exchange’s external investments;  
(8) Draft the budgets and final accounts of the futures exchange;  
(9) Draft plans for merger, division, dissolution, and liquidation of the futures exchange;  
(10) Draft plans for changing the name, domicile, or place of business of the futures exchange;  
(11) Decide on organizational set-up, and hire and dismiss staff;  
(12) Decide on staff remuneration and rewards and penalties;  
(13) Other powers provided in the articles of association or conferred by the council.  
Where the general manager is temporarily unable to perform duties, a deputy general manager designated by the general manager shall act in the general manager’s stead.

**Article 35**  
Where a futures exchange appoints or removes middle-level managers, it shall report to the CSRC within 10 days from the date of the decision.

## Section 2  Corporate Futures Exchanges

**Article 36**  
A corporate futures exchange shall establish a shareholders’ meeting. The shareholders’ meeting is the authority of the futures exchange and is composed of all shareholders.

**Article 37**  
The shareholders’ meeting shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) The powers specified in subparagraph (1) and subparagraphs (4) through (7) of Article 20 of these Measures;  
(2) Elect and replace directors and supervisors other than those serving as employee representatives;  
(3) Deliberate on and approve the work reports of the board of directors, the board of supervisors, and the general manager;  
(4) Decide on other major matters submitted by the board of directors;  
(5) Other powers provided in the articles of association.

**Article 38**  
The convening and rules of procedure of the shareholders’ meeting shall comply with the articles of association of the futures exchange.  
Within 10 days from the end of the meeting, the futures exchange shall submit all meeting documents to the CSRC.

**Article 39**  
A futures exchange shall establish a board of directors with a term of three years.

**Article 40**  
The board of directors shall be responsible to the shareholders’ meeting and exercise the following powers:  
(1) Convene meetings of the shareholders’ meeting and report on its work thereto;  
(2) Draft the articles of association, trading rules, and amendments thereto, and submit them to the shareholders’ meeting for review and approval;  
(3) Deliberate on the budgets and final accounts proposed by the general manager, and submit them to the shareholders’ meeting for adoption;  
(4) Deliberate on plans for merger, division, dissolution, and liquidation of the futures exchange, and submit them to the shareholders’ meeting for adoption;  
(5) Supervise the implementation of resolutions of the shareholders’ meeting and the board by the general manager;  
(6) Exercise the powers specified in subparagraphs (5) through (13), (15), and (16) of Article 25 of these Measures;  
(7) Other powers provided in the articles of association and conferred by the shareholders’ meeting.

**Article 41**  
A futures exchange shall have one chair and one to two vice-chairs of the board. The appointment and removal of the chair and vice-chairs shall be nominated by the CSRC and adopted by the board. The chair shall not concurrently serve as the general manager.

**Article 42**  
The chair shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Preside over meetings of the shareholders’ meeting and the board, and the day-to-day work of the board;  
(2) Organize and coordinate the work of specialized committees;  
(3) Inspect the implementation of board resolutions and report to the board.  
The vice-chairs shall assist the chair. Where the chair is temporarily unable to perform duties, a vice-chair or director designated by the chair shall act in the chair’s stead.

**Article 43**  
The convening and rules of procedure of board meetings shall comply with the articles of association of the futures exchange.  
Within 10 days from the end of the meeting, the board shall submit the meeting resolutions and other meeting documents to the CSRC.

**Article 44**  
The board may, as needed, establish the specialized committees provided for in Article 32 of these Measures. Each specialized committee shall be responsible to the board; its duties, term of office, and composition shall be prescribed by the board.

**Article 45**  
A futures exchange shall have independent directors. Independent directors shall be nominated by the CSRC and adopted by the shareholders’ meeting.

**Article 46**  
A futures exchange may have a board secretary. The board secretary shall be nominated by the CSRC and adopted by the board.  
The board secretary shall be responsible for preparing meetings of the shareholders’ meeting and the board, keeping documents, and administering shareholder records of the futures exchange, among other matters.

**Article 47**  
A futures exchange shall have one general manager and several deputy general managers. The general manager and deputy general managers shall be appointed or removed by the CSRC. The term of office of the general manager shall be three years and may not exceed two consecutive terms.  
The general manager is the legal representative of the futures exchange and shall be a director.

**Article 48**  
The general manager shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Organize the implementation of the systems and resolutions adopted by the shareholders’ meeting and the board;  
(2) The powers set out in subparagraphs (2) through (12) of Article 34 of these Measures;  
(3) Other powers provided in the articles of association or conferred by the board.  
Where the general manager is temporarily unable to perform duties, a deputy general manager designated by the general manager shall act in the general manager’s stead.

**Article 49**  
A futures exchange shall establish a board of supervisors with a term of three years. The board of supervisors shall have no fewer than three members and shall have one chair and one to two vice-chairs. The appointment and removal of the chair and vice-chairs of the board of supervisors shall be nominated by the CSRC and adopted by the board of supervisors.

**Article 50**  
The board of supervisors shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Inspect the finances of the futures exchange;  
(2) Supervise the conduct of directors and senior management personnel in performing their duties;  
(3) Submit proposals to meetings of the shareholders’ meeting;  
(4) Other powers provided in the articles of association.

**Article 51**  
The convening and rules of procedure of meetings of the board of supervisors shall comply with the articles of association of the futures exchange.  
Within 10 days from the end of the meeting, the board of supervisors shall submit the meeting resolutions and other meeting documents to the CSRC.

**Article 52**  
Article 35 of these Measures shall apply to corporate futures exchanges.

# Chapter IV  Administration of Members

**Article 53**  
Members of a futures exchange shall be enterprises or other economic organizations registered within the territory of the People’s Republic of China.

**Article 54**  
The acquisition of membership shall be subject to the approval of the futures exchange.  
The approval or revocation of a member’s qualification by the futures exchange shall be reported to the CSRC.

**Article 55**  
A futures exchange shall formulate membership administration measures specifying the conditions and procedures for the acquisition and termination of membership and matters relating to supervision and administration of members.

**Article 56**  
Members of a membership futures exchange shall enjoy the following rights:  
(1) Participate in the members’ general assembly and exercise rights to elect, to be elected, and to vote;  
(2) Engage in trading, clearing, delivery, and other business as prescribed by the futures exchange;  
(3) Use the trading facilities provided by the futures exchange and obtain information and services relating to futures trading;  
(4) Transfer membership as prescribed;  
(5) Jointly propose the convening of an extraordinary members’ meeting;  
(6) Exercise the right to appeal in accordance with the articles of association and trading rules;  
(7) Other rights provided in the articles of association.

**Article 57**  
Members of a membership futures exchange shall perform the following obligations:  
(1) Comply with relevant state laws, administrative regulations, rules, and policies;  
(2) Comply with the articles of association, trading rules, implementing details, and relevant decisions of the futures exchange;  
(3) Pay various fees as prescribed;  
(4) Implement resolutions of the members’ general assembly and the council;  
(5) Accept the supervision and administration of the futures exchange.

**Article 58**  
Members of a corporate futures exchange shall enjoy the following rights:  
(1) The rights set out in subparagraphs (2) and (3) of Article 56 of these Measures;  
(2) Exercise the right to appeal in accordance with the trading rules;  
(3) Other rights provided in the trading rules of the futures exchange.

**Article 59**  
Members of a corporate futures exchange shall perform the obligations set out in subparagraphs (1) through (3) and (5) of Article 57 of these Measures.

**Article 60**  
A futures exchange shall, each year, conduct sample or comprehensive inspections on members’ compliance with the trading rules and implementing details, and report the inspection results to the CSRC.  
When exercising supervisory powers, a futures exchange may, within the scope of authority and procedures provided in its articles of association, trading rules, and implementing details, conduct investigations and collect evidence from members, and members shall cooperate.

**Article 61**  
Upon approval by the CSRC, a futures exchange may implement either a full-member clearing system or a tiered member clearing system.

**Article 62**  
Under a full-member clearing system, all members of the futures exchange shall be qualified to clear with the futures exchange.

**Article 63**  
Under a full-member clearing system, members of the futures exchange shall consist of futures company members and non-futures-company members. Futures company members shall conduct business within the scope approved by the CSRC; non-futures-company members shall not engage in the futures company business prescribed by the *Regulation on the Administration of Futures Trading*.

**Article 64**  
Under a full-member clearing system, the futures exchange clears with its members, and members clear with their entrusted clients.

**Article 65**  
Under a tiered member clearing system, members of the futures exchange shall consist of clearing members and non-clearing members. Clearing members shall be qualified to clear with the futures exchange, while non-clearing members shall not.  
The futures exchange clears with clearing members; clearing members clear with non-clearing members; and non-clearing members clear with their entrusted clients.

**Article 66**  
Clearing members shall consist of trading clearing members, comprehensive clearing members, and special clearing members.  
Comprehensive and special clearing members may conduct clearing for non-clearing members with whom they have entered into clearing agreements. Trading clearing members shall not conduct clearing for non-clearing members.

**Article 67**  
An applicant to become a clearing member shall have obtained CSRC approval for clearing business qualifications.

**Article 68**  
Under a tiered member clearing system, a futures exchange may, based on the creditworthiness and business operations of clearing members, restrict the scope of their clearing business, but shall report such restriction to the CSRC within three days.

# Chapter V  Basic Business Rules

**Article 69**  
Margin collected by a futures exchange from members may only be used to secure the performance of futures contracts and shall not be seized, frozen, deducted, or subject to compulsory enforcement. A futures exchange shall open a dedicated settlement account at a futures margin depository bank to hold margin funds on a segregated basis, and shall not misappropriate such funds.  
Margin shall consist of settlement reserves and trading margin. “Settlement reserves” refer to margin not occupied by contracts; “trading margin” refers to margin occupied by contracts.  
Under a tiered member clearing system, a futures exchange shall collect margin only from clearing members.

**Article 70**  
A futures exchange shall establish a margin administration system, which shall include:  
(1) The standards and forms for collecting margin from members;  
(2) The minimum balance of members’ settlement reserves in the dedicated settlement account;  
(3) Disposal methods when a member’s settlement reserve balance falls below the minimum specified by the futures exchange.  
The minimum balance of settlement reserves shall be paid by the member to the futures exchange with its own funds.

**Article 71**  
A futures exchange may accept the following securities to offset margin:  
(1) Standard warehouse receipts recognized by the futures exchange;  
(2) Tradable treasury bonds;  
(3) Other securities recognized by the CSRC.  
Where securities as provided in the preceding paragraph are used to offset margin, the offset period shall not exceed the valid term of the relevant securities.

**Article 72**  
Where standard warehouse receipts are used to offset margin, the futures exchange shall calculate their value on the basis of the settlement price on the trading day immediately preceding the offset date of the nearest delivery-month futures contract for the commodity corresponding to the warehouse receipt.  
Where treasury bonds are used to offset margin, the futures exchange shall calculate their value on the basis of the lower of the closing prices on the Shanghai Stock Exchange or the Shenzhen Stock Exchange on the trading day immediately preceding the offset date.  
The futures exchange may, in light of market conditions, adjust the benchmark calculation value of securities used to offset margin.

**Article 73**  
The amount of securities used to offset margin shall not exceed the lower of the following standards:  
(1) 80% of the benchmark calculation value of the securities;  
(2) Four times the member’s actual monetary funds held in the futures exchange’s dedicated settlement account.

**Article 74**  
Losses, fees, payments, taxes, and other amounts arising from futures trading shall be paid in monetary funds and shall not be settled by using the amount of securities offset against margin.

**Article 75**  
Where a client uses securities to offset margin, the member shall submit the received securities to the futures exchange.  
Where a client of a non-clearing member uses securities to offset margin, the non-clearing member shall submit the received securities to the clearing member, which shall then submit them to the futures exchange.

**Article 76**  
Where a client uses securities to offset margin, the futures exchange shall accurately reflect the type and quantity of the securities used for the offset under that client’s trading code.

**Article 77**  
A futures exchange implementing a tiered member clearing system shall establish a settlement guaranty fund system. The settlement guaranty fund shall consist of a basic settlement guaranty fund and a variable settlement guaranty fund.  
The settlement guaranty fund shall be paid by clearing members to the futures exchange with their own funds. The settlement guaranty fund belongs to the clearing members and is used to address the default risk of clearing members. The futures exchange shall administer and use it in accordance with relevant provisions and shall not divert it to other uses.  
Where a futures exchange adjusts the standard amount of the basic settlement guaranty fund, it shall report to the CSRC before the adjustment.

**Article 78**  
A futures exchange shall allocate 20% of its fee income to a risk reserve fund, which shall be accounted for separately and held in a dedicated account.  
The CSRC may, in light of the futures exchange’s business scale, development plan, and potential risks, determine the scale of the risk reserve fund.

**Article 79**  
Futures trading shall implement a client trading code system. Members and clients shall comply with the “one account, one code” requirement and shall not mix codes in trading.

**Article 80**  
Futures trading shall implement a position limit system and a hedging approval system.

**Article 81**  
Futures trading shall implement a large position reporting system. Where a member or client’s position reaches the reporting standard prescribed by the futures exchange, the member or client shall report to the futures exchange. If a client fails to report, the member shall report to the futures exchange.  
The futures exchange may formulate and adjust the reporting standards for positions in light of market risk conditions.

**Article 82**  
Futures trading shall implement a daily mark-to-market settlement system.

**Article 83**  
Under a full-member clearing system, a futures exchange shall conduct risk management for its members, and members shall conduct risk management for their entrusted clients.  
Under a tiered member clearing system, a futures exchange shall conduct risk management for clearing members; clearing members shall conduct risk management for non-clearing members with whom they have entered into clearing agreements; and members shall conduct risk management for their entrusted clients.

**Article 84**  
Where a member defaults in futures trading, it shall bear liability for breach.  
A futures exchange shall first use the margin of the defaulting member to bear such liability. If the margin is insufficient, then: under a full-member clearing system, the futures exchange shall bear the liability using the defaulting member’s own funds, the futures exchange’s risk reserve fund, and the futures exchange’s own funds; under a tiered member clearing system, the futures exchange shall bear the liability using the defaulting member’s own funds, the settlement guaranty fund, the futures exchange’s risk reserve fund, and the futures exchange’s own funds.  
Where a futures exchange bears liability on behalf of a member with the settlement guaranty fund, the risk reserve fund, or its own funds, it shall acquire the corresponding right of recourse against the defaulting member.

**Article 85**  
Where there is reason to believe that a member or client has violated the trading rules and implementing details of the futures exchange and is causing or is likely to cause a significant impact on the market, the futures exchange may, in order to prevent further expansion of the consequences of the violation, take the following temporary disposal measures against such member or client:  
(1) Restrict deposits;  
(2) Restrict withdrawals;  
(3) Restrict opening of new positions;  
(4) Raise margin standards;  
(5) Order position reduction within a specified time;  
(6) Enforce forced liquidation.  
Where a futures exchange takes the measures set out in subparagraphs (4), (5), or (6) of the preceding paragraph in accordance with the procedures prescribed in the trading rules and implementing details, it shall promptly report to the CSRC after taking such measures.  
When taking temporary disposal measures, the futures exchange shall notify the member or client in the manner prescribed by the trading rules and implementing details and specify the grounds for such measures.

**Article 86**  
Where futures prices hit the up-limit or down-limit for several consecutive sessions in the same direction, the futures exchange may adopt measures such as adjusting the limit range, raising trading margin standards, and reducing positions according to certain principles to defuse risks.

**Article 87**  
A futures exchange shall implement a risk warning system. Where it deems necessary, it may, separately or simultaneously, require members and clients to report relevant information, conduct reminder interviews, or issue risk warning letters, in order to warn against and defuse risks.

**Article 88**  
Where any of the following circumstances occurs during futures trading, a futures exchange may declare the occurrence of an abnormal situation and take emergency measures to defuse risks:  
(1) Trading cannot proceed normally due to force majeure such as earthquake, flood, or fire, or for reasons not attributable to the futures exchange such as computer system failures;  
(2) A member experiences a clearing or delivery crisis that is causing or is likely to cause a significant impact on the market;  
(3) The circumstances provided for in Article 86 of these Measures occur, and risks remain unresolved after corresponding measures have been taken;  
(4) Other circumstances specified in the trading rules and implementing details of the futures exchange.  
Before declaring the occurrence of an abnormal situation and deciding to take emergency measures, the futures exchange shall report to the CSRC.

**Article 89**  
Where a futures exchange declares the occurrence of an abnormal situation and decides to suspend trading, the suspension shall not exceed three trading days, unless an extension is approved by the CSRC.

**Article 90**  
A futures exchange shall publish the following information in an appropriate manner:  
(1) Real-time quotations;  
(2) Rankings of open interest and trading volume;  
(3) Other information specified in the trading rules and implementing details of the futures exchange.  
Where futures trading involves physical delivery of commodities, the futures exchange shall also publish information on the number of standard warehouse receipts and available storage capacity.

**Article 91**  
A futures exchange shall compile weekly, monthly, and annual reports on trading conditions and publish them in a timely manner.

**Article 92**  
A futures exchange shall keep records relating to futures trading, clearing, and delivery for not less than 20 years.

# Chapter VI  Supervision and Administration

**Article 93**  
Where a futures exchange formulates or amends its articles of association or trading rules; lists, suspends, cancels, or resumes trading of a product; or lists, amends, or terminates a contract, it shall obtain the approval of the CSRC.

**Article 94**  
A futures exchange shall formulate measures for investigating and handling violations of its trading rules and implementing details, and submit them to the CSRC for approval.  
Where members and their clients, designated delivery warehouses, futures margin depository banks, or other futures market participants commit violations relating to futures business, the futures exchange shall promptly investigate and handle such violations within the scope of duties prescribed by the measures referred to in the preceding paragraph; matters beyond such scope shall be reported to the CSRC.

**Article 95**  
When formulating or amending the implementing details of its trading rules, a futures exchange shall seek the opinions of the CSRC and, prior to official release and implementation, report them to the CSRC.

**Article 96**  
The trading and clearing systems and trading and clearing operations of a futures exchange shall meet the requirements for the safe custody and monitoring of futures margin and shall truthfully, accurately, and completely reflect changes in members’ margin.

**Article 97**  
In accordance with the CSRC’s provisions on the safe custody and monitoring of futures margin, a futures exchange shall submit relevant information to the margin safe-custody and monitoring institution.

**Article 98**  
For a corporate futures exchange, acquisitions by the exchange of its own shares, transfers of shares by shareholders, or any other disposition of shares shall be subject to the approval of the CSRC.

**Article 99**  
Senior management personnel of a futures exchange shall meet the conditions required by the CSRC. Without the approval of the CSRC, the chair and vice-chairs of the council, the chair and vice-chairs of the board of directors, the chair and vice-chairs of the board of supervisors, the general manager, deputy general managers, and the board secretary shall not concurrently hold positions in any profit-making organization.  
Without approval, other staff of the futures exchange and non-member directors shall not, in any form, concurrently hold positions in member entities of the futures exchange or other profit-making organizations related to futures business.

**Article 100**  
Staff of a futures exchange shall consciously abide by relevant laws, administrative regulations, rules, and policies; perform their duties faithfully and diligently; uphold honesty and creditworthiness; and possess good professional ethics.  
Staff of a futures exchange shall not engage in futures trading, shall not disclose inside information or use inside information to obtain illegal gains, and shall not seek benefits from members or clients of the futures exchange.  
Where, in performing duties, a staff member encounters a situation involving a conflict of interest with himself/herself or his/her relatives, such staff member shall recuse himself/herself.

**Article 101**  
The income of a futures exchange shall be administered and used in accordance with relevant state provisions, but shall first be used to ensure the operation and improvement of the trading venues and facilities of the futures exchange.

**Article 102**  
A futures exchange shall fulfill the following reporting obligations to the CSRC:  
(1) Within four months after the end of each year, submit an annual financial report audited by an accounting firm with qualifications in securities and futures;  
(2) Within 15 days after the end of each quarter and within 30 days after the end of each year, submit quarterly and annual work reports on its operations and on the implementation of relevant laws, administrative regulations, rules, and policies;  
(3) Other matters as required by the CSRC.

**Article 103**  
Upon the occurrence of any of the following major events, a futures exchange shall promptly report to the CSRC:  
(1) Discovery that staff of the futures exchange are engaged in, or may be engaged in, serious violations of relevant state laws, administrative regulations, rules, or policies;  
(2) Litigation involving the futures exchange amounting to 10% or more of its net assets or having a significant impact on its operational risk;  
(3) Major financial expenditures or investment matters of the futures exchange and major financial decisions that may bring about significant financial or operational risks;  
(4) Other matters as required by the CSRC.

**Article 104**  
The CSRC may, in light of market conditions, adjust the margin standards collected by a futures exchange, and suspend, resume, or cancel trading of a particular futures product.

**Article 105**  
Where the CSRC deems that abnormal market conditions have arisen in the futures market, it may decide to adopt necessary risk-disposal measures such as delayed market opening, suspension of trading, or early market closure.

**Article 106**  
Where the CSRC deems it necessary, it may issue reminders to senior management personnel of a futures exchange.

**Article 107**  
Where a CSRC dispatched office takes regulatory measures to dispose of risks of members of a futures exchange, and such measures are approved by the CSRC, the futures exchange shall cooperate by restricting members’ fund transfers, restricting members from opening new positions, transferring positions, enforcing forced liquidation, and other measures.

**Article 108**  
The CSRC may station inspectors at a futures exchange. Inspectors shall perform their duties in accordance with relevant provisions of the CSRC.  
A futures exchange shall cooperate with inspectors in the performance of their duties.

**Article 109**  
A futures exchange shall pay futures market regulatory fees in a timely manner in accordance with relevant state provisions.

# Chapter VII  Legal Liability

**Article 110**  
Where a futures exchange fails to fulfill the reporting obligations prescribed in Articles 15, 35, 52, 68, 88, 95, 102, and 103 of these Measures, or fails to submit relevant documents, materials, and information as prescribed in Articles 23, 30, 38, 43, 51, and 97 of these Measures, it shall be punished in accordance with Article 68 of the *Regulation on the Administration of Futures Trading*.

**Article 111**  
Where a futures exchange commits any of the following acts, it shall be punished in accordance with Article 69 of the *Regulation on the Administration of Futures Trading*:  
(1) Changing its name or registered capital without approval;  
(2) Establishing branches or any other trading venues without approval;  
(3) Violating provisions on using securities to offset margin;  
(4) Failing to inspect members as required;  
(5) Failing to establish or implement a client trading code system or a margin administration system;  
(6) Trading and clearing systems and operations not conforming to Article 96 of these Measures.

**Article 112**  
Where staff of a futures exchange violate Article 100 of these Measures, they shall be punished in accordance with Article 82 of the *Regulation on the Administration of Futures Trading*.

# Chapter VIII  Supplementary Provisions

**Article 113**  
Where futures trading is conducted in other trading venues approved by the CSRC, these Measures shall apply mutatis mutandis.

**Article 114**  
These Measures shall come into force on April 15, 2007. The *Measures for the Administration of Futures Exchanges* promulgated on May 17, 2002 (CSRC Order No. 6) shall be repealed simultaneously.
