# Guidelines for the Internal Control System on Futures Hedging Operations of State-Owned Cotton-Related Enterprises  
(Zhengjian Qihuo Zi [2004] No. 32)

## Chapter I  General Provisions

**Article 1**  
To guide and regulate futures hedging activities conducted by cotton production and operation enterprises in which state-owned assets hold a controlling position (hereinafter referred to as “state-owned cotton-related enterprises”), to effectively prevent and resolve risks, and to maintain order in the futures market, these Guidelines are formulated pursuant to the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*.

**Article 2**  
State-owned cotton-related enterprises may enter the futures market only to conduct hedging operations and shall not engage in other futures trading activities.

**Article 3**  
In conducting hedging operations, state-owned cotton-related enterprises shall comply with the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading* and these Guidelines, and follow the principles of openness, fairness, impartiality, and good faith.

**Article 4**  
In conducting hedging operations, state-owned cotton-related enterprises shall, in accordance with these Guidelines, strengthen internal management and risk control, strictly observe all management systems, and ensure standardized operations.

## Chapter II  Conditions for Hedging Operations

**Article 5**  
A state-owned cotton-related enterprise engaging in hedging operations shall satisfy the following conditions:  
(1) The futures varieties traded shall be limited to the enterprise’s own products from production and operation or the raw materials required for production;  
(2) It shall have sound internal control, risk management, and internal management systems for hedging;  
(3) Personnel in charge of hedging operations shall be vice general manager or above of the enterprise or senior managers of an equivalent level, and shall possess certain professional knowledge of futures and experience in risk management;  
(4) It shall have at least one trader with experience in futures trading, who holds a futures practitioner qualification and has no record of misconduct;  
(5) It shall have proprietary funds commensurate with the margin required for hedging;  
(6) It shall comply with other provisions of the China Securities Regulatory Commission (CSRC).

**Article 6**  
State-owned cotton-related enterprises conducting hedging operations shall, in accordance with relevant provisions, obtain approval from the competent authority and obtain an approval document. Cotton production and operation enterprises that have loans from the Agricultural Development Bank of China and apply to conduct futures business shall handle the matter pursuant to the relevant provisions of the Agricultural Development Bank of China.

**Article 7**  
After obtaining the approval document, the enterprise shall apply to a futures brokerage company to open a hedging trading account. Based on the enterprise’s hedging application materials, the futures brokerage company shall apply to the futures exchange for a hedging position not exceeding the enterprise’s physical spot purchase-and-sale scale; the futures exchange shall review and approve the hedging application in accordance with the relevant provisions and procedures.  
A state-owned cotton-related enterprise that is a proprietary (self-operated) member of a futures exchange may apply directly to the futures exchange for hedging positions.

**Article 8**  
State-owned cotton-related enterprises shall file the actual quota approved by the futures exchange with the competent authority.

## Chapter III  Internal Control System

**Article 9**  
State-owned cotton-related enterprises shall establish an authorization system for futures hedging operations. When opening a hedging trading account with a futures brokerage company, the account-opening agreement shall be signed by the enterprise’s legal representative or by a person authorized in writing by the legal representative.

**Article 10**  
Authorization for futures hedging operations includes trading authorization and authorization for the transfer of trading funds. The enterprise shall keep the authorized traders and the authorized fund transfer personnel mutually independent and mutually restraining.

**Article 11**  
A trading authorization letter shall list the names of personnel authorized to engage in hedging transactions, the specific varieties permitted, and trading limits; an authorization letter for fund transfers shall list the names of personnel authorized to transfer funds and the fund limits.

**Article 12**  
Authorization letters for futures hedging operations shall be issued by the enterprise’s legal representative or the vice general manager in charge of futures business; authorizations involving fund transfers shall also be consented to by the vice general manager in charge of finance.

**Article 13**  
After an authorization letter is issued, the enterprise shall promptly notify all relevant parties.  
An authorized person may operate within the authorized scope only after obtaining written authorization.

**Article 14**  
Personnel authorized to conduct hedging transactions shall possess corresponding professional knowledge of futures.

**Article 15**  
Where the enterprise’s legal representative or the vice general manager in charge of futures business changes, the enterprise shall immediately notify all relevant parties. From the date of such change, the former legal representative or the former vice general manager in charge of futures business shall no longer have the power to issue authorization letters.

**Article 16**  
Where the enterprise’s hedging personnel change, the enterprise shall immediately notify all relevant parties. From the date of such change, the former hedging personnel shall no longer enjoy any of the rights previously granted.

**Article 17**  
State-owned cotton-related enterprises shall establish a reporting system for hedging operations. Relevant personnel shall regularly report their work to the leader in charge of futures business and to the general manager.

**Article 18**  
Traders shall regularly report to the enterprise’s leader in charge of futures business on the status of new positions, current positions, planned opening and closing of positions, and basic market information.

**Article 19**  
Risk management personnel shall regularly submit written reports to the enterprise’s leader in charge of futures business on position risk status, margin utilization, cumulative realized profit and loss from settlements, and the implementation of the hedging plan. The leader in charge shall review and sign the report and return it to the risk management personnel.

**Article 20**  
Fund transfer personnel shall regularly report to the financial leader on settlement profit and loss, position risk, and margin utilization, and shall at the same time communicate such information to the risk management personnel and the leader in charge of futures business.

**Article 21**  
State-owned cotton-related enterprises shall establish a records management system. Business files such as hedging plans, original trading documents, and settlement documents shall be kept for at least ten years. Account-opening documents, authorization documents, and other relevant archives shall be kept for at least fifteen years.

**Article 22**  
State-owned cotton-related enterprises shall establish a confidentiality system. Personnel related to futures business shall comply with the enterprise’s confidentiality rules and shall sign confidentiality agreements with the enterprise.  
Without permission, personnel related to futures business shall not disclose the enterprise’s hedging plans, trading conditions, settlement conditions, funding status, etc.

**Article 23**  
According to business needs, state-owned cotton-related enterprises shall implement a hierarchical authorization management system for the computer systems used for hedging operations. When IT administrators change positions or leave their posts, passwords shall be changed in a timely manner.

## Chapter IV  Risk Management System

**Article 24**  
When conducting futures hedging operations, state-owned cotton-related enterprises shall establish a strict and effective risk management system, specifying internal risk reporting systems, risk-handling procedures, and so forth. By adopting ex-ante, in-process, and ex-post risk control measures, they shall prevent, detect, and defuse risks.

**Article 25**  
State-owned cotton-related enterprises shall effectively segregate the posts and personnel performing the functions of trading, risk management, and settlement (fund transfers), so as to ensure mutual supervision and checks. Enterprises with the requisite conditions may establish dedicated trading and risk management departments for hedging operations; fund transfers and financial risk control shall be organized and implemented by the enterprise’s finance department.

**Article 26**  
State-owned cotton-related enterprises shall establish risk management posts independent of trading and settlement, staffed with appropriate personnel. Their principal responsibilities shall include:  
(1) Participating in the formulation of the enterprise’s risk management systems and procedures for hedging;  
(2) Supervising the implementation of hedging operations;  
(3) Verifying whether traders’ conduct conforms to the hedging plan and specific trading schemes;  
(4) Monitoring and assessing the risk status of futures positions to ensure the normal conduct of hedging operations;  
(5) Identifying, reporting, and handling risk incidents in accordance with procedures.

**Article 27**  
State-owned cotton-related enterprises shall strictly control the total size of position openings and the holding periods for futures positions. The opening and closing of futures positions shall match the quantity and timing of the physical assets being hedged.

**Article 28**  
Where a state-owned cotton-related enterprise becomes involved in judicial proceedings, it shall assess the impact on ongoing futures transactions and take corresponding measures to guard against legal risks.

**Article 29**  
Leaders in charge of the enterprise’s spot, futures, and finance functions shall regularly study and discuss risk management issues arising in hedging operations.

**Article 30**  
The authority that approves hedging operations shall, from time to time, inspect the participation of state-owned cotton-related enterprises in hedging transactions, the movement of funds, and other related circumstances, and may require enterprises to provide relevant materials. The futures brokerage company serving the enterprise shall actively assist the approving authority in risk prevention.

## Chapter V  Hedging Plans and Business Procedures

**Article 31**  
The hedging plan shall be jointly formulated by the enterprise’s spot department and futures business department based on the volume and timing of the enterprise’s spot business. The hedging plan formulated by the enterprise shall bear the comments and signature of the leader in charge of futures business and be submitted to the enterprise’s legal representative for approval.

**Article 32**  
A hedging plan shall specify the futures brokerage company proposed to be selected; the spot variety, quantity, months, and position side to be hedged; and indicate the required futures margin or financial support and other matters.

**Article 33**  
A hedging plan shall implement the following principles:  
(1) The underlying of the hedged futures variety shall be consistent with the products handled in the enterprise’s spot operations or the raw materials it requires;  
(2) The futures position size shall not exceed the spot quantity hedged;  
(3) The futures position holding period shall be basically consistent with the holding period of the spot being hedged.

**Article 34**  
After a specific hedging scheme of a state-owned cotton-related enterprise is approved according to procedure, it shall be archived with both the spot department and the futures department.

**Article 35**  
Based on the hedging scheme, the state-owned cotton-related enterprise shall sign a contract with a futures brokerage company, open a corporate futures trading account, designate specific order-placers and fund transfer personnel, and transfer the required margin into the trading account.

**Article 36**  
A state-owned cotton-related enterprise shall open positions within the time prescribed by the futures exchange, and in accordance with the trading side and quota approved by the exchange.

**Article 37**  
After trades are executed and confirmed, traders shall promptly report to the relevant departments and responsible leaders. The report shall include the traded variety, time of execution, transaction price, quantity executed, and the direction and size of the position.

**Article 38**  
Hedging plans may be concluded by:  
(1) Closing out the futures positions; or  
(2) Making physical delivery.

> **Note:** These Guidelines were repealed by the *CSRC Announcement [2015] No. 9 — Announcement on the Publication of the “Decision on Repealing Certain Securities and Futures Rules” (Batch 13)*, issued on April 20, 2015.
