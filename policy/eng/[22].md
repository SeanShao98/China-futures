# Measures for the Administration of Futures Brokerage Companies  
(CSRC Order No. 7)

## Chapter I  General Provisions

**Article 1**  
To strengthen the supervision and administration of futures brokerage companies, protect the lawful rights and interests of futures investors, and promote the sound development of the futures market, these Measures are formulated pursuant to the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*.

**Article 2**  
These Measures apply to futures brokerage companies registered within the territory of the People’s Republic of China.

**Article 3**  
The China Securities Regulatory Commission (hereinafter, the “CSRC”) shall exercise supervision and administration over futures brokerage companies in accordance with the law.  
The CSRC’s regional securities regulatory offices, agencies, and resident commissioners’ offices (hereinafter, the “CSRC local offices”) shall, pursuant to these Measures and CSRC authorization, supervise and administer futures brokerage companies and their branches.

**Article 4**  
Futures brokerage companies shall join the China Futures Association (CFA).  
The CFA shall conduct self-regulatory administration of futures brokerage companies in accordance with these Measures and as authorized by the CSRC.

---

## Chapter II  Establishment, Changes, and Termination

**Article 5**  
In addition to the conditions set out in the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, the establishment of a futures brokerage company shall also meet the following requirements:  
(1) Senior management personnel with the required qualifications;  
(2) A corporate governance structure consistent with the modern enterprise system;  
(3) Other conditions prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 6**  
A futures brokerage company may apply to operate the following futures businesses:  
(1) Futures brokerage;  
(2) Futures consulting and training;  
(3) Other businesses approved by the CSRC.

**Article 7**  
A futures brokerage company may, as needed, apply to establish business departments, subsidiaries (branches), and other branches permitted by the CSRC.  
To apply to establish a business department, the company shall meet the following conditions:  
(1) No major violations or irregularities in the preceding year;  
(2) The proposed head and practitioners of the business department possess the required qualifications;  
(3) The company has complete management systems for the proposed business department;  
(4) The proposed business department has business premises and facilities suitable for brokerage operations;  
(5) Other conditions that the CSRC may require based on prudential regulation.  
The measures for administering subsidiaries and other branches shall be formulated separately by the CSRC.

**Article 8**  
A futures brokerage company shall not, under the name of a “representative office,” “liaison office,” etc., in effect establish a business department; shall not establish a business department through joint venture, cooperation, or consortium; and shall not contract out or lease a business department.

**Article 9**  
Applications for the establishment, merger, or division of a futures brokerage company shall be preliminarily reviewed by the CSRC local office and submitted to the CSRC for approval.  
The establishment of a business department shall be approved by the CSRC local office where the department is to be located and filed with the CSRC.

**Article 10**  
For the changes listed in Items (1), (2), (4), (5), and (6) of Article 26 of the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, and for changes of any shareholder that holds more than 10% equity in a futures brokerage company or has de facto control over it, approval shall be granted by the CSRC local office and filed with the CSRC. However, where a shareholder that will hold more than 10% equity or de facto control is to be changed, that shareholder’s qualification shall first be approved by the CSRC.

**Article 11**  
Where a futures brokerage company undergoes any of the following changes, it shall, within 10 working days after the change, file with the CSRC local office:  
(1) Amendments to the articles of association;  
(2) Changes involving equity of 10% or less;  
(3) Other changes as prescribed by the CSRC.  
If any of the above changes is inconsistent with laws, regulations, rules, or policy provisions, the CSRC local office has the authority to require the company to rectify.

**Article 12**  
Where a futures brokerage company is dissolved, a liquidation group shall be formed pursuant to law to carry out liquidation. During liquidation, the liquidation group shall formulate a liquidation plan, clean up property, prepare balance sheets and property inventories, handle claims and debts, settle taxes, and dispose of remaining property.  
The CSRC local office shall supervise the liquidation group’s handling of investors’ positions and margins.

## Chapter III  Basic Rules for Futures Brokerage Business

**Article 13**  
A futures brokerage company shall adhere to the principle of good faith, execute investors’ mandates with appropriate skills, due care, and diligence, and safeguard investors’ lawful rights and interests.

**Article 14**  
A futures brokerage company shall consciously avoid conflicts of interest with investors; where they cannot be avoided, the company shall ensure investors are treated fairly.

**Article 15**  
A futures brokerage company shall explain the risks of futures trading to investors and keep at its business premises relevant futures laws and regulations, exchange trading rules, and its brokerage business rules and implementing details for investors to inspect.

**Article 16**  
Unless otherwise provided by the CSRC, an investor opening an account must hold a PRC identity document (for natural persons) or valid certificates evidencing PRC legal-person status or other economic-organization status.

**Article 17**  
Before opening an account for an investor, the company shall present the *Futures Trading Risk Disclosure Statement*, obtain the investor’s signature acknowledging understanding, and sign a written *Futures Brokerage Agreement*. The company shall not open an account for any investor who has not signed a written Futures Brokerage Agreement.  
The format and content of the Risk Disclosure Statement are uniformly prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 18**  
In the brokerage agreement, the investor shall specify the agent(s) authorized to place orders, transfer funds, and handle other related matters, agree on the means of contact and order placement, and deposit the agent’s specimen signature.

**Article 19**  
Under any of the following circumstances, the futures brokerage company may terminate the brokerage agreement with the investor, settle the investor’s account, and close it:  
(1) The natural-person investor dies or loses civil capacity, or the legal-person investor is terminated;  
(2) The investor is declared by a people’s court to enter bankruptcy proceedings;  
(3) Circumstances agreed between the company and the investor that do not violate relevant laws and regulations;  
(4) Other circumstances recognized by the CSRC.

**Article 20**  
An investor may terminate the entrustment relationship with the futures brokerage company by closing the account and completing the required procedures.

**Article 21**  
In addition to the circumstances stipulated in Article 30 of the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*, the following entities and individuals shall not engage in futures trading:  
(1) Natural persons without or with limited civil capacity;  
(2) Staff of the China Futures Association, futures exchanges, or futures brokerage companies and their spouses.

**Article 22**  
Personnel engaged in futures business at a futures brokerage company shall hold the qualification for futures practitioners.  
Advertising and promotional materials released by a futures brokerage company shall be filed with the CSRC local office.

**Article 23**  
A futures brokerage company shall assign trading codes to investors in accordance with exchange coding rules and file them with the exchange; cancellation of trading codes shall also be filed with the exchange.

**Article 24**  
A futures brokerage company shall establish investor account-opening files, which shall be kept confidential except as required by law for inspection; the company shall also establish an error-order log to record the handling of erroneous orders.

**Article 25**  
A futures brokerage company shall establish and improve an investor complaint handling system, and archive investors’ complaints and the results of handling.

**Article 26**  
A futures brokerage company may, in the brokerage agreement, agree on trading risk-control conditions and corresponding measures.

**Article 27**  
A futures brokerage company shall open a dedicated investor margin account at a clearing bank designated by the exchange, to hold investors’ margins separately from the company’s own funds.  
Transfers of investor margins by a futures brokerage company must meet one of the following conditions:  
(1) Payment of margin balances pursuant to the investor’s instructions;  
(2) Depositing margin or settlement differences with the exchange on behalf of the investor;  
(3) Brokerage commissions, taxes, and other fees payable by the investor to the company;  
(4) Conditions agreed by both parties that do not violate laws and regulations;  
(5) Other conditions recognized by the CSRC.

**Article 28**  
Investors may place trading orders in writing, by telephone, by computer, or via online entrustment.  
For written orders, investors shall fill out written order tickets; for telephone orders, the company must record the calls; for computer, online, or other methods, the company shall retain records sufficient to prove the order contents.  
“Online entrustment” refers to an order-placement method whereby the company provides, via the internet, services for investors who hold accounts at the company to submit futures trading orders.

**Article 29**  
Where a company provides online entrustment services, it shall establish a risk management system for online trading and give special risk warnings to investors regarding online trading.

**Article 30**  
A futures brokerage company shall transmit investors’ orders according to the principle of time priority.

**Article 31**  
After the close of each trading day, a futures brokerage company shall prepare trade settlement reports for investors. Investors are entitled to learn the contents of such reports at the time and in the manner agreed in the brokerage agreement.

**Article 32**  
If an investor has objections to the contents of a trade settlement report, a written objection shall be submitted to the company within the time agreed in the brokerage agreement; where there is no objection, the investor shall confirm in the agreed manner. If the investor neither confirms nor raises objections within the agreed time, it shall be deemed a confirmation of the report.  
Where objections exist, the company shall verify within the time agreed in the brokerage agreement against original order records and trading records.  
An investor who fails to raise objections within the agreed objection period may not raise them thereafter.

**Article 33**  
Disputes over futures business between futures brokerage companies, or between futures brokerage companies and investors, may be submitted to the China Futures Association for mediation.

**Article 34**  
Where trading results do not conform to an investor’s order, or forced liquidation does not meet statutory or agreed conditions due to the company’s fault, the company shall, within the time agreed in the brokerage agreement, re-execute the investor’s order or restore the liquidated position, and compensate the direct losses thereby incurred.

**Article 35**  
A futures brokerage company shall compile investors’ order records and trade settlement records into monthly statements.

**Article 36**  
Account-opening data, order records, trade settlement records, and other business records shall be kept for at least five years.

## Chapter IV  Routine Supervision

**Article 37**  
*Futures Brokerage Business Licenses* and *Business Department Operating Licenses* are uniformly designed and printed by the CSRC.  
Forgery, alteration, leasing, lending, transfer, or sale of such licenses is prohibited.

**Article 38**  
Where a license (original or copy) is lost or seriously damaged, the company shall, within 30 days of discovery, declare it void in a CSRC-recognized newspaper and apply to the CSRC for reissuance with the announcement attached.

**Article 39**  
The contents of the business license of a futures brokerage company or its business department must be consistent with the corresponding license.

**Article 40**  
A futures brokerage company shall not cite the CSRC’s business permission as publicity or a guarantee of its operating capacity or financial soundness.

**Article 41**  
A futures brokerage company shall, at least once a year, publish in public media the names, addresses, telephone numbers, and names of the principal persons in charge of its lawful branches.

**Article 42**  
A futures brokerage company shall establish a risk management department or position and create and strictly implement risk management systems.

**Article 43**  
A futures brokerage company shall establish a compliance review department or position to periodically or ad hoc review the company’s financial and business status and its implementation of applicable laws, regulations, rules, policies, articles, and business rules, and prepare compliance review reports.

**Article 44**  
A futures brokerage company shall establish and improve a financial safety assessment and management system in accordance with CSRC provisions, and submit relevant materials as required by the CSRC.  
Financial/accounting functions and settlement functions must be separated into different departments.

**Article 45**  
Application or filing materials submitted by a futures brokerage company to the CSRC and its local offices must be true, accurate, and complete.

**Article 46**  
A futures brokerage company shall, each year, engage an accounting firm with appropriate qualifications to audit its operational and financial status and issue an audit report.  
The list of engaged qualified accounting firms shall be filed with the CSRC local office; if a company changes its engaged firm, it must report the change and explain the reasons to the CSRC local office within three working days after the change.

**Article 47**  
A futures brokerage company shall not provide guarantees to third parties, nor become an investor bearing unlimited liability.

**Article 48**  
The CSRC shall establish a supervisory reporting system to oversee the financial soundness of futures brokerage companies.

**Article 49**  
The CSRC and its local offices may inspect futures brokerage companies and their business departments at any time.

**Article 50**  
A CSRC local office may require a futures brokerage company—or may directly engage a qualified accounting firm—to conduct special audits or examinations. Costs of audits or examinations engaged by the company shall be borne by the company; those engaged by the CSRC local office shall be borne by the CSRC.

**Article 51**  
Under the CSRC’s arrangements, CSRC local offices are responsible for the annual inspections of futures brokerage companies and their business departments within their jurisdictions.  
The CSRC may, as needed for regulation, stipulate the conditions, forms, scope, and focus of each year’s annual inspection and supervise such inspections.  
The format of the annual inspection report, inspection marks, and inspection seals shall be uniformly prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 52**  
Where a company or its business department passes the annual inspection, the CSRC shall affix an annual inspection mark or stamp to its *Futures Brokerage Business License* or *Business Department Operating License*.  
Where the conditions for annual inspection are not met, the CSRC shall order suspension for rectification or revoke the company’s or business department’s licenses.

**Article 53**  
Where a futures brokerage company is suspected of serious violations, the CSRC may, during investigation, suspend part of its futures business, and impose corresponding measures based on the investigation’s conclusions.

**Article 54**  
The CSRC may establish, as needed, a special Futures Investor Protection Fund to compensate investors for margin losses when a futures brokerage company encounters a margin-refund crisis.  
The sources, administration, and use of the Fund shall be formulated separately by the CSRC.

## Chapter V  Special Procedures for Margin-Refund Crises

**Article 55**  
Where a futures brokerage company has experienced or may experience an investor margin-refund crisis that seriously impairs investors’ interests, the CSRC has the authority to decide on special handling.  
A special handling decision by the CSRC shall specify:  
(1) The name of the company subject to special handling;  
(2) The reasons for special handling;  
(3) The period of special handling.

**Article 56**  
Upon entry into special handling, the shareholders of the futures brokerage company shall organize a special handling working group. Where necessary, the CSRC local office may dispatch representatives or engage intermediaries to participate.  
Special handling work shall be subject to the supervision of the CSRC local office.

**Article 57**  
Depending on the circumstances, the special handling working group may take the following measures:  
(1) Verify investors’ positions, margin balances, and the causes of the margin-refund crisis;  
(2) Dispose of the company’s assets and recover due receivables;  
(3) Recover misappropriated investor margins;  
(4) Decide on plans for handling investors’ positions and margins;  
(5) Formulate and implement the company’s cleanup and rectification plan;  
(6) Other measures deemed necessary by the working group.

**Article 58**  
If, during special handling, the working group deems liquidation necessary, it shall, upon approval by the shareholders’ meeting, submit the matter to the CSRC for approval.

**Article 59**  
Special handling shall terminate upon any of the following:  
(1) Expiration of the specified or extended period;  
(2) Restoration of the company’s normal operating capacity before the period expires;  
(3) Merger of the company or declaration of bankruptcy before the period expires.

## Chapter VI  Penalties

**Article 60**  
Where a futures brokerage company commits any of the following violations of these Measures, penalties shall be imposed pursuant to Article 59 of the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*:  
(1) Accepting futures trading orders from investors who have not completed account-opening procedures;  
(2) Inducing investors to trade through false publicity;  
(3) Failing to perform reporting or filing obligations as required;  
(4) Failing to meet CSRC financial management requirements after a prescribed rectification period;  
(5) Application materials containing false statements or major omissions;  
(6) Establishing a business department by way of joint venture, cooperation, or consortium;  
(7) Contracting out or leasing a business department.
