### Chapter I  General Provisions

**Article 1**  These Regulations are formulated to standardize futures-trading conduct, strengthen supervision and administration of futures trading, maintain order in the futures market, prevent risks, and protect the lawful rights and interests of all parties to futures trading as well as the public interest.

**Article 2**  Any engagement in futures trading and related activities must comply with these Regulations.

**Article 3**  Futures trading shall follow the principles of openness, fairness, impartiality, and good faith. Fraud, insider trading, price manipulation, and other illegal acts are prohibited.

**Article 4**  Futures trading must be conducted on a futures exchange. Off-exchange (over-the-counter) futures trading without going through a futures exchange is prohibited.

**Article 5**  The China Securities Regulatory Commission (hereinafter, the **CSRC**) shall exercise centralized and unified supervision and administration over the futures market.

---

### Chapter II  Futures Exchanges

**Article 6**  The establishment of a futures exchange shall be subject to approval by the CSRC.  
Without CSRC approval, no entity or individual may establish a futures exchange or a de facto futures exchange.

**Article 7**  A futures exchange is a non-profit organization that implements self-regulation pursuant to its articles of association. A futures exchange shall bear civil liability with all of its property.

**Article 8**  Members of a futures exchange shall be enterprise legal persons registered within the territory of the People’s Republic of China. Membership is subject to approval by the exchange and the payment of a membership fee.  
Exchange members consist of **futures-brokerage members** and **non-broker members**.

**Article 9**  A futures exchange shall establish a board of directors. The chair and vice-chairs shall be nominated by the CSRC and elected by the board.  
A futures exchange shall have a general manager and deputy general managers appointed and removed by the CSRC. The general manager is the legal representative of the exchange.

**Article 10**  Persons who fall under the circumstances set forth in Article 101 of the Securities Law or other circumstances prescribed by the CSRC may not serve as senior managers or financial and accounting personnel of a futures exchange.

**Article 11**  Exchange staff shall faithfully perform their duties; they may not, in any manner, conduct futures trading for themselves, nor may they disclose inside information or use it for unlawful gain.

**Article 12**  Exchange staff shall recuse themselves from duties where there is a conflict of interest involving themselves or their relatives.

**Article 13**  Exchange staff may not take positions with a member unit of the same exchange during their term of office or within one year after leaving the exchange.  
State civil servants may not hold positions in futures exchanges.

**Article 14**  A futures exchange shall perform the following functions:  
(1) Provide the venue, facilities, and services for futures trading;  
(2) Design futures contracts and arrange for their listing;  
(3) Organize and supervise trading, clearing, and delivery;  
(4) Ensure the performance of futures contracts;  
(5) Establish and enforce the risk-management systems specified in Article 35 of these Regulations;  
(6) Other functions prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 15**  A futures exchange may not engage in trust investment, stock trading, investment in non-self-use real estate, or other businesses unrelated to its functions.  
A futures exchange is prohibited from directly or indirectly participating in futures trading.

**Article 16**  When abnormal market conditions arise, a futures exchange may, within the scope of authority and procedures set out in its articles of association, adopt the following emergency measures and shall immediately report to the CSRC:  
(1) Increase margin requirements;  
(2) Adjust daily price-limit ranges;  
(3) Limit the maximum position size of members or clients;  
(4) Temporarily suspend trading;  
(5) Adopt other emergency measures.  
“Abnormal market conditions” refer to conduct that manipulates the market and seriously distorts price formation during trading, force majeure events, and other circumstances prescribed by the CSRC.  
Once abnormal conditions cease, the exchange shall promptly lift the emergency measures.

**Article 17**  A futures exchange shall obtain CSRC approval for any of the following:  
(1) Formulating or amending its articles of association or business rules;  
(2) Listing, suspending, delisting, or relisting a futures product;  
(3) Listing, amending, or terminating a futures contract;  
(4) Other matters prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 18**  The exchange’s income shall be managed and used in accordance with state provisions and may not be distributed to members or diverted to other uses.  
After paying public-welfare funds in accordance with state provisions, post-tax income shall be transferred in full to the exchange’s reserve fund for covering losses in subsequent years.

**Article 19**  The merger or division of a futures exchange shall be subject to CSRC approval.

**Article 20**  A futures exchange shall be dissolved upon any of the following:  
(1) Its operating term under the articles expires and the general meeting of members resolves not to extend it;  
(2) The general meeting of members resolves to dissolve;  
(3) The CSRC decides to close it.  
Dissolution under subparagraphs (1) and (2) above shall be subject to CSRC approval.

### Chapter III  Futures Brokerage Companies

**Article 21**  The establishment of a futures brokerage company shall comply with the Company Law and meet the following conditions:  
(1) Minimum registered capital of RMB 30 million;  
(2) Principal managers and business personnel must possess futures-practitioner qualifications;  
(3) Fixed business premises and qualified trading facilities;  
(4) Sound internal management systems;  
(5) Other conditions prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 22**  To establish a futures brokerage company, CSRC approval must be obtained and a futures-brokerage business license issued by the CSRC; registration shall be completed with the State Administration for Industry and Commerce.  
Without CSRC approval, no entity or individual may engage in futures-brokerage business, nor may they use “futures brokerage,” “futures agency,” or similar wording in their names.

**Article 23**  A futures brokerage company may, as needed, establish business departments as branch offices. Establishment shall meet CSRC requirements, be approved by the CSRC, obtain an operating permit from the CSRC, and be registered with the State Administration for Industry and Commerce.  
A business department shall conduct business within the authorization of the brokerage company; civil liability shall be borne by the brokerage company.

**Article 24**  A futures brokerage company shall, upon client authorization, conduct futures trading in its own name for the client; the trading results shall be borne by the client.

**Article 25**  Except for accepting client orders to buy and sell exchange-listed futures contracts and providing related clearing, delivery, and services, a futures brokerage company may not engage in other businesses.  
A futures brokerage company may not engage in, or in disguised form engage in, proprietary futures trading.

**Article 26**  A futures brokerage company shall obtain CSRC approval and complete change registration with the State Administration for Industry and Commerce for any of the following:  
(1) Changing its legal representative;  
(2) Changing registered capital;  
(3) Changing shareholders or equity structure;  
(4) Changing domicile or business premises;  
(5) Changing or terminating a business department;  
(6) Other circumstances prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 27**  When a futures brokerage company dissolves due to any of the following, it shall settle entrusted business and lawfully return client margins:  
(1) Upon expiration of its operating term, the shareholders’ meeting resolves not to extend;  
(2) The shareholders’ meeting resolves to dissolve;  
(3) Dissolution due to merger or division;  
(4) Bankruptcy;  
(5) Closure decided by the CSRC.  
Dissolution under subparagraphs (1), (2), and (3) above shall be subject to CSRC approval.  
Upon dissolution, the company shall cancel its registration with the State Administration for Industry and Commerce.

### Chapter IV  Basic Rules of Futures Trading

**Article 28**  Participants in exchange trading must be members of the futures exchange. A brokerage member may only accept client orders to conduct brokerage business; a non-broker member may only conduct proprietary trading.  
Members shall appoint floor representatives to enter the trading floor. A floor representative may accept only the trading instructions of his or her own member unit and may not accept instructions from other entities or individuals, provide advice to them, or trade for personal account.

**Article 29**  Before accepting a client’s order to trade futures, a brokerage company shall first present the **Risk Disclosure Statement** to the client; after the client’s signature confirmation, a written contract shall be signed.  
A brokerage company may not guarantee profits to clients, agree to share profits or losses with clients, accept orders placed in an individual’s name by a company, enterprise, or other economic organization, or re-delegate entrusted business or accept re-delegated business.

**Article 30**  The following entities and individuals may not engage in futures trading, and brokerage companies may not accept their orders:  
(1) Financial institutions, public institutions, and state organs;  
(2) CSRC staff;  
(3) Persons barred from the futures market;  
(4) Entities that cannot provide account-opening documents;  
(5) Other entities and individuals prohibited by the CSRC.

**Article 31**  Clients may issue trading instructions to brokerage companies in writing, by telephone, or by other methods prescribed by the CSRC.  
Client instructions shall be clear and complete.

**Article 32**  A brokerage company shall execute futures trades based on client instructions. It may not execute trades without client authorization or beyond the scope of the client’s authorization.

**Article 33**  Market quotations provided by a brokerage company to clients shall be true and accurate; it may not conceal material matters or induce clients to place orders by improper means.

**Article 34**  A futures exchange shall promptly disclose, for listed contracts, information such as trading volume, transaction prices, open interest, high and low prices, opening and closing prices, and other information that should be disclosed, and shall ensure the truthfulness and accuracy of such information. An exchange may not publish price forecasts.

**Article 35**  A futures exchange shall, in accordance with state provisions, establish and improve the following risk-management systems:  
(1) Margin system;  
(2) Mark-to-market (daily settlement) system;  
(3) Daily price-limit system;  
(4) Position limits and large-trader reporting;  
(5) Risk reserve fund system;  
(6) Other systems prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 36**  The margin system shall be strictly implemented. Margins collected by an exchange from members, and by a brokerage company from clients, may not be lower than CSRC standards and shall be segregated from proprietary funds and kept in special accounts.  
Margins collected by the exchange from members belong to the members; except for use in members’ trade settlement, diversion is strictly prohibited.  
Margins collected by a brokerage company from clients belong to the clients; except as required by CSRC provisions for lodging margin with the exchange and for trade settlement on behalf of clients, diversion is strictly prohibited.  
A brokerage company shall open a separate special account and set a unique trading code for each client; mixed-code trading is prohibited.

**Article 37**  Exchanges and brokerage companies shall accrue, manage, and use risk reserve funds in accordance with provisions of the CSRC and the Ministry of Finance; embezzlement is prohibited.

**Article 38**  Transaction fees charged by exchanges to members and by brokerage companies to clients shall be implemented pursuant to unified provisions of the competent departments under the State Council.

**Article 39**  Futures trading shall adopt centralized competitive bidding and the matching principle of price priority and time priority.

**Article 40**  Clearing of futures trades shall be uniformly organized by the exchange.  
The exchange shall implement a daily settlement system and notify members of the day’s settlement results after the market closes.  
Brokerage companies shall clear with clients based on the exchange’s settlement results and notify clients in a timely manner.

**Article 41**  Where a member’s margin is insufficient, the member must make a margin call. If the member fails to replenish margin within the time uniformly prescribed by the exchange, the exchange shall forcibly close out the member’s positions, and relevant expenses and losses shall be borne by the member.  
Where a client’s margin is insufficient and the client fails to replenish within the time uniformly prescribed by the brokerage company, the brokerage company shall forcibly close out the client’s positions, and relevant expenses and losses shall be borne by the client.

**Article 42**  Delivery in futures trading shall be uniformly organized by the exchange. An exchange may not restrict the total quantity of physical delivery.  
Delivery warehouses shall be designated by the exchange. The exchange shall enter into agreements with delivery warehouses to clarify mutual rights and obligations.

**Article 43**  A delivery warehouse may not:  
(1) Issue false warehouse receipts;  
(2) Restrict, in violation of exchange business rules, the inbound or outbound movement of deliverable commodities;  
(3) Disclose trade secrets related to futures trading;  
(4) Participate in futures trading;  
(5) Engage in other conduct prohibited by the CSRC.

**Article 44**  In case of default by a member, the member’s margin shall first be used to bear default liability; if insufficient, the exchange shall bear such liability with its risk reserve fund and proprietary funds, thereby obtaining a corresponding right of recourse against the member.  
In case of a client default, the brokerage company shall act mutatis mutandis in accordance with the preceding paragraph.

**Article 45**  Exchanges and brokerage companies shall ensure the completeness and security of trading, clearing, and delivery records.

**Article 46**  No entity or individual may fabricate or disseminate rumors concerning futures trading, collude maliciously, engage in concerted buying or selling, or otherwise manipulate futures prices.

**Article 47**  No entity or individual may use credit funds or fiscal funds for futures trading.  
Financial institutions may not provide financing or guarantees for futures trading.

**Article 48**  State-owned enterprises and enterprises in which state-owned assets hold a controlling or dominant position may engage in futures trading only for hedging purposes and shall comply with the following:  
(1) Futures varieties traded shall be limited to their own products or raw materials required for production and operation;  
(2) The total volume of futures trading shall be commensurate with their contemporaneous spot trading volume;  
(3) Other provisions of the CSRC.  
Enterprises under the preceding paragraph shall submit a document signed by the legal representative when conducting hedging business, and the exchange or brokerage company shall review and consent in accordance with the preceding provisions.

**Article 49**  Without approval, no entity or individual may directly or indirectly engage in overseas futures trading. Where hedging on overseas futures markets is genuinely required, the CSRC shall, together with relevant departments under the State Council, conduct examination and, upon approval by the State Council, issue an overseas futures business license.  
Futures brokerage companies are prohibited from engaging in overseas futures trading.

### Chapter V  Supervision and Administration

**Article 50**  Exchanges and brokerage companies shall periodically submit financial statements, relevant materials, and audit reports to the CSRC.

**Article 51**  The CSRC may at any time inspect the business and financial conditions of exchanges and brokerage companies; it is entitled to require exchanges and brokerage companies to provide relevant materials, and to require exchanges to provide information on members and brokerage companies to provide information on clients. When necessary, it may inspect the futures-related business and financial conditions of members and clients.  
Where suspected violations are discovered during inspection, the CSRC may obtain and seal up relevant documents and materials, and shall make a timely disposition within the prescribed period.  
CSRC staff shall present valid credentials during inspections.

**Article 52**  The CSRC has the power to inquire into and investigate entities and individuals suspected of futures violations; it may inquire into unit deposit accounts opened by exchanges, brokerage companies, members, and clients at commercial banks or other financial institutions; where there is evidence of transferring or concealing illicit funds, it may apply to judicial organs for freezing. Relevant entities and individuals shall provide support and cooperation.

**Article 53**  When abnormal market conditions arise, the CSRC may adopt necessary risk-disposal measures.

**Article 54**  The CSRC shall implement a qualification system for senior managers of exchanges and brokerage companies and for other futures practitioners.

**Article 55**  Exchanges shall, in accordance with these Regulations and CSRC provisions, establish and improve rules, strengthen risk control over exchange trading activities, and strengthen supervision and administration over members and exchange staff.  
Brokerage companies shall establish and improve internal business management rules, strengthen the review of client creditworthiness, and, pursuant to exchange requirements, report lists and trading conditions of large traders to the exchange.

**Article 56**  Upon the departure of a general manager of an exchange, the exchange shall engage an intermediary institution qualified for the relevant audit to conduct an exit audit. Where the CSRC deems it necessary, it may designate an intermediary institution to conduct such audit.

### Chapter VI  Penalties

**Article 57**  Where a futures exchange commits any of the following, it shall be ordered to make corrections, given a warning, and have illegal gains confiscated:  
(1) Admitting members in violation of provisions;  
(2) Charging transaction fees in violation of provisions;  
(3) Using or distributing income in violation of provisions;  
(4) Failing to disclose information as required;  
(5) Failing to fulfill reporting obligations to the CSRC as required;  
(6) Failing to submit documents or materials to the CSRC as required;  
(7) Failing to accrue, manage, or use risk reserve funds as required;  
(8) Restricting the total quantity of physical delivery;  
(9) Employing unqualified futures practitioners;  
(10) Other violations of CSRC provisions.  
For the foregoing, the persons directly in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given disciplinary sanctions and may be fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 100,000.  
Where subparagraph (2) applies, the exchange shall be ordered to return overcharged transaction fees.

**Article 58**  Where a futures exchange commits any of the following, it shall be ordered to correct, given a warning, have illegal gains confiscated, and be fined one to five times the illegal gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 100,000, a fine between RMB 100,000 and RMB 500,000 shall be imposed; where circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend business for rectification:  
(1) Formulating or amending its articles or business rules without approval;  
(2) Listing, suspending, delisting, or relisting a futures product without approval;  
(3) Listing, amending, or terminating a futures contract without approval;  
(4) Allowing members to trade when margins are insufficient;  
(5) Directly or indirectly participating in futures trading, or engaging in unrelated business;  
(6) Collecting margin in violation of provisions or misappropriating margin;  
(7) Forging, altering, or failing to preserve trading, clearing, and delivery records as required;  
(8) Failing to establish or implement price limits, position limits, and large-trader reporting;  
(9) Refusing or obstructing CSRC supervision and inspection;  
(10) Other violations of CSRC provisions.  
The persons directly in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given disciplinary sanctions and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 100,000.

**Article 59**  Where a futures brokerage company commits any of the following, it shall be ordered to correct, given a warning, have illegal gains confiscated, and be fined one to three times the illegal gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 100,000, a fine between RMB 100,000 and RMB 300,000 shall be imposed; where circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend business for rectification or have its futures-brokerage license revoked:  
(1) Accepting orders from entities or individuals who do not meet the required conditions;  
(2) Accepting orders placed in an individual’s name by a company, enterprise, or other economic organization;  
(3) Re-delegating entrusted business or accepting re-delegated business;  
(4) Allowing clients to trade when margins are insufficient;  
(5) Engaging in proprietary trading or other business in violation of these Regulations;  
(6) Failing to submit documents or materials to the CSRC as required;  
(7) Failing to accrue, manage, or use risk reserve funds as required;  
(8) Forging, altering, or failing to preserve trading, clearing, and delivery records as required;  
(9) Employing unqualified futures practitioners;  
(10) Establishing a business department without approval;  
(11) Handling change matters without approval;  
(12) Forging, altering, leasing, lending, buying, or selling brokerage or operating licenses;  
(13) Conducting mixed-code trading in violation of provisions;  
(14) Refusing or obstructing CSRC supervision and inspection;  
(15) Other violations of CSRC provisions.  
The persons directly in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given disciplinary sanctions and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 50,000.

**Article 60**  Where a brokerage company commits any of the following fraudulent acts against clients, it shall be ordered to correct, given a warning, have illegal gains confiscated, and be fined one to five times the illegal gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 100,000, a fine between RMB 100,000 and RMB 500,000 shall be imposed; where circumstances are serious, it shall be ordered to suspend business for rectification or have its brokerage license revoked:  
(1) Failing to present the risk disclosure statement as required, guaranteeing profits to clients, or agreeing to share profits or bear losses with clients;  
(2) Executing trades without client authorization or beyond the scope of authorization;  
(3) Providing false market quotations or information, or using other improper means to induce clients to place orders;  
(4) Providing false trade confirmations to clients;  
(5) Failing to transmit client orders to the exchange;  
(6) Misappropriating client margin;  
(7) Other fraudulent conduct as prescribed by the CSRC.  
The persons directly in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given disciplinary sanctions and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 100,000; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued in accordance with law.  
Any entity or individual that fabricates and disseminates false information affecting futures trading and disrupts the market shall be punished by reference to the preceding two paragraphs; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued.

**Article 61**  Insiders possessing inside information on futures trading, or persons who illegally obtain such information, who, before the information that may materially affect futures prices is made public, use the information to trade futures or disclose it to others for trading, shall have illegal gains confiscated and be fined one to five times such gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 100,000, a fine between RMB 100,000 and RMB 500,000 shall be imposed; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued.

**Article 62**  Any entity or individual committing any of the following acts to manipulate futures prices shall be ordered to correct, have illegal gains confiscated, and be fined one to five times such gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 200,000, a fine between RMB 200,000 and RMB 1,000,000 shall be imposed; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued:  
(1) Acting alone or in collusion to use capital or position advantages, or information advantages, to engage in joint or continuous trading to manipulate prices;  
(2) Colluding intentionally to trade with each other at prearranged times, prices, and methods to affect prices or volumes;  
(3) Self-dealing (wash trades) to affect prices or volumes;  
(4) Hoarding physical commodities to influence market conditions;  
(5) Other price-manipulation acts prescribed by the CSRC.  
For entities committing the foregoing, the persons directly in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given disciplinary sanctions and fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 100,000.

**Article 63**  Where a delivery warehouse commits any act listed in Article 43, it shall be ordered to correct, given a warning, have illegal gains confiscated, and be fined one to five times such gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 100,000, a fine between RMB 100,000 and RMB 500,000 shall be imposed. Where circumstances are serious, the exchange shall suspend or revoke its delivery-warehouse qualification. The persons directly in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be given disciplinary sanctions.

**Article 64**  Where state-owned enterprises or enterprises in which state-owned assets hold a controlling or dominant position violate these Regulations in engaging in futures trading, or where any entity or individual uses credit or fiscal funds for futures trading, a warning shall be given, illegal gains confiscated, and a fine of one to five times such gains imposed; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 100,000, a fine between RMB 100,000 and RMB 500,000 shall be imposed; the persons directly in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be disciplined by demotion up to dismissal; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued.

**Article 65**  Any entity or individual that, in violation of these Regulations, engages in overseas futures trading without authorization shall be banned, have illegal gains confiscated, and be fined one to five times such gains; where there are no illegal gains or such gains are less than RMB 200,000, a fine between RMB 200,000 and RMB 1,000,000 shall be imposed; the persons directly in charge and other directly responsible personnel shall be fined between RMB 10,000 and RMB 100,000 and disciplined by demotion up to dismissal; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued.  
Any entity or individual that illegally establishes or de facto establishes a futures exchange or futures brokerage company, or engages in brokerage without authorization, shall be banned and punished in accordance with the preceding paragraph; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued.

**Article 66**  Where exchange staff, during their tenure or within one year after leaving the exchange, take positions with a member unit of the same exchange, or disclose state secrets or members’/clients’ trade secrets learned in the course of their duties, they shall be given disciplinary sanctions; where a crime is constituted, criminal liability shall be pursued.

**Article 67**  Where violations of these Regulations are serious, the CSRC may designate the relevant individuals, entities, or directly responsible personnel as persons barred from entering the futures market.

**Article 68**  Where CSRC staff disclose state secrets or members’/clients’ trade secrets learned in the course of their duties, or engage in malfeasance, dereliction of duty, abuse of power, or bribery, criminal liability shall be pursued where a crime is constituted; otherwise, administrative sanctions shall be imposed in accordance with law.

**Article 69**  Administrative penalties for exchanges and their members, brokerage companies and their clients, futures practitioners, and delivery warehouses shall be decided by the CSRC; administrative penalties for other entities or individuals shall be decided by the relevant authorities within their statutory powers.

### Chapter VII  Supplementary Provisions

**Article 70**  For the purposes of these Regulations:  
(1) “Futures trading” means centralized buying and selling of a futures contract on a futures exchange.  
(2) “Futures contract” means a standardized contract uniformly formulated by a futures exchange that specifies delivery of a certain quantity and quality of a commodity at a specified time and place in the future.  
(3) “Margin” means funds paid by a futures trader in accordance with prescribed standards for clearing and performance assurance.  
(4) “Clearing” means the settlement of gains and losses between trading parties based on settlement prices published by the exchange.  
(5) “Delivery” means the process whereby, upon contract maturity, open positions are closed by transferring ownership of the commodity specified in the contract pursuant to exchange rules and procedures.  
(6) “Offset (Close-out)” means the act of a trader buying or selling a futures contract with the same variety, quantity, and delivery month but in the opposite direction to close a futures position.  
(7) “Open interest” means the number of open (unclosed) contracts held by traders.  
(8) “Position limit” means the maximum open interest permitted by the exchange for a trader.  
(9) “Warehouse receipt” means the standardized delivery voucher issued by a delivery warehouse and recognized by the exchange.  
(10) “Trade matching” means the process by which an exchange’s electronic trading system pairs buy and sell orders.  
(11) “Daily price limit” means that, within a trading day, a futures contract’s price may not rise above or fall below a prescribed percentage; bids and offers beyond the limit are invalid and cannot be executed.  
(12) “Inside information” means non-public information that may materially affect futures prices, including: policies formulated by the CSRC and other relevant departments that may materially affect prices; exchange decisions that may materially affect prices; capital and trading movements of exchange members and clients; and other important information designated by the CSRC as having a significant impact on prices.  
(13) “Insiders possessing inside information” means persons who, due to managerial, supervisory, or professional positions, or as employees or professional advisors in the performance of duties, can access or obtain inside information, including: exchange senior managers such as the chair, vice-chairs, general manager, and deputy general managers, and other staff who can access inside information due to their positions; CSRC staff and staff of other relevant departments; and other persons prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 71**  These Regulations shall come into force on September 1, 1999.
