# Measures for the Administration of Futures Exchanges

(CSRC Futures [1999] No. 43)

---

# Chapter I  General Provisions

**Article 1**  
For the purpose of strengthening the administration of futures exchanges, clarifying their powers and responsibilities, preventing market risks, and maintaining the normal order of the futures market, these Measures are formulated in accordance with the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*.

**Article 2**  
These Measures apply to futures exchanges established within the territory of the People’s Republic of China.

**Article 3**  
A “futures exchange” as referred to in these Measures means a legal person established in accordance with the conditions prescribed by the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading* and these Measures, that is non-profit in nature, performs the functions stipulated in the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading* and these Measures, and implements self-regulation pursuant to its articles of association.

**Article 4**  
The China Securities Regulatory Commission (hereinafter, the “CSRC”) shall, in accordance with law, exercise centralized and unified supervision and administration over futures exchanges.

---

# Chapter II  Establishment, Change, and Termination

**Article 5**  
The establishment of a futures exchange shall be subject to the approval of the CSRC. Without such approval, no entity or individual may establish, or in a disguised form establish, a futures exchange.  
The name of a futures exchange shall include the wording “Commodity Exchange” or “Futures Exchange.” No other entity or individual may use the wording “Commodity Exchange,” “Futures Exchange,” or words of similar meaning.

**Article 6**  
The registered capital of a futures exchange shall be divided into equal shares as membership fees, to be subscribed by members.  
The equity of a futures exchange shall be jointly enjoyed by its members and, during the period of the exchange’s existence, may not be distributed to members from the accumulation thereof.

**Article 7**  
A futures exchange shall perform the following functions:  
(1) Provide venues, facilities, and related services for futures trading;  
(2) Formulate and implement the business rules of the exchange;  
(3) Design futures contracts and arrange for their listing;  
(4) Organize and supervise futures trading, settlement, and delivery;  
(5) Formulate and implement risk management systems and control market risks;  
(6) Ensure the performance of futures contracts;  
(7) Publish market information;  
(8) Supervise the futures business of members and inspect members’ illegal or irregular acts;  
(9) Supervise the futures business of designated delivery warehouses;  
(10) Other functions as prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 8**  
A futures exchange shall not engage in trust investment, stock trading, investment in non-self-use real estate, or other businesses unrelated to its functions; except for providing performance guarantees for the fulfillment of futures contracts, it shall not provide guarantees for other entities or individuals; nor shall it publish texts or materials forecasting futures prices.  
A futures exchange is prohibited from directly or indirectly participating in futures trading.

**Article 9**  
An applicant for establishing a futures exchange shall submit the following documents and materials to the CSRC:  
(1) An application letter;  
(2) Draft articles of association and trading rules;  
(3) The business plan of the futures exchange;  
(4) A list of prospective members of the exchange;  
(5) A list and résumés of candidates for the council (board of directors);  
(6) Supporting documents and explanatory materials regarding premises, equipment, and funds;  
(7) A list and résumés of proposed senior management personnel;  
(8) Other documents and materials as required by the CSRC.

**Article 10**  
The articles of association of a futures exchange shall specify the following matters:  
(1) Purpose of establishment and functions;  
(2) Name, domicile, and place of business;  
(3) Registered capital and the members’ capital contributions;  
(4) Term of operation;  
(5) Acquisition of membership;  
(6) Rights and obligations of members;  
(7) Establishment of organizational bodies, their powers, and rules of procedure;  
(8) Selection, appointment and removal of management personnel, and their duties;  
(9) Basic business rules;  
(10) Risk reserve fund administration system;  
(11) Financial accounting and internal audit systems;  
(12) Conditions and procedures for change and termination, and methods of liquidation;  
(13) Procedures for amending the articles of association;  
(14) Other matters that need to be provided for in the articles of association.

**Article 11**  
The trading rules of a futures exchange shall specify the following matters:  
(1) Trading venue and hours;  
(2) Listed products and futures contracts;  
(3) Procedures for suspension, resumption, and cancellation of futures contract trading;  
(4) Management system for the trading floor;  
(5) Rules for brokerage and proprietary trading;  
(6) Risk control system and procedures for handling abnormal trading situations;  
(7) Systems for futures settlement and delivery;  
(8) Systems for the administration and use of margin;  
(9) Methods for publishing futures trading information;  
(10) Violations and defaults and measures for handling them;  
(11) Methods for handling trading disputes;  
(12) Other matters that need to be specified in the trading rules.

**Article 12**  
Mergers and divisions of futures exchanges shall be subject to the examination and approval of the CSRC.  
A merger of futures exchanges may take the form of absorption or the establishment of a new exchange; the rights and obligations of the parties prior to the merger shall be succeeded to by the surviving or newly established futures exchange.  
Where a futures exchange is divided, its rights and obligations shall be succeeded to by the futures exchanges resulting from the division.

**Article 13**  
Without the approval of the CSRC, a futures exchange shall not establish branches or any other futures trading venues.

**Article 14**  
Where any of the following changes occurs, a futures exchange shall obtain the approval of the CSRC:  
(1) Change of name;  
(2) Change of domicile or place of business;  
(3) Other changes as prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 15**  
A futures exchange shall be terminated under any of the following circumstances:  
(1) The term of operation as provided in the articles of association expires and the members’ general assembly decides not to extend it;  
(2) The members’ general assembly decides to dissolve the exchange;  
(3) Dissolution due to merger or division;  
(4) The CSRC decides to close the exchange.  
Where a futures exchange is dissolved under items (1), (2), or (3) of the preceding paragraph, such dissolution shall be subject to the approval of the CSRC.  
Upon termination of a futures exchange, a liquidation group shall be formed to conduct liquidation. The liquidation plan formulated by the liquidation group shall be submitted to the CSRC for approval.

**Article 16**  
Where a futures exchange is terminated, the CSRC shall make a public announcement. Members of the futures exchange shall settle all futures trading business within 90 days from the date of announcement.

# Chapter III  Organizational Structure

**Article 17**  
A futures exchange shall establish a members’ general assembly and a council.

**Article 18**  
The members’ general assembly is the authority of the futures exchange and is composed of all members.  
The members’ general assembly shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Adopt the articles of association and trading rules of the exchange and draft amendments thereto;  
(2) Elect and remove member directors;  
(3) Deliberate on and approve the work reports of the council and the general manager;  
(4) Deliberate on and approve the budgets and final accounts of the exchange;  
(5) Deliberate on the use of the exchange’s risk reserve fund;  
(6) Decide on increases or decreases of the exchange’s registered capital;  
(7) Decide on matters relating to merger, division, dissolution, and liquidation of the exchange;  
(8) Decide on other major matters submitted by the council;  
(9) Other powers provided in the articles of association of the exchange.

**Article 19**  
The members’ general assembly shall be convened by the council and, in general, shall be held once a year.  
An extraordinary members’ meeting shall be convened under any of the following circumstances:  
(1) The number of member directors falls below the minimum number prescribed by these Measures or below two-thirds of the number specified in the articles of association;  
(2) A joint proposal by more than one-third of the members;  
(3) The council deems it necessary.

**Article 20**  
Meetings of the members’ general assembly shall be presided over by the chair of the council. Where the chair is, for special reasons, unable to perform duties, a vice-chair designated by the chair or another director shall preside. Members shall be informed of the matters to be deliberated no later than 30 days before the meeting is held. An extraordinary members’ meeting shall not adopt resolutions on matters not listed in the notice.

**Article 21**  
A meeting of the members’ general assembly shall be valid only if more than two-thirds of the members are present, and each member shall have one vote.  
Resolutions of the members’ general assembly shall be adopted by a vote of more than one-half of all members. Resolutions on merger, division, dissolution and liquidation of the exchange, or on increases or decreases of the exchange’s registered capital, shall be adopted by a vote of more than two-thirds of all members.

**Article 22**  
A member shall attend the members’ general assembly through its legal representative.  
If the legal representative is unable to attend, the member may authorize a proxy to attend the meeting. The proxy shall submit a power of attorney to the exchange and exercise voting rights within the scope of authorization.

**Article 23**  
Minutes shall be kept for voting matters at the members’ general assembly and shall be signed by the directors present at the meeting.  
Within three days after the end of the meeting, the exchange shall file all meeting documents with the CSRC for the record.

**Article 24**  
The council is the standing body of the members’ general assembly and is responsible to it.  
The council shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Convene meetings of the members’ general assembly and report on its work thereto;  
(2) Supervise the implementation of the resolutions of the members’ general assembly and the council;  
(3) Supervise the performance of duties by the general manager;  
(4) Draft the articles of association, trading rules, and amendments thereto, and submit them to the members’ general assembly for adoption;  
(5) Deliberate on the budgets and final accounts proposed by the general manager and submit them to the members’ general assembly for adoption;  
(6) Deliberate on plans for merger, division, dissolution, and liquidation of the exchange and submit them to the members’ general assembly for adoption;  
(7) Decide on the establishment of specialized committees;  
(8) Decide on the admission of members;  
(9) Decide on penalties for members with serious violations;  
(10) Decide on changes of the exchange;  
(11) Powers to take temporary disposal measures when circumstances under Article 44 arise;  
(12) Powers to take emergency measures under abnormal circumstances;  
(13) Review and approve implementing rules and measures formulated pursuant to the trading rules;  
(14) Review and approve the use and administration of the risk reserve fund;  
(15) Review and approve development plans and annual work plans proposed by the general manager;  
(16) Other powers provided in the articles of association and conferred by the members’ general assembly.  
The powers set forth in items (8) and (9) of the preceding paragraph may be delegated by the council to specialized committees.

**Article 25**  
The council shall be composed of 9 to 15 members, among whom non-member directors shall account for no less than one-third and no more than one-half of the total number of council members.  
Each director shall serve a term of three years, and no director may serve more than two consecutive terms.  
Member directors shall be elected by the members’ general assembly from candidates proposed by the council or jointly by one-fifth or more of the members. Non-member directors shall be appointed by the CSRC.

**Article 26**  
The council shall have one chair and one to two vice-chairs. The chair and vice-chairs shall be nominated by the CSRC and elected by the council.  
The chair shall not concurrently serve as the general manager.

**Article 27**  
The chair shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Preside over the members’ general assembly;  
(2) Preside over meetings of the council;  
(3) Preside over the day-to-day work of the council;  
(4) Organize and coordinate the work of specialized committees;  
(5) Inspect the implementation of council resolutions and report to the council.  
The vice-chairs shall assist the chair. Where the chair is temporarily unable to perform duties, a vice-chair designated by the chair or another director shall act in the chair’s stead.

**Article 28**  
Council meetings shall be held once every six months. All directors shall be notified at least 10 days prior to each meeting.  
An extraordinary council meeting shall be convened under any of the following circumstances:  
(1) Upon proposal by the CSRC;  
(2) Where the exchange declares the occurrence of an abnormal situation or circumstances under Article 44 arise;  
(3) Upon a joint proposal by more than one-third of the directors.  
For extraordinary meetings, the method and time limits for notice may be otherwise specified.

**Article 29**  
A council meeting shall be valid only if more than two-thirds of the directors are present; resolutions shall be adopted by a vote of more than one-half of all directors.  
Within three days after the end of the meeting, the council shall file the meeting resolutions and other meeting documents with the CSRC for the record.

**Article 30**  
Council meetings shall be attended by directors in person. A director who is unable to attend shall, in writing, entrust another director to attend on his or her behalf; the letter of authorization shall specify the scope of authorization. Each director may accept authorization from only one other director.  
Minutes shall be kept for voting matters at council meetings and signed by the directors present and by the recorder.

**Article 31**  
The council of a futures exchange may establish specialized committees such as supervision, trading, delivery, membership qualification review, mediation, and finance committees.  
The specific duties of specialized committees shall be determined by the council, and the committees shall be responsible to the council.

**Article 32**  
A futures exchange shall have one general manager and several deputy general managers. The general manager and deputy general managers shall be appointed or removed by the CSRC. The term of office of the general manager shall be three years and may not exceed two consecutive terms.  
The general manager is the legal representative of the futures exchange and shall serve ex officio as a director.

**Article 33**  
The general manager shall exercise the following powers:  
(1) Organize the implementation of the systems and resolutions adopted by the members’ general assembly and the council;  
(2) Preside over the day-to-day operations of the exchange;  
(3) Draft implementing rules and measures pursuant to the articles of association and trading rules;  
(4) Draft and implement approved development plans and annual work plans of the exchange;  
(5) Draft the budgets and final accounts of the exchange;  
(6) Draft plans for merger, division, dissolution, and liquidation of the exchange;  
(7) Draft plans for changes to the exchange;  
(8) Decide on the organizational set-up of the exchange and hire and dismiss staff;  
(9) Decide on staff remuneration and rewards and penalties;  
(10) Other powers provided in the articles of association or conferred by the council.  
Where the general manager is temporarily unable to perform duties, a deputy general manager designated by the general manager shall act in the general manager’s stead.

**Article 34**  
The chair of the council, the general manager, and the deputy general managers shall not concurrently hold positions as State civil servants.

**Article 35**  
The appointment and removal of middle-level managers of a futures exchange shall be filed with the CSRC for the record.

**Article 36**  
None of the following persons may serve as senior management personnel, financial and accounting personnel, or directors of a futures exchange:  
(1) Persons falling under any of the circumstances listed in Article 10 of the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*;  
(2) Practitioners of a futures brokerage company or other financial institution who were dismissed for illegal acts, for a period of five years from the date of dismissal;  
(3) State civil servants who were expelled from public office, for a period of five years from the date of expulsion;  
(4) Persons whom the CSRC has announced to be prohibited from entry into the securities and futures markets.

**Article 37**  
Where the general manager or deputy general managers of a futures exchange, during their term of office, commit acts that violate relevant state laws, regulations, rules, policies, or the exchange’s articles of association or business rules, or for other reasons are not suitable to continue serving, the CSRC shall remove them from office in accordance with procedures.

# Chapter IV  Administration of Members

**Article 38**  
Members of a futures exchange shall be corporate legal persons registered within the territory of the People’s Republic of China and shall have good credit standing.  
Members of a futures exchange are divided into futures brokerage company members and non–futures brokerage company members.

**Article 39**  
Acquisition of membership shall be subject to the approval of the futures exchange, and the membership fee shall be paid.

**Article 40**  
Transfer or succession of membership shall be subject to the approval of the futures exchange and the completion of relevant formalities.

**Article 41**  
A futures exchange shall formulate membership administration measures.  
Such measures shall include:  
(1) Conditions and procedures for acquiring membership;  
(2) Conditions and procedures for changing membership;  
(3) Supervision and administration of members;  
(4) Handling of members’ violations and defaults;  
(5) Other matters that need to be provided for in the membership administration measures.

**Article 42**  
Members shall enjoy the following rights:  
(1) Participate in the members’ general assembly and exercise rights to elect, to be elected, and to vote;  
(2) Exercise the right to appeal in accordance with the articles of association and trading rules of the exchange;  
(3) Engage in futures trading on the exchange;  
(4) Use the trading facilities provided by the exchange and obtain information and services related to futures trading;  
(5) Transfer membership as prescribed;  
(6) Jointly propose the convening of an extraordinary members’ meeting;  
(7) Other rights provided in the articles of association of the exchange.

**Article 43**  
Members shall perform the following obligations:  
(1) Comply with relevant state laws, regulations, rules, and policies;  
(2) Comply with the articles of association, business rules, and relevant decisions of the exchange;  
(3) Pay various fees as prescribed;  
(4) Implement resolutions of the members’ general assembly and the council;  
(5) Accept the business supervision of the exchange;  
(6) Other obligations provided in the articles of association of the exchange.

**Article 44**  
Where there is reason to believe that a member or client has violated the business rules of the exchange and is causing or is likely to cause a significant impact on the market, the exchange may, in order to prevent further expansion of the consequences of the violation, take the following temporary disposal measures against such member or client:  
(1) Restrict deposits;  
(2) Restrict withdrawals;  
(3) Restrict the opening of new positions;  
(4) Raise margin ratios;  
(5) Order position reduction within a specified time;  
(6) Enforce forced liquidation.  
The temporary disposal measures set forth in items (1), (2), and (3) of the preceding paragraph may be decided by the general manager of the exchange; other temporary disposal measures shall be decided by the council and filed with the CSRC for the record.  
When taking temporary disposal measures, the exchange shall notify the member or client in the manner prescribed by the exchange’s business rules and specify the grounds for such measures.

**Article 45**  
Members of a futures exchange shall use their trading seats in accordance with relevant provisions.  
It is strictly prohibited for members to lease, contract out, or otherwise permit other institutions or individuals to use all or part of their seats.

**Article 46**  
Members of a futures exchange shall accept the supervision and administration of the exchange.  
When exercising supervisory powers, the exchange may, within the scope of authority and procedures provided in its articles of association, trading rules, and implementing details, conduct investigations and collect evidence from members, and members shall cooperate.

# Chapter V  Basic Business Rules

**Article 47**  
Futures trading must be conducted through centralized bidding on the futures exchange, and over-the-counter transactions not matched through centralized bidding on the exchange are prohibited.  
Futures trading shall follow the principles of price priority and time priority in matching trades.

**Article 48**  
The transaction fees collected by the exchange through its members shall not exceed the standards specified in the futures contracts.

**Article 49**  
Margin collected by the exchange from its members shall be used to secure the performance of futures contracts.  
Margin is owned by the members. The exchange shall open a dedicated settlement account with its designated clearing bank to hold members’ margin funds on a segregated basis and shall not misappropriate such funds.

**Article 50**  
A futures exchange may stipulate the minimum balance of members’ margin in the dedicated settlement account. Where a member’s margin balance falls below the minimum stipulated by the exchange, the exchange shall notify the member in the manner and within the time limit prescribed by the business rules to provide additional margin, and the member shall make timely replenishment; if the member fails to do so within the time limit, the exchange shall enforce forced liquidation until the remaining margin reaches the prescribed minimum balance.  
Where an instruction for forced liquidation cannot be executed due to market reasons, the exchange shall not bear liability.  
Fees and losses arising from forced liquidation shall be borne by the member.

**Article 51**  
A futures exchange may stipulate the margin ratios to be collected from members, which shall not be lower than the ratios prescribed by the CSRC.  
Margin shall be paid in monetary funds. Offsetting margin with negotiable treasury bonds eligible for listing and circulation or with standard warehouse receipts shall comply with relevant provisions.  
Bank guarantees, bank deposit certificates, and custodial vouchers for treasury bonds shall not be used to offset margin.

**Article 52**  
A futures exchange shall allocate 20% of its fee income to a risk reserve fund. Where the balance of the risk reserve fund reaches ten times the registered capital of the exchange, further allocations may cease upon approval by the CSRC.  
The CSRC may, in light of the exchange’s business scale, development plan, and potential risks, decide whether to continue allocating to the risk reserve fund.  
The risk reserve fund shall be accounted for separately and held in a dedicated account.

**Article 53**  
Settlement of futures trading shall be organized in a unified manner by the exchange, and a daily mark-to-market settlement system shall be implemented.  
After the market closes each day, the exchange shall promptly notify members of the trading and settlement results. Members shall confirm the results within the prescribed time.

**Article 54**  
A futures exchange shall implement a client trading code system. Futures brokerage company members and clients must comply with the “one account, one code” requirement and shall not mix codes in trading.

**Article 55**  
A futures exchange shall implement a hedging position approval system.  
Where a non–futures brokerage company member or a client applies for hedging positions, it/he shall submit materials such as scope of business, operating performance, and spot purchase-and-sale contracts to the exchange; the exchange shall determine the hedging positions upon review.  
Where a client applies for hedging positions, the futures brokerage company member shall review the submitted materials and then submit them to the exchange for review.  
A non–futures brokerage company member or client shall submit materials truthfully when applying for hedging positions. Where such member or client intentionally conceals facts and fails to perform the obligation of truthful submission, or negligently fails to perform such obligation, the exchange shall have the right to cancel its hedging positions, and the futures brokerage company shall have the right to terminate the brokerage contract with such client; losses thereby caused shall be borne by the client.  
Hedging positions shall not be reused and may only be closed out within the prescribed period or settled by physical delivery.

**Article 56**  
A futures exchange shall implement a speculative position limit system, formulating separate maximum position limits for speculative positions held by members and clients in non-delivery months and in delivery months.  
Where a member or client holds positions exceeding the maximum position limit, the exchange shall have the right to enforce forced liquidation for the excess portion; fees and losses arising from forced liquidation shall be borne by the member, and profits shall be handled in accordance with relevant state provisions.  
Positions held by the same client at different futures brokerage company members shall be aggregated for calculation.

**Article 57**  
A futures exchange shall implement a large-position reporting system. When a member’s or client’s positions reach 80% of the maximum speculative position limit, the non–futures brokerage company member or the futures brokerage company on behalf of the client shall report relevant information to the exchange.  
The exchange may, in light of market risk conditions, adjust the position reporting standards.  
Positions held by the same client at different futures brokerage company members shall be aggregated for calculation.

**Article 58**  
When futures prices hit the up-limit or down-limit for several consecutive sessions in the same direction, the exchange may adopt measures such as adjusting the limit range, raising trading margin ratios, and reducing positions according to certain principles to defuse risks.  
The exchange shall adopt the measures under the preceding paragraph in accordance with the conditions and procedures prescribed in its trading rules and implementing details.

**Article 59**  
During futures trading, if any of the following circumstances occurs, the exchange may declare the occurrence of an abnormal situation and take emergency measures to defuse risks:  
(1) Trading cannot proceed normally due to force majeure such as earthquake, flood, or fire, or for reasons not attributable to the exchange such as computer system failures;  
(2) A member encounters a clearing or delivery crisis;  
(3) The circumstance described in Article 58 of these Measures occurs, and risks remain unresolved after corresponding measures have been taken;  
(4) Other circumstances specified in the exchange’s business rules.  
Where an abnormal situation under item (1) of the preceding paragraph occurs, the general manager of the exchange may take emergency measures such as delayed market opening or suspension of trading; where abnormal situations under items (2), (3), or (4) occur, the council may decide to take emergency measures such as delayed market opening, suspension of trading, adjusting the limit range, raising trading margin ratios, ordering position reduction within a specified time, enforcing forced liquidation, or restricting withdrawals.  
When taking emergency measures, the exchange shall notify members in the manner prescribed by its business rules.

**Article 60**  
Before declaring the occurrence of an abnormal situation and deciding to take emergency measures, the exchange must report to the CSRC.  
Under any of the following circumstances, the CSRC has the authority to veto:  
(1) The proposed emergency measures contravene relevant state laws, regulations, rules, policies, or the exchange’s articles of association, trading rules, and relevant implementing details;  
(2) The proposed emergency measures have not been adopted through the prescribed procedures;  
(3) The proposed emergency measures are not conducive to defusing futures market risks.

**Article 61**  
Where the exchange declares the occurrence of an abnormal situation and decides to suspend trading, the suspension shall not exceed three trading days, unless an extension is approved by the CSRC.

**Article 62**  
A futures exchange shall publish real-time quotations in an appropriate manner.  
Real-time quotations shall include the following:  
(1) Names of futures products and contract months;  
(2) Opening price, highest price, lowest price, latest price, bid price, bid size, ask price, ask size, closing price, settlement price, and the settlement price of the previous trading day;  
(3) Price change of the latest price compared with the settlement price of the previous trading day;  
(4) Trading volume and open interest calculated on a double-counted basis;  
(5) Other matters required by the CSRC to be disclosed.

**Article 63**  
A futures exchange shall, on a daily basis and in an appropriate manner, publish the following market quotations:  
(1) Names of futures products and contract months;  
(2) Opening price, highest price, lowest price, closing price, settlement price of the previous trading day, and settlement price of the day;  
(3) Price change of the closing price compared with the settlement price of the previous trading day;  
(4) Breakdown and totals of trading volume and open interest calculated on a double-counted basis;  
(5) Other matters required by the CSRC to be published.

**Article 64**  
After the market closes each day, the exchange shall, for actively traded contracts, publish by contract month and by long/short side the list of the top twenty members by open interest and the corresponding positions, and the list of the top twenty members by trading volume and the corresponding volumes.  
After the market closes on each Friday, the exchange shall publish, for each product, the number of standard warehouse receipts, weekly changes in the number of standard warehouse receipts, and the storage capacity available for futures delivery.  
The exchange shall publish the results of delivery matching after delivery matching is completed, and shall publish the volume of physical deliveries after the final delivery day.

**Article 65**  
A futures exchange shall compile weekly, monthly, and annual reports on trading conditions within its market and publish them in a timely manner.

**Article 66**  
Where a member fails to perform a futures contract, the exchange shall successively assume performance on its behalf in the following order:  
(1) Utilize the defaulting member’s margin;  
(2) Utilize the exchange’s risk reserve fund;  
(3) Utilize the exchange’s own funds.  
Where the exchange has performed on behalf of a member by the methods set forth in items (2) and (3) of the preceding paragraph, it shall, in accordance with law, seek recourse against that member.

**Article 67**  
Delivery in futures trading shall be organized in a unified manner by the exchange.  
The exchange shall not restrict the total amount of physical delivery.

**Article 68**  
Designated delivery warehouses shall be designated by the exchange. The exchange shall enter into agreements with designated delivery warehouses to specify the rights and obligations of both parties, and shall supervise and administer the business of designated delivery warehouses related to futures delivery.  
Designated delivery warehouses shall ensure the quantity and quality of the deliverable goods represented by standard warehouse receipts.

**Article 69**  
Standard warehouse receipts shall be issued by designated delivery warehouses and shall take effect upon registration with the exchange.  
Standard warehouse receipts may be transferred, and both parties to the transfer must register the transfer with the exchange. A transfer of a standard warehouse receipt that is not registered shall be invalid.

**Article 70**  
A futures exchange shall ensure the completeness and safety of records relating to futures trading, settlement, and delivery.  
The retention period for the foregoing records shall be not less than twenty years.

# Chapter VI  Supervision and Administration

**Article 71**  
A futures exchange shall obtain the approval of the CSRC for the following matters:  
(1) Formulating or amending its articles of association and trading rules;  
(2) Listing, suspending, canceling, or resuming trading of futures products;  
(3) Listing, amending, or terminating futures contracts;  
(4) Conducting networked trading among futures exchanges;  
(5) Other matters as prescribed by the CSRC.  
Where a futures exchange formulates or amends business rules other than the articles of association and trading rules, it shall file them with the CSRC for the record.

**Article 72**  
Upon the departure of the general manager, the council of the exchange shall engage an intermediary institution qualified to conduct securities- and futures-related business to carry out an exit audit. The intermediary engaged and the audit report shall be filed with the CSRC for the record.  
Where the CSRC deems it necessary, it may designate an intermediary institution or dispatch personnel to participate in the exit audit.

**Article 73**  
The chair of the council, the general manager, and the deputy general managers of a futures exchange shall not concurrently hold positions in any profit-making organization.  
Non-member directors and staff of a futures exchange shall not, in any form, concurrently hold positions in member entities of the exchange or other profit-making entities related to futures trading.

**Article 74**  
Staff of a futures exchange shall not seek benefits from members or clients of the exchange, and shall not, directly or indirectly, engage in futures trading.

**Article 75**  
The income of a futures exchange shall be administered and used in accordance with relevant provisions and shall be used on a priority basis to ensure the normal operation and improvement of the trading venues and facilities of the exchange.

**Article 76**  
A futures exchange shall fulfill the following reporting obligations to the CSRC:  
(1) Within three months after the end of each year, submit financial accounting reports audited by an accounting firm recognized by the CSRC;  
(2) Within 15 days after the end of each quarter and within 30 days after the end of each year, submit quarterly and annual work reports on its operations and on the implementation of relevant laws, regulations, rules, and policies;  
(3) Other reporting matters as required by the CSRC.

**Article 77**  
Upon the occurrence of major events, a futures exchange shall report to the CSRC in a timely manner.  
“Major events,” as referred to in the preceding paragraph, include:  
(1) Discovery that market participants or staff of the exchange are engaged in, or may be engaged in, serious violations of relevant state laws, regulations, rules, or policies;  
(2) Litigation involving the exchange or its staff in an amount exceeding RMB 500,000;  
(3) Other matters specified by the CSRC.

**Article 78**  
Where members of a futures exchange violate the business rules of the exchange, the exchange shall handle such violations in a timely manner and report to the CSRC.

**Article 79**  
The CSRC may, in light of market conditions, adjust trading margin ratios and transaction fee ratios charged by futures exchanges, and suspend, resume, or cancel trading of a particular futures product.

**Article 80**  
When abnormal conditions arise in the futures market, the CSRC may decide to take emergency measures such as delayed market opening, suspension of trading, adjusting the limit range, raising trading margin ratios, ordering position reduction within a specified time, enforcing forced liquidation, or restricting withdrawals.

**Article 81**  
The CSRC may station inspectors at a futures exchange. Inspectors shall perform their duties in accordance with relevant provisions of the CSRC.  
A futures exchange shall cooperate with inspectors in the performance of their duties.

**Article 82**  
A futures exchange shall pay futures market regulatory fees to the CSRC in a timely manner in accordance with relevant state provisions.

---

# Chapter VII  Penalties

**Article 83**  
Where a futures brokerage company member leases, contracts out, or otherwise permits other institutions or individuals to use all or part of its seat, punishment shall be imposed in accordance with Article 59 of the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*.  
Where a non–futures brokerage company member commits the conduct described in the preceding paragraph, it shall be ordered to make corrections and its illegal gains shall be confiscated; where the circumstances are serious, the exchange shall be ordered to cancel its membership.

**Article 84**  
Other violations of these Measures shall be punished in accordance with the *Interim Regulations on the Administration of Futures Trading*.

**Article 85**  
Where violations of these Measures cause damage to others, civil liability shall be borne in accordance with law.

---

# Chapter VIII  Supplementary Provisions

**Article 86**  
“Business rules,” as used in these Measures, refers to the trading rules of a futures exchange and their implementing details.

**Article 87**  
Articles of association, trading rules, and implementing details of futures exchanges that became effective prior to the implementation of these Measures shall remain in force, but must be brought into conformity with these Measures within the time limit prescribed by the CSRC.

**Article 88**  
Where provisions previously issued by the CSRC prior to the implementation of these Measures are inconsistent with these Measures, these Measures shall prevail.

**Article 89**  
These Measures shall be interpreted by the CSRC.

**Article 90**  
These Measures shall come into force on September 1, 1999.
